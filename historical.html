Revisit the glorious past

1

Mauryan Heritage: Bhopal's UNESCO Heritage Sites of the Bhimbetka Rock Shelters and the Sanchi Stupa, with ancient relics, intricate carvings, and Buddhist architecture dating back to the 3rd century BCE, exhibit Mauryan heritage and devotion.

2

Pallava Heritage: A UNESCO heritage site, the intricate rock-cut temples, iconic structures like the Shore Temple, Arjuna's Penance, the Five Rathas, and exquisite sculptures of Mahabalipuram’s monuments date back to the 7th century reflecting Pallava splendour.

3

Hoysala Heritage: The unique charm of the age-old temples of Chennakesava and Hoysaleswara in Halebidu epitomizes the Hoysala dynasty's exquisite heritage. These UNESCO-listed sites showcase intricate carvings and remarkable architecture from the 12th century.

4

Wodeyar Heritage: The Wodeyar dynasty's Mysore Palace, Jaganmohan Palace, and Lalitha Mahal showcase the brilliance of its architectural elegance. The Mysore Palace is a grand example of Indo-Saracenic architecture, adorned with intricate details and majestic domes. Its royal heritage, along with the other landmarks, offers glimpses into the dynasty's historical influence.

5

Maratha Heritage: Vadodara boasts the grand Indo-Saracenic Laxmi Vilas Palace, a patronage of the Gaekwads, while Indore houses the landmarks of the Rajwada and Lal Baag Palaces, left behind by the Holkars, all of which reflect the Marathas' glittering royal legacy. These marvels are an embodiment of regal elegance and cultural significance.

6

Mughal Heritage: Witness the grandeur of Mughal heritage, blending Persian, Islamic, and Indian architecture into timeless marvels of history at the Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, and Fatehpur Sikri. These UNESCO World Heritage Sites make Agra a gateway to the past, offering a captivating journey through centuries of art, culture, and imperial legacy.
