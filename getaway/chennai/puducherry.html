Puducherry

The French Riviera of the East” Pondicherry with its hotels, restaurants and shops sells a seductive vision of the French continental aesthetic enhanced by its Indian artists and designers.

Streets that jostle with festivities and the petite cafes that spot the bougainvillaea-d boulevards come together to create an enthralling ambience. Pondicherry's calm has resonated with legions of tourists. Much of its quietude is foretold by Auroville and The Sri Aurobindo Ashram. Not far behind are its beaches and the plethora of monuments with a French sheen which isn't just a peel-away layer. Pondy has evolved with the epoch but has acquiesced with its past most faithfully. You can enjoy fabulous shopping, French food, beer, plenty of yoga and meditation while you're here and experience the lifestyle of the natives.

Indulgence

Auroville tops the list of must-visits. Explore the White Town on foot and see Goubert Avenue and Rock Beach. Try scuba diving at The Chunnambar Boat House and The Karaikal Beach. Visit La Boutique d' Auroville, Mirra Boutique, and Kalki Boutique to pick up some home décor items and feast on a French breakfast followed by a hearty south Indian lunch.

Best time to visit

December to March is an ideal time to visit Pondicherry because of all the festivities, including Christmas and New Year. The Aurobindo Ashram celebrates the birthday of Mirra Alfassa, the foundress of Auroville, and the International Yoga Festival is observed from the 4th of January to the 7th. The vibrancy of Pongal, celebrated during winter is a brilliant treat to the senses.

Trivia

The Governor of the French East India Company established a commercial centre in Pondicherry in 1674, making it the principal French base in India.

Pondicherry Beach

White Town, Pondicherry 605001

Good To Know: This is easily one of the most stunning parts of Pondicherry’s White Town, neighbouring the famous landmarks of Dupleix Park and The War Memorial.

Paradise Beach

Manaveli Rd, Chinna Veerampattinam, Pudukuppam, Puducherry 605007

Chunnambar Beach is now popularly known as Paradise Beach for its beauty is nothing less than paradisiacal. The beach is away from the mainland and the only way to access it is via the ferry from the Chunnambar Boat House. This journey is speckled by palm trees and luxuriant mangroves. The beach and its concomitant journey is a treasure trove of delights much unlike anything in Pondicherry.

Good To Know: You can even find dolphins in the water. Many rare species of birds frequent this stretch as well.

Ousteri Wetland & National Park

Pondicherry Villupuram Road, Pondicherry Ho, Pondicherry 605001

Colloquially called Osudu Lake, the national park was built around a century ago. Together they sustain avifauna and house numerous species of plants and trees. Due to its diverse flora and fauna, Osudu Lake is reckoned one of the most important wetlands in Asia. The atmosphere of the place is still and soft. You feel closest to its nature when you venture around it on a boat.

Good To Know: This expansive park stretching over 390 acres is rich in aquatic life and attracts scores of migratory birds.

French War Memorial

Mahe de Labourdonnais St, White Town, Puducherry, 605001

Located close to the Pondicherry beach, the French War Memorial is dedicated to the soldiers who were martyred during World War One and belongs to the French government. A plaque commemorating their names rests above it. The monument comprises four pillars enclosing the statue of a soldier poised with his gun. Situated behind the memorial, is a bronze structure marking Joseph Francois Dupleix’s (a former Governor General of Dutch India) arrival in Pondicherry.

Good To Know: On Bastille Day (14th July), it is illuminated in honour of the martyred soldiers.

The Statue of Dupleix

Beach Road, Pondicherry Ho, Pondicherry 605001 (Goubert Avenue)

In 1742, Joseph François Dupleix became the Governor General of all the French colonies in India. In recognition of his contributions, a statue in his honour was commissioned in 1870. You will come across this statue at Place du Republique, Goubert Avenue. It is not far away from the Rock Beach and overlooks a children’s park on one end. The 3m high statue stands atop a skilfully carved granite column with admirable geometric specificity.

Good To Know: This is one of the most popular attractions here and often covered as a part of the Rock beach trail

Basilica of the Sacred Heart of Jesus

Door No, 132, South Boulevard, near Railway Station, MG Road Area, Puducherry, 605001

The grotto of the Sacred Heart of Jesus on the church premises leads one into the church. A total of 24 columns support the interior of the church that presents an extraordinary blend of typical French and generic south-Indian architecture. The stained-glass panels within illustrate significant events from Jesus’ life and the patron saints associated with the Sacred Heart. Chandeliers light up the inside of this spacious church with an ethereal glow.

Good To Know: Built in 1902, this basilica is a prime example of neo-gothic architecture.

Church of Our Lady of Angels

Rue, Dumas St, White Town, Puducherry, 605001

This church is more than its opulent white and golden interiors. The Capuchins of the Franciscan order established three churches within the same area since 1674 as all the extinct edifices were wrecked by decay or British extremism. Architecturally, the church blends harmoniously with its landscape. The front façade is a salmon/yellow affair, the polygonal dome and the two bell towers appear as a triangle atop the church- corpus.

Good To Know: Inside the premises you can also find the stupendous Joan of Arc statue and the Old Capuchin Cemetery.

Auroville

Auroville Rd, Auroville, Puducherry, 605101

The “City of Dawn” was inaugurated by The Mother in 1968 to foster spirituality, sustainability and self-sufficiency. At the centre of this township of 3930 acres is the Matrimandir, an ornate geodesic dorm and an architectural masterpiece that honour the universal mother figure. Auroville’s magnitude and magnanimity can be felt in its verdure, hotels, restaurants and cafes. The services offered by this community include workshops, retreats and even internships.

Good To Know: It is recognised by UNESCO as “a project of importance to the future of humanity”.

4, St Martin St, White Town, Puducherry, 605001

Raj Niwas presents a fascinating conglomeration of Indian and French architecture. It is the official residence of the Lieutenant Governor of Pondicherry. FAQs

How’s the weather in Pondicherry?

Pondicherry’s weather is mostly warm throughout the year. The summer months are tedious while the winters are comfortable and pleasant. The monsoon rains are quite the transitional period between these two prominent seasons. You don’t have to carry any warm clothes as such except for the stray stole or light cardigan. Keep your wardrobe cotton, light and breathable; and your sunscreens, sunglasses handy.

How expensive is Pondicherry?

It all depends on how much you want to spend. Pondicherry can be as expensive or as cheap as you will it to be. Accommodation, food and travel are available at various price points. You can splurge or restrain yourself as much as you want.

How long should my Pondicherry trip be?

Well, if you’re just going to hit the tourist destinations, 4 days should be enough. If you’re pressed for time, go to Auroville- an international township and the microcosm of Pondy. If you spare a week, you can wander off into the expanse of the land and traverse to quaint towns and villages.

How’s the food in Pondicherry?

The food in Pondy is delightful. You will come across an abundance of Tamil and French foods being sold at posh restaurants and street-side stalls. North Indian cuisine is also available in the cafes and other eateries that intersperse the expanse.

Where do I stay in Pondicherry?

In Pondy you can stay at one of the French houses that have been converted hotels. Besides, there are hostels at comparatively lower prices perfect for a budget trip.

Is alcohol allowed in Pondicherry?

Yes! And at very cheap rates. Puducherry, being a union territory doesn’t levy GST on alcoholic beverages. However, drinking is not permissible in public places.

Is Pondicherry safe?

Pondicherry is safe for travellers and has great couple-friendly accommodations. Whether you’re a girl sauntering along the lanes or a family with kids, you can be at relative ease while touring. The local residents are accustomed to seeing tourists of all kinds. So, chances are nobody is going to bat an eyelid irrespective of how conventional or offbeat you are in your approach to Pondy.

What should I wear in Pondicherry?

Wear whatever makes you feel comfortable. Carry linen and cotton clothes because of the temperature. However, wear something ethnic or traditionally Indian when you visit the mosques or temples.
