Shimla

Nestled among the mystical embrace of oaks, cedars, pines and rhododendrons, Shimla will entice you with its freshness and beauty.

Are you looking to have a conversation with the clouds while seeking solitude in the arms of nature? To experience the wild rush of adrenaline while trekking, rafting or paragliding? Do you want to watch the partition struggle unfold in front of your eye at the Viceregal Lodge? Are you waiting to let your inner shopaholic escape while at the same time save your pocket from burns? Shimla, the erstwhile summer capital of the sub-continent, is the comforting haven that will offer you them all and even happily surprise you with a warm snuggle making you forget your daily hassles.

Must Visit

Take the train ride from Kalka to Shimla, trek to the top of Jakhoo Hill and witness the splendour of the Chadwick Waterfalls to soak in the beauty. Go rafting at Tattapani, paragliding at Kangra Valley, skiing down the fluffy snow-covered slopes of Kufri and ice-skating at the only open ice-skating rink in Asia.

Best time to visit

Tourism usually thrives during summer (March-June) when the weather is calm and pleasant with a sweet breeze playing with your hair. You find Shimla cloaked in white during winter (November-February) and you can have an invigorating snowfall experience if you are lucky. You can also enjoy skiing and skating in the pure white snow.

Trivia

The famed Kalka-Shimla train route is recognised as an UNESCO World Heritage Site while the railway station connects Shimla to the plains.

Shimla offers adventure activities like trekking, mountain biking, paragliding, and river rafting. The Kufri and Naldehra regions are ideal for skiing and golfing, while dense forests offer great trekking and camping experiences.

Shimla is a romantic getaway with its charming colonial architecture, snow-capped peaks, and tranquil nature. Honeymooners can enjoy scenic walks on the Mall Road, peaceful moments at Summer Hill, and stunning views from Jakhoo Hill.

Shimla offers wellness options, with renowned Ayurvedic centers like Wildflower Hall and The Oberoi offering treatments, spas, and therapies in serene surroundings. Visitors can rejuvenate through holistic wellness practices amidst cool mountain air.

Shimla’s natural beauty and luxury venues, like The Oberoi Cecil and Radisson Jass, make it a great MICE destination. These venues offer modern facilities with scenic views, perfect for conferences, retreats, and business events in a tranquil environment.

Away from the chaos of the metropolis, Shimla is like a safe haven that envelopes you with a distinctly old-world charm as soon as you step foot in the enchanting hill station. Shimla has something in store for everybody – beautiful nature parks for leisurely strolls, colourful shopping streets for shopaholics, invigorating adventure sports, and a host of other activities, leaving you spoilt for choice.

Jakhoo Temple

Temple Hanumanji, Jakhu, Shimla, Himachal Pradesh 171001

Situated atop the highest peak in Shimla on Jakhoo Hill, with the the stunning Shivalik ranges as a backdrop, Jakhoo Temple is a marvel in itself. With a Hanuman Statue of 700 m surpassing even the height of Christ the Redeemer, it is a must visit. Lord Hanuman is believed to have rested here during his search for the Sanjeevani Booti. The verdant Deodar trees and an abundance of monkeys make this temple appear truly mystic.

Good To Know: You can either walk or take a fun pony ride from the Ridge to the temple through the breathtakingly beautiful scenery.

Kufri

Shimla, Himachal Pradesh 171012

10 km away from Shimla, Kufri is a snow-covered paradise throughout the year with an unparalleled view of the pristine white peaks, luxurious cedar woods and pretty flowers only add to its mystical charm. Nag Temple at Mahasu Peak offers a spiritual experience and mesmerizing views of the shrines of Kedarnath and Badrinath. It is also the base point for many treks which are bound to satiate your adrenaline rush.

Good To Know: ‘Kufri Fun World’ here boasts the world’s highest Go-Kart track and is a must if you’re visiting with kids.

Skiing in Kufri

Kufri, Shimla, Himachal Pradesh 171012

Kufri, with its pristine snow carpet cut by electrifying slopes and valleys, is the perfect skier’s paradise. Located 16km away from Shimla, it is the winter sports capital of Shimla. Head to this adventure hotspot and let your adrenaline flow by indulging in various exhilarating activities amidst the white snow.

Good To Know: Skiing is the prime crowd puller, with the professionals flaunting their immaculate skills as the whoosh by you down the slopes and the amateurs sliding and tumbling in the snow, nonetheless, having the time of their lives.

Chail

Himachal Pradesh 173217

Chail is a quaint little hamlet basking in the glory of the being the erstwhile summer capital of the Patiala Kings. Hidden amongst deodar and pine trees, it boasts the world’s highest cricket ground and the majestic heritage hotel, Chail Palace, all surrounded by breathtakingly beautiful scenery. Also known as a hiker’s paradise, it is bound to leave your senses refreshed with its enchanting embrace.

Good To Know: As a nature lover, you can also visit the Chail Wildlife Sanctuary which is home to the Himalayan black deer, sambar, common langur and goral.

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Shimla?

Shimla’s top tourist attractions are Mall Road, The Ridge, Jakhoo Temple, Christ Church, Kufri and Shimla State Museum.

What is the best time to visit Shimla?

The best time to visit Shimla is during the summer months from March to June and the autumn months from September to November. These periods offer pleasant weather and clear skies ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities. Winter months (December to February) are popular for snowfall and winter sports.

How do I get to Shimla?

Shimla is accessible by road from major cities like Delhi, Chandigarh, and Ambala. The nearest broad gauge railway station is Kalka, and from there, the UNESCO World Heritage Kalka-Shimla Railway connects Shimla through a scenic route. The nearest airport is Jubbarhatti Airport, approximately 22 km away.

What are the accommodation options in Shimla?

Shimla offers a range of accommodation options including hotels, resorts, guesthouses, and heritage properties. Popular choices include The Oberoi Cecil, Wildflower Hall, Clarkes Hotel, and Radisson Hotel Shimla.

What should I pack for a trip to Shimla?

Pack according to the season: light clothing for summers and warmer clothing for winters, especially during December to February when it can get quite cold. Comfortable shoes for walking and trekking are recommended.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Shimla?

Shimla offers a variety of North Indian and Himachali cuisines. Local specialties include Himachali dishes like Sidu, Chana Madra, Babru, and various traditional sweets. Mall Road and The Ridge are popular spots to try local delicacies.

Can I find adventure activities or sports in Shimla?

Yes, Shimla offers a range of adventure activities depending on the season. In winter, visitors can enjoy skiing in Kufri and Narkanda. Other activities include trekking, paragliding, rafting (nearby rivers), and exploring nature trails.
