Shimla

Nestled among the mystical embrace of oaks, cedars, pines and rhododendrons, Shimla will entice you with its freshness and beauty.

Are you looking to have a conversation with the clouds while seeking solitude in the arms of nature? To experience the wild rush of adrenaline while trekking, rafting or paragliding? Do you want to watch the partition struggle unfold in front of your eye at the Viceregal Lodge? Are you waiting to let your inner shopaholic escape while at the same time save your pocket from burns? Shimla, the erstwhile summer capital of the sub-continent, is the comforting haven that will offer you them all and even happily surprise you with a warm snuggle making you forget your daily hassles.

Indulgence

Take the train ride from Kalka to Shimla, trek to the top of Jakhoo Hill and witness the splendour of the Chadwick Waterfalls to soak in the beauty. Go rafting at Tattapani, paragliding at Kangra Valley, skiing down the fluffy snow-covered slopes of Kufri and ice-skating at the only open ice-skating rink in Asia.

Best time to visit

Tourism usually thrives during summer (March-June) when the weather is calm and pleasant with a sweet breeze playing with your hair. You find Shimla cloaked in white during winter (November-February) and you can have an invigorating snowfall experience if you are lucky. You can also enjoy skiing and skating in the pure white snow.

Trivia

The famed Kalka-Shimla train route is recognised as an UNESCO World Heritage Site while the railway station connects Shimla to the plains.

Jakhoo Temple

Temple Hanumanji, Jakhu, Shimla, Himachal Pradesh 171001

Situated atop the highest peak in Shimla on Jakhoo Hill, with the the stunning Shivalik ranges as a backdrop, Jakhoo Temple is a marvel in itself. With a Hanuman Statue of 700 m surpassing even the height of Christ the Redeemer, it is a must visit. Lord Hanuman is believed to have rested here during his search for the Sanjeevani Booti. The verdant Deodar trees and an abundance of monkeys make this temple appear truly mystic.

Good To Know: You can either walk or take a fun pony ride from the Ridge to the temple through the breathtakingly beautiful scenery.

Kufri

Shimla, Himachal Pradesh 171012

10 km away from Shimla, Kufri is a snow-covered paradise throughout the year with an unparalleled view of the pristine white peaks, luxurious cedar woods and pretty flowers only add to its mystical charm. Nag Temple at Mahasu Peak offers a spiritual experience and mesmerizing views of the shrines of Kedarnath and Badrinath. It is also the base point for many treks which are bound to satiate your adrenaline rush.

Good To Know: ‘Kufri Fun World’ here boasts the world’s highest Go-Kart track and is a must if you’re visiting with kids.

Chail

Himachal Pradesh 173217

Chail is a quaint little hamlet basking in the glory of the being the erstwhile summer capital of the Patiala Kings. Hidden amongst deodar and pine trees, it boasts the world’s highest cricket ground and the majestic heritage hotel, Chail Palace, all surrounded by breathtakingly beautiful scenery. Also known as a hiker’s paradise, it is bound to leave your senses refreshed with its enchanting embrace.

Good To Know: As a nature lover, you can also visit the Chail Wildlife Sanctuary which is home to the Himalayan black deer, sambar, common langur and goral.

Himachal State Museum: Museum Road, near Doordarshan Kendra, Chaura Maidan, Shimla, Himachal Pradesh 171004

Housed inside a vintage Victorian mansion ‘Inverarm’, the museum holds many keys to the past, taking you on a time machine ride. Home to various exclusive artefacts collected from across the country such as antique coins, handicrafts and paintings, you are bound to get lost in the realms of India’s rich history.

Good To Know: The most sought-after section of the museum is the huge library which stores more than 10,000 books, journals and magazines and the doll gallery housing charming little bride and groom dolls.

Summer April - June

Sunshine floods the slopes of Shimla during Summer (May – June), with the average temperature ranging between 16°C and 30°C. This is the peak tourist season, with warm weather throughout, making it comfortable for tourists to explore the magic of Shimla. You can also find Shimla, beautifully adorned, to celebrate festivals like Holi in March, Baisakhi in April, and the grand Sipi Fair in May.

Good To Know: It is the peak season to indulge in adrenaline-boosting adventure activities like paragliding, rafting, hiking, camping, and trekking.

Monsoon July - September

Monsoon in Shimla lasts from July to Mid-October with the average temperature ranging from 14°C and 20°C. There is incessant rainfall with frequent landslides, making it difficult for tourists to explore freely. However, by virtue of it being an off-season, most of the hotels have lower tariffs. Hence, it is the perfect time to explore Shimla on a budget. Keep your raincoats and boots ready and you're good to go.

Good To Know: You can also experience the Rhyali Festival, which is celebrated with much pomp to express gratitude to the Rain Gods.

Winter December - February

The pristine white snow-clad slopes of Himachal, sparkling in the Sun, is just like a page out of a fairytale. The winter season starts in November, with December and January being the coldest months. The average temperature ranges from 8°C to a minimum of -6°C giving you all the chills. Festivals of Navaratri, Diwali, Bhoj Fair, Christmas, ice skating carnival in December and Lohri in January are celebrated at this time.

Good To Know: Indulge in snowball fights, ice skating, skiing, hiking and trekking in the snow-clad slopes. Make sure to carry thick woolens to beat the frost weather.

FAQs

Which is a better place to visit, Old Digha or New Digha?

The choice really does lie with the visitor because preferences matter. While Old Digha’s spaced-out hotels and calm shores are perfect for those on a honeymoon, New Digha’s sandy shores, proximity to the park and aquarium and star-rated hotels make it frequently visited as well. Since they’re only 3kms apart, it’s possible to experience both the calm and the bustle.

Which are the top beaches of Digha?

The Old and New Digha beaches along with Udaipur and Shankarpur Beaches are some of the cleanest in West Bengal and offer a charm of their own. Whether you seek seclusion in the waters in a romantic getaway or the classic sandy shores, both can be found on these beaches. The food stalls and water sports available only make the whole experience more exciting.

Are water sports available in Digha?

They are available in abundance on all the beaches, especially the most frequented New Digha Beach. The stalls provide you with equipment and assistance for any activity of your choice. You can be sure to have your enjoyment multiplied with the dash of adrenaline this will bring you. Wearing suitable clothes that won’t wear you down with their weight once wet or fly about if you’re in the air is highly advisable as that makes the experience all the more pleasant.

Which are the best places to eat in Digha?

With the place rapidly developing, the eateries and restaurants in Digha are increasing in number and standard. Local food stalls and those by the beach are sure to give you the perfect savouries to snack on while you laze on the beach or indulge in water sports. Other restaurants that will cater to your seafood cravings and match your expectations are The Bite Restaurant, Mahabhoj Restaurant, Restaurant Bhorpet, Aqua Blues and Koshe Kosha. If you’re looking for cheaper options but good quality food, the Bay Cuffe Restaurant and Elgin Restaurant will fit the bill. All in all the food experience in Digha is one to look forward to and delight in.

What is the fastest route to Digha from Kolkata?

The fastest way to travel to Digha is by cab. It takes approximately 2 hours 33 minutes to get there but the travel can be a bit pricey, ranging from Rs 3,000 to Rs 4,000. Cheaper options will take you longer to reach Digha but the journey will be equally exciting if you’re surrounded by your loved ones. You can take a train from Howrah Station and reach in 3 hours 25 minutes, tickets will be priced anywhere between Rs 150 and Rs 850. Buses from Esplanade will take you there in 4 hours 15 minutes and can charge you Rs 150 to Rs 350.

Is any music or dance festival hosted in Digha?

If you enjoy music and dance performances, visiting Digha in December is what will make your holiday even more pleasant. The town celebrates the Digha Beach Festival during this time when a multitude of stalls is set up for the carnival. You can relish the delicious appetizers, play games, or watch the music recital and dance showcase that features performers from all over the country.
