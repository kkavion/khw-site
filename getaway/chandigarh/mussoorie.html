Mussoorie

Mussoorie's luxuriant oaks and pines and splendid scenery will usher you into a land where nature is all that matters.

Envelope your senses in the lingering aroma of tea and immerse yourself in the soothing backdrop of the snow-clad Himalayan mountains. Embark on an exhilarating trekking trip to Lal Tibba to get an alluring and salubrious view of the Doon Valley or feel the adrenaline rush as you surround yourself with the vivid spectrum of flora and fauna inside the Benog Wildlife Sanctuary. Thrive in the ecstatic melting pot of cultures and review the mouthwatering delicacies starting from the momos to the local rustic dishes to appease your appetite.

Indulgence

Picnic at Gun Hill, or trek through the Oak-clad dusty trails of the Lal Tibba. You can paraglide over the mountains and paddle-boat in the Mussoorie Lake for a surreal experience. Step into the world of nature at the Benog Wildlife Reserve. Do savour momos and thukpas and shop for Pashmina shawls and scarves.

Best time to visit

Summer is an ideal time to engage in the choicest of adventure sports while simultaneously observing nature at its best in the 'Queen of Hills'. However, the footfall thus remains a little high during this time so take necessary steps in advance to get the best of experience. If travelling some other time, remember, Mussoorie is always ethereal and enchanting.

Trivia

Mussoorie has been a popular getaway for many of the elite including the Nehru family, Sachin Tendulkar and Devanand who even owns a house here!

A tranquil walk with scenic mountain views and fresh air

Swarming street with shops, cafes, and vibrant local life

Lal Tibba

Lal Tibba Binoculars bazar, Road, Landour, Mussoorie, Uttarakhand 248179

6 km away from the main city, Lal Tibba boasts of being a beautiful vantage point that offers an alluring view of the Mussoorie horizon nestled within snow-clad mountains. Riddled with beautiful British architecture, it is reminiscent of the glorious past of Uttarakhand and the ‘Queen of the Hills’. Indulge in an exhilarating trek and climb up the mountain top to immerse yourself in the wide spectrum of nature.

Good To Know: The telescopes installed here turn Lal Tibba into a hotspot for nature lovers and a panoramic setting for photographers.

Mussoorie, Uttarakhand

Located 400 ft above the Mall Road, the Gun Hill is the second-highest vantage point of Mussoorie and it is surrounded by a vivid spectrum of nature. Plan a relaxing picnic with your loved ones amidst the velvety green hues of the enchanting and majestic Gun Hill.

Good To Know: Truly one of the best ways to observe the myriads of colours coming together is by the cable car or the ropeway.

Benog Wildlife Sanctuary

Mussorie Range, Uttarakhand 248179

Good To Know: Trek along the heart of this quaint little sanctum, hear the majestic roar of the leopard in the ‘call of the wind’, and make friends with the exquisite quails, mountain deer, and red-billed blue magpie.

Airway Jolly Grant International Airport

Since Mussoorie is a relatively smaller hill station, it does not have any viable option for direct flights. However, the nearest airport is the Jolly Grant Airport located 57.7km away in Dehradun which is connected to most of the major cities of India including the metropolitan states. However international flights are not yet available thus opt for a connecting flight from Delhi. From Dehradun, it is smooth, hassle-free ride to Mussoorie, entangled by the enchanting beauty of the hill station.

Railway Dehradun Railway Station

Mussoorie lacks the benefit of having its own railway station. Thus, there are no direct trains connected to the ‘Queen of the Hills'. The nearest railway is the Dehradun Railway Station which is located 35.3 km away. The distance between the station and Mussoorie can be covered by a comfortable car ride or a bumpy bus ride.

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Mussoorie?

The top tourist attractions of Mussoorie are Gun Hill, Kempty Falls, Lal Tibba, Camel's Back Road, Mussoorie Lake and Mall Road.

What is the best time to visit Mussoorie?

The best time to visit Mussoorie is during the summer months from March to June and the autumn months from September to November. These periods offer pleasant weather ideal for sightseeing.

How do I get to Mussoorie?

Mussoorie is accessible by road from major cities like Delhi, Dehradun, and Haridwar. The nearest railway station is Dehradun, approximately 35 km away, and the nearest airport is Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun, about 60 km away.

What are the accommodation options in Mussoorie?

Mussoorie offers a variety of accommodation options ranging from luxury hotels and resorts to budget guesthouses and homestays. Popular choices include Jaypee Residency Manor, JW Marriott Mussoorie Walnut Grove Resort & Spa, and Honeymoon Inn Mussoorie.

What are some nearby attractions or day trips from Mussoorie?

Some of the nearby places to visit from Mussoorie are: Dhanaulti (approx. 30 km away) Landour (adjacent to Mussoorie) Dehradun (for attractions like Robber's Cave and Sahastradhara)

What should I pack for a trip to Mussoorie?

Pack according to the season: light clothing for summers and warmer clothing for winters, especially during December to February. Comfortable shoes for walking and trekking are recommended.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Mussoorie?

Mussoorie offers a variety of North Indian and Tibetan cuisines. Local specialties include momos, thukpa, and Garhwali dishes like Phanu and Kafuli.

Can I find adventure activities or sports in Mussoorie?

Mussoorie offers adventure activities such as trekking, paragliding (seasonal), zip-lining, and rock climbing. Adventure enthusiasts can also explore nearby areas like Dhanaulti for more outdoor adventures.
