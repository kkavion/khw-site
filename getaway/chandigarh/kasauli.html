Kasauli

Enchant yourself with Kasauli's tall rain-washed trees and radiant flowers as you wander through the cobbled forested pathways and breathe in the freshness of the air!

Nestled amidst the Himalayan Mountains, the tiny cantonment town of Kasauli evokes the same feelings of emotional contentment that the river Wye had evoked in Wordsworth. Sprawling over the forested slopes, Kasauli is a cosy town where time itself seems to come to a standstill. This quaint secluded place is guaranteed to fill you to the brim with a rare tranquillity. Located in the Solan district of the state of Himachal Pradesh, with winding cobbled paths, enchanting forests, and an untainted beauty, it is the perfect place to escape from the din of everyday city life.

Must Visit

When in Kasauli, witness the stunning sunset at Sunset Point and trek along the famous trails of Kalka, Garkhal and Jabli. The cable car ride on the Timber Trail will allow you a breathtaking view of the scenic landscape and so will paragliding from Dagshai. Relish the locally brewed wines at Kasauli Brewery and buy specialty products like designer ceramics and antique jewellery from the stores at Lower Mall Road.

Best time to visit

April to June is the perfect time to seek a cool refuge in Kasauli from the Indian summers. In the winter months, the small town turns into a chilly paradise and often experiences snowfall in late December and January. September to November is a splendid time to plan a trip to the immaculate town of Kasauli to soak up the winter sun and have fun bonfire nights!

Trivia

British officer Henry Lawrence was the first foreigner to build a house here even before Kasauli was founded. His cottage named Sunnyside stands intact till today.

Kasauli allows you to find thrill wherever you seek it. Amidst its scenic landscapes are a range of activities like trekking through the Gilbert Trail, rock climbing, & paragliding. The verdure and cobbled pathways provide the scope for nature walks, bird-watching, & camping.

This quiet hill station offers a relaxing escape for newlyweds. The charm lies in its peaceful atmosphere, misty mountains, and lush scenery. A romantic stroll along the Gilbert Trail, cozy stays in boutique hotels, and ethereal sunsets in a warm embrace will enliven your visit here.

Charming colonial architecture, misty hills, and quiet walking trails grace this hill station that is popular for being a retreat away from crowded tourist spots. Kasauli's untouched beauty allows a deep dive into nature that lets you discover yourself.

This wellness retreat has renowned resorts like Kasauli Resort and Baikunth Resort that offer spa treatments, yoga, and meditation to completely rejuvenate the body. Popular treatments include Ayurvedic massages, detox therapies, and aromatherapy, all amidst tranquil surroundings.

Gurkha Fort

Subathu, Himachal Pradesh 173206

The Gurkha Fort in Subathu is a delightful amalgamation of history and the enriching beauty of nature. The fortification was established by Chief Amar Singh Thapa to strengthen the Gurkhas against the British Army. Though the Gurkhas suffered defeat, the fort still tells stories of their bravery. It is 16 km from Parwanoo and is now a major cantonment of the Indian Army.

Good To Know: The fort houses 180-year-old cannons that were used in those wars and are of immense historic significance.

Kasauli Brewery

Kasauli, Himachal Pradesh 173204

Edward Dyer had established the eminent Kasauli Brewery even before the town came into existence. Popularly known as Mohan Meakin, this distillery is renowned for its single malt whiskeys and the original Indian pale ale. Its colonial background adds the aspect of heritage to the brewery making it an important sightseeing spot.

Good To Know: What makes the brewery even more interesting for tourists is the chance to observe the making of whiskey and even get a taste of the distilled whiskey.

Famous names here include Pinekonez which is a souvenir and antique shop, Jakkimulls, a readymade garment retailer, and, Kasauli Kraft which is a superior handicrafts store.

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Kasauli?

The top tourist attractions in Kasauli are Monkey Point, Mall Road, Christ Church, Gilbert Trail, Kasauli Brewery and Sunset Point.

What is the best time to visit Kasauli?

The best time to visit Kasauli is during the summer months from March to June and the autumn months from September to November. These periods offer pleasant weather and clear skies ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

How do I get to Kasauli?

Kasauli is accessible by road from major cities like Delhi, Chandigarh, and Shimla. The nearest railway station is Kalka, approximately 25 km away, and the nearest airport is Chandigarh Airport, about 65 km away.

What are the accommodation options in Kasauli?

Kasauli offers a range of accommodation options including hotels, resorts, guesthouses, and homestays. Popular choices include Kasauli Resort, Kasauli Castle Resort, and Baikunth Resort.

What are the activities and things to do in Kasauli?

When in Kasauli, you can enjoy scenic walks and hikes on Gilbert Trail and Sunset Point, visit Kasauli Brewery and taste local brews, explore the historic Christ Church and other colonial-era architecture and shop for souvenirs and local handicrafts on Mall Road.

What are some nearby attractions or day trips from Kasauli?

Some of the nearby attractions or day trips from Kasauli are: Dagshai (approx. 10 km away) Solan (approx. 25 km away) Parwanoo and Timber Trail (approx. 30 km away) Shimla (approx. 70 km away)

What should I pack for a trip to Kasauli?

Pack according to the season: light clothing for summers and warmer clothing for winters, especially during December to February. Comfortable shoes for walking and trekking are recommended.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Kasauli?

Kasauli offers a variety of North Indian and Himachali cuisines. Local specialties include Himachali dishes like Sidu, Chana Madra, and Babru, along with Tibetan momos and thukpa.

Can I find adventure activities or sports in Kasauli?

While Kasauli is more known for its relaxed atmosphere and scenic beauty, adventure enthusiasts can enjoy activities such as trekking, nature walks, and birdwatching in the surrounding forests.
