Pench

Pench National Park is a treasure chest of flora, fauna, and immense natural beauty that will allow you to live the wild-spirited life of Mowgli.

If you want to live wild and free with plentiful thrilling activities, Pench is your ideal travel destination! The silver ribbon of the Pench River encircles the verdant pastures of land, becoming the life force of the tribal villages as well as the wildlife. Boat rides on gorgeous canoes, elephant safaris, and open-air jeep safaris will keep you on your toes throughout your stay! Be it a glimpse of a Royal Bengal Tiger, or a magnificent shot of multi-hued migratory birds, you can rest assured that you will return with many stories to tell. Pench is a veritable haven for curious wildlife enthusiasts and thrill-seeking nature lovers alike.

Must Visit

Bask in the beauty of nature and observe the wildlife at Sitaghat and Raiyakasaa, where the magic happens. Go on jeep safaris, elephant rides and a boat safari in the calm waters. Experience camping at the Pench Jungle Camp and feel the thrill of leopards and tigers in the vicinity. Try mutton rogan josh, kebabs, dal bafla and poha.

Best time to visit

The ideal time to visit Pench National Park is during winter when the weather is pleasant and travelling into the forest is comfortable. However, the active period for spotting wildlife is between February and June. The humid summers make it difficult to traverse forest tracks and hence most tourists prefer the less balmy and pleasant climate of the winter days.

Trivia

Your dream of living Mowgli's adventurous life in the very setting of Rudyard Kipling's "The Jungle Book" can become a reality in the forests of Pench.

Interest
  • Wildlife

With vibrant foliage and diverse plant species, Pench is a luxurious abode for Bengal tigers, leopards, deer, and over 200 bird species. Safaris through the forest will showcase nature's abundance here, which makes it the prime spot for wildlife enthusiasts.

Pench Jungle Safari

Pench Villas Rd, Madhya Pradesh 480881

Wildlife safaris entail riding into the deep woods in open-air jeeps in the hopes of spotting royal Bengal tigers, gorgeous leopards, or exuberant spotted deer. You can enter the Core Zone through Turia, Karmajhiri, or Jamtara gates. Most accommodations lie adjacent to them. Forest lodges, tree house resorts, or jungle camps with tents for guestrooms make for rustic stays.

Good To Know: Safaris set out in the wee hours of the morning or the early hours of the evening though the timings are judiciously varied between long summer days and shorter winter ones.

Pench River

Pench National Park, Madhya Pradesh

As a mostly landlocked region, Pench is quite literally sustained by the eddying blue waters of the Pench River. The river meanders graciously through the National Park –its course dotted with viewpoints abuzz with eager bird watchers as birds coming swooping in herds over the river. Away from the sinuous lullaby of the river, you will find shallow lakes that almost camouflage with the green background.

Good To Know: There are dams and reservoirs where you can take photographs, and marvel to see immense water bodies contained in a state of obedient stasis.

Sitaghat

Pench National Park, Madhya Pradesh

Sitaghat, a bird-watching spot on the Pench River, is where you should settle down for a reprieve on your safaris. Alight from your jeeps, stretch your legs, and take deep breaths of the earthy forest-scented air. Follow the unrestrained flights of the motley of migratory birds and be sure to stay back long enough to enjoy the sunset with the sun slipping out of the sky into the blue of the river’s end.

Good To Know: The dry rocks of the hills are covered by indulgent tropical grasslands and this intersection creates the perfect setting for visiting birds.

Raiyakassa

Pench National Park, Madhya Pradesh

In the middle of the Pench National Park stands a watchtower that offers a helicopter view of the whole Park. As you stand atop this tower, the forest will unfold before you like a vibrant picture book! If you are a dedicated thrill-seeker, you can even spend a night at the watchtower as you have a much higher chance of spotting wild animals then.

Good To Know: This is the best place to spot wildlife in Pench so keep your binoculars handy as that errant flash of amber in the distant greens may well be a tiger or a leopard brushing past your sight!

Pench Jungle Camp

Tehsil - Kurai, District Seoni, Avarghani, Madhya Pradesh 480881

The Pench Jungle Camp is the epicentre of multifarious activities. You can set off on a wooded trail for nature walks or trekking. You can even rent bicycles and go cycling around the closely spaced villages within the Park. The Pench journey is more than chasing after the elusive Royal Bengal Tigers. Camping in the rustic forest is an experience that in itself will open your mind to the beauty of nature!

Good To Know: The campfire here is a point of attraction for travellers looking to make merry in large groups and especially at night.

Pench's tropical deciduous forest-type of climate ensures the weather remains hot, humid, and muggy for most of the year. In summer the air hangs still and heavy over the trees. The pressure abates with a light sprinkling of showers in the short monsoon that restores the sheen of freshness to the forests. It is in the closing months of the year that the National Park is at peak activity as the lowered temperature and humidity result in an influx of happy, enthusiastic wildlife travellers!

Summer April - June

The long, hot summers take a toll on life in Pench, as the temperatures soar in the 30s as a high average. Residents are sluggish, with muted down activities- animals retire to their hideouts in the shade during the daytime and the forest wears a general vestment of sloth.

Good To Know: Despite the inclemency of the summers, some wildlife buffs, trickle into the forests but it remains difficult to experience Pench to the fullest regardless.

Monsoon July - September

In Monsoon, Pench looks like a kaleidoscope of greens, dotted with condensation, every branch, leaf, and bough sparkling after a cleansing shower. For those living in the forests, these are the days when travel and communication become harder but they can be witnesses to the virginal beauty of the forests.

Good To Know: The rainfall in Pench is moderate to heavy, and these months are also the breeding season for wildlife so the Park remains closed till the middle of October.

FAQs

What is Pench National Park known for?

Pench National Park is known for its rich wildlife, including a significant population of tigers, leopards, deer, and various bird species. The park is also famous for being the inspiration for Rudyard Kipling's "The Jungle Book."

How do I get to Pench National Park?

The nearest airport to Pench National Park is Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar International Airport in Nagpur, approximately 90 km away. The nearest railway station is also in Nagpur. From Nagpur, visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach the park.

What is the best time to visit Pench National Park?

The best time to visit Pench National Park is from November to May. The park is closed during the monsoon season from July to September. The winter months (November to February) are particularly pleasant, while the summer months (March to May) offer better chances of spotting tigers near water sources.

What are the accommodation options in Pench?

Pench offers a range of accommodation options including resorts, lodges, and camps. Popular choices include Pench Jungle Camp, Tiger N Woods Resort, Tuli Tiger Corridor Resort, and Pench Tree Lodge.

What are the safari options in Pench National Park?

Visitors can opt for jeep safaris and elephant safaris. Jeep safaris are available in the morning and afternoon and provide an excellent opportunity to explore the park and spot wildlife. Elephant safaris offer a different perspective and are usually conducted in limited areas.

What are the entry points or gates to Pench National Park?

The main entry gates to Pench National Park are Turia Gate, Karmajhiri Gate, Jamtara Gate, and Khursapar Gate. Turia Gate is the most popular and frequently used entry point.

Are there any local attractions or activities outside Pench National Park?

Nearby attractions include the Pench River, Rukhad Wildlife Sanctuary, and Pachdhar Pottery Village. Visitors can also explore local villages and enjoy nature walks, birdwatching, and cultural experiences.

What should I pack for a trip to Pench National Park?

Pack light and comfortable clothing suitable for the season. Neutral-colored clothing is recommended for safaris to blend in with the surroundings. Don't forget essentials like sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, binoculars, and a good camera for wildlife photography. Warm clothing is necessary for early morning and evening safaris during the winter.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in the Pench region?

The cuisine in the Pench region includes a mix of Maharashtrian and Madhya Pradesh dishes. Local specialties include Poha, Sabudana Khichdi, and traditional tribal dishes. Most resorts and lodges offer a variety of Indian and continental cuisines to cater to tourists.

Is Pench National Park safe for tourists?

Pench National Park is generally safe for tourists. It is advisable to follow the guidelines provided by park authorities and guides during safaris and to avoid venturing into the forest alone.

Can I find guided tours or naturalists in Pench National Park?

Yes, guided tours and experienced naturalists are available in Pench National Park. They can enhance the safari experience by providing valuable insights into the flora, fauna, and the park's ecosystem.
