Mysuru

With its majestic Mysore Palace, grand festivals, splendid hills, and scenic lakes, Mysore is the perfect city to feel royal and luxurious.

Visit the Mysore and Jaganmohan Palaces to witness their extravagance firsthand, along with the temple cities of Ranganathaswamy Srirangapatna and Nanjanagud. The glassy lakes of Karanji, Kukkarahalli and Blue Lagoon are the perfect places to see a beautiful sunset. Do remember to purchase the famous silk of Mysore and try the delicacy of Mysore Pak in great abundance!

Best time to visit

The great Dusshera festivities of Mysore make October to February the perfect time for you to visit. You get to indulge in the vibrant celebrations of Diwali with all its visual extravagance of lights and fire-crackers and the period is apt for adventures like trekking, skydiving, and camping. With occasional showers and beautiful skies, it makes for ideal visits to viewpoints and lakes.

Trivia

This city of glittering royal heritage and a popular tourist destination, was once the capital of the Wodeyar Dynasty around seven thousand years ago.

Karanji Lake

Jockey Quarters, Mysuru, Karnataka 570011

Located at the base of Chamundi Hills, Karanji Lake is a sanctuary for thousands of residential and migratory birds like pelicans, ibis, cormorants, egrets, painted storks, and a myriad of other exotic winged creatures. Tourists are usually inquisitive about hitting the butterfly park at the centre of the lake.

Good To Know: A walk along the longest walk-through aviary or a boat ride on the serene waters or a view from the high observational tower looking upon the entire plethora of greenery is truly grand and overwhelming.

Kukkarahalli Lake

Kukkarahalli, Saraswathipuram, Mysuru, Karnataka

In the soft light of the early evening, Kukkarahalli Lake takes on a touch of mystic aura with the hazy Chamundi hills on the horizon. This picturesque setting was an inspiration for poet laureate Kuvempu who penned down several verses on its quiet waters, perfect reflections, and the surrounding natural appeal.

Good To Know: Located inside the University of Mysore, exotic birds like Little Cormorants, Openbill Storks, Eurasian Spoonbills, Black-crowned Night Herons, and Oriental Darters glide across the waters during winter.

Himavad Gopalaswamy Betta Hill

Himavad Gopalaswamy Hills Rd, Sri Hangala, Karnataka 571126

At a height of 1454 m, Himavad Gopalaswamy Betta is extensively timbered. The temple of Venugopalaswamy here is often visited by pilgrims. A fascinating sight is the minuscule figures of the animals eating their way into the woodland. The guest house at the peak looks back on these expeditions with rare photographs of the Maharajahs and royal aristocrats with their prized tigers

Good To Know: Located in the Bandipur National Park, it is frequented by elephants, Sloth Bears, and the elusive Tiger, sometimes even visible to the naked eye.

Mysore Palace

Sayyaji Rao Rd, Agrahara, Chamrajpura, Mysuru, Karnataka 570001

Resident to the Wadiwar dynasty, this grand palace at the centre of Mysore is the most popular tourist destination in the city. The three-story grey granite building with pink-marble domes and huge central arches creates a picturesque image of Royalty. The frescos on the ceiling, intricate wall art, detailed carvings on pillars, life-like paintings, murals, display of arms, and stained glass windows overwhelm one and all.

Good To Know: Built in an Indo-Saracenic style, the architecture blends Gothic, Rajput, Hindu, and Mughal into a composite magnificence.

Jaganmohan Palace

8J4X+QW5, Jagan Mohan Palace Road, Subbarayanakere, Chamrajpura, Mysuru, Karnataka 570004

Constructed by Krishnaraja Wodeyar III in a chiefly Hindu style, the Jaganmohan Palace housed the royal family during the construction of the Mysore Palace. Its white glistening walls dotted with intricate religious motifs, crafted miniature temples and extensive use of stained glass is a soothing sight.

Good To Know: It has the Sri Jayachamarajendra Art Gallery with a collection of over 2000 paintings, including Raja Ravi Verma’s depiction of Indian mythology in oil, and others by Nikolai Roerich, Rabindranath Tagore, and Jayachamarajendra Wodeyar.

Lalitha Mahal Palace

Lalitha Mahal Palace Road, Lalithmahal Nagar, Siddhartha Layout, Mysuru, Karnataka 570028

Converted into a five-star heritage hotel, the Lathilal Mahal was built in 1921 for the Viceroy of India in a purely Italian palazzo style on an elevated ground. Its white walls seem to sparkle in the sun and its surrounding landscape gardens give a Victorian essence to the place. With a wide courtyard, marble floors, a Belgian glass dome, balustrade staircases, portraits of royalties, and carved wood shutters, a stay at this place is a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Good To Know: The palace lights up in a golden hue in the evening, giving it its best look.

Srirangapatna

Karnataka 571438

Situated by the river Kaver, Srirangapatna is a temple town located 18km from Mysore in the Mandya district of Karnataka. Originally built by the Ganga Dynasty in the 9th century, its architecture has traces of Hoysala and Vijayanagar styles. Ranganathaswamy Temple

CMFH+XJ4, Srirangapatna, Karnataka 571438

Though built by Thirumalaiah, the Ganga Chieftain, the Ranganathaswamy Temple in Srirangapatna has notable traces of Vijayanagara, Hoysala, and Wodeyars' contributions. Ramganatha, the deity is depicted in a colossal image of a reclining posture. The temple has stood through floods and has been invaded repeatedly over the centuries. A walk around the sacred grounds with shrines and intricate sculptures can transport you to a time in history.

Good To Know: It is the largest functioning Hindu Temple in the world and one of the Divya Desams dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

FAQs

Which is a better place to visit, Old Digha or New Digha?

The choice really does lie with the visitor because preferences matter. While Old Digha’s spaced-out hotels and calm shores are perfect for those on a honeymoon, New Digha’s sandy shores, proximity to the park and aquarium and star-rated hotels make it frequently visited as well. Since they’re only 3kms apart, it’s possible to experience both the calm and the bustle.

Which are the top beaches of Digha?

The Old and New Digha beaches along with Udaipur and Shankarpur Beaches are some of the cleanest in West Bengal and offer a charm of their own. Whether you seek seclusion in the waters in a romantic getaway or the classic sandy shores, both can be found on these beaches. The food stalls and water sports available only make the whole experience more exciting.

Are water sports available in Digha?

They are available in abundance on all the beaches, especially the most frequented New Digha Beach. The stalls provide you with equipment and assistance for any activity of your choice. You can be sure to have your enjoyment multiplied with the dash of adrenaline this will bring you. Wearing suitable clothes that won’t wear you down with their weight once wet or fly about if you’re in the air is highly advisable as that makes the experience all the more pleasant.

Which are the best places to eat in Digha?

With the place rapidly developing, the eateries and restaurants in Digha are increasing in number and standard. Local food stalls and those by the beach are sure to give you the perfect savouries to snack on while you laze on the beach or indulge in water sports. Other restaurants that will cater to your seafood cravings and match your expectations are The Bite Restaurant, Mahabhoj Restaurant, Restaurant Bhorpet, Aqua Blues and Koshe Kosha. If you’re looking for cheaper options but good quality food, the Bay Cuffe Restaurant and Elgin Restaurant will fit the bill. All in all the food experience in Digha is one to look forward to and delight in.

What is the fastest route to Digha from Kolkata?

The fastest way to travel to Digha is by cab. It takes approximately 2 hours 33 minutes to get there but the travel can be a bit pricey, ranging from Rs 3,000 to Rs 4,000. Cheaper options will take you longer to reach Digha but the journey will be equally exciting if you’re surrounded by your loved ones. You can take a train from Howrah Station and reach in 3 hours 25 minutes, tickets will be priced anywhere between Rs 150 and Rs 850. Buses from Esplanade will take you there in 4 hours 15 minutes and can charge you Rs 150 to Rs 350.

Is any music or dance festival hosted in Digha?

If you enjoy music and dance performances, visiting Digha in December is what will make your holiday even more pleasant. The town celebrates the Digha Beach Festival during this time when a multitude of stalls is set up for the carnival. You can relish the delicious appetizers, play games, or watch the music recital and dance showcase that features performers from all over the country.
