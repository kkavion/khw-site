Hassan

Hassan is just the place to visit for its scenic beauty, Hoysala architecture and the calmness it offers.

The picturesque city of Hassan with its panoramic beauty, magnificent Hoysala architecture, typical of Karnataka, manifested in its age-old temples, distinctive culture and crafts combined with the various kinds of cuisines is the perfect place to rejuvenate your soul. The peaceful calmness and tranquility of this place is in perfect harmony with the purity of its atmosphere surrounding the numerous temples and ancient monuments. At a distance of around 187 kms from Bangalore, the city of Hassan, which acquires its name from its presiding Goddess deity Hasanamba meaning ‘Smiling Mother’ in Kannada, lies sprawlingly in the Malnad and Maidan districts of Karnataka.

Must Visit

In Hassan, visit Bahubali Statue, Rosary Church and Manjarabad Fort. Inspect the exquisite 12th century temples of Koravangla, Kedareshwara, Ishvara and Hoysala. Go trekking in Kabbaligere Hill, rafting and kayaking in the Tungabhadra River. Try the filter coffee, shop for silk, sandalwood and ivory.

Best time to visit

Winter is the perfect time to get lost in a beautiful place like Hassan. You would need no one, but a heart to feel, a mind to get lost and a cold atmosphere to experience to the fullest. The weather is perfect to scout the temple towns and spend hours gazing at its beauty without feeling even a bit of exhaustion.

Trivia

Hassan is also famously known as the temple architecture capital of Karnataka due to its historic temple towns.

Interest
  • Historical

Hassan is a historical destination, known for its proximity to the ancient Hoysala temples of Belur and Halebidu. These UNESCO-listed sites showcase intricate carvings and remarkable architecture from the 12th century, making Hassan a gateway to Karnataka's rich cultural and historical heritage.

Shravanbelagola

Shravanbela Gola (Rural) Karnataka 573135

A major Jain pilgrimage site, the origin of Shravanabelagola dates back to the 3rd century BCE. Numerous dynasties, including the Mauryas, Chalukyas, Rashtrakutas, and Hoysalas, have lived here through the years. The massive monolithic monument of Lord Gommateshwara Bahubali is the primary draw here. Lord Bahubali is portrayed standing, his body covered in vines and ant hills, signifying his extended period of austerity and meditation.

Good To Know: Shravanabelagola has good road connectivity from Hassan, the closest train station being 52 km away.

Bucheshwara Temple

Chandpur Sea Beach, Chandapur, West Bengal 721423

The historic Hindu temple known as Bucheshvara Temple is situated in the Karnataka district of Hassan near Korvangla village. It is a modest but exquisite example of Hoysala architecture from the 12th century. Rich officer Buchiraja constructed the shrine to commemorate the coronation of Hoysala King Veera Ballala II. Lord Shiva is the object of devotion at the Bucesvara Temple.

Good To Know: In addition to Bucheswara Temple, the area is home to two other temples honouring Govindeshwara and Nageshwara.

Ishwara Temples

8796+926, Huliyar Rd, Muzawar Mohalla, Arsikere, Karnataka 573103

In Arasikere, there existed an extensive network of Hindu temples, including the Ishvara temple. The majority of these, along with its artwork, were demolished and disfigured. To the immediate north of the Ishvara temple within the present compound is the smaller and more damaged Shivalaya twin temple.

Good To Know: There are red-stone pillars in this double temple with a shared hall. Shiva lingas can be found in the two sanctums, and the hall is held up by twenty-four red stone pillars with twenty-one lotus-filled ceiling niches.

Kedareshwara Temples

6X5V+HXJ, Kedareshwara Temple Rd, Halebeedu, Karnataka 573121

The town of Halebidu is home to the Kedareshwara Temple devoted to Lord Shiva. Its elaborate carvings, minute sculptures, decorative embellishments and star-shaped floor design are superb illustrations of Hoysala architecture. The temple suffered deterioration over the ages, in part because of invasions. It has since undergone restoration and preservation in order to retain its historical and architectural relevance for visitors.

Good To Know: The Hoysaleswara Temple is another temple of architectural repute in the town of Halebidu.

Bisle Ghat

City Palace, collectorate, Mohalla Ladiya, Alwar, Rundh Bhakhera, Rajasthan 301001

Bisle Ghat is renowned for the Western Ghats' picturesque appeal, thick forests, and breath-taking panoramic views. Nature enthusiasts, photographers, and people looking for a peaceful getaway all frequent this location. A well-liked viewpoint offering a broad perspective of the Western Ghats is the Bisle Viewpoint. It provides an amazing view of the Dodda Betta, Kumaraparvatha, and Pushpagiri peaks.

Good To Know: The Jenukallu Gudda is a prominent hill that adventure seekers hike to get a wonderful view of the surrounding hills and valley.

FAQs

What are the must-visit tourist attractions in Hassan?

The must-visit attractions in Hassan are Hoysaleswara Temple in Halebidu, Chennakesava Temple in Belur, Shravanabelagola (Gomateshwara Statue), Shettihalli Rosary Church, Manjarabad Fort and Gorur Dam.

What is the best time to visit Hassan for tourism?

The best time to visit Hassan is between October and March, when the weather is nice and ideal for sightseeing.

How do I get to Hassan?

Hassan is accessible via road and rail. The nearest major airport is Kempegowda International Airport in Bengaluru, which is around 185 kilometers away. Hassan has its own railway station, which is well connected to major cities in Karnataka and surrounding states.

What are the best places to stay in Hassan for tourists?

The best tourist accommodations can be found at Hoysala Village Resort, Mallige Residency, Southern Star, Hotel Raama and The Ashhok Hassan.

What local cuisines should tourists try in Hassan?

Tourists should try local South Indian dishes like Ragi Mudde, Akki Roti, Bisi Bele Bath, and desserts like Mysore Pak and Holige.

Are there any guided tours available in Hassan?

Yes, there are various guided excursions available in Hassan that visit significant attractions like as Halebidu, Belur, and Shravanabelagola. These tours can be booked through local travel companies or hotels.

What are some off-the-beaten-path attractions in Hassan?

Some off-beat attractions in Hassan are the Shettihalli Rosary Church, Bisle Ghat, Mosale Hoysala Temples and Ramadevara Betta Hill

Can tourists engage in any adventure activities in Hassan?

While Hassan is well renowned for its historical and cultural features, adjacent Sakleshpur provides adventurous activities such as trekking, camping, and touring coffee farms.
