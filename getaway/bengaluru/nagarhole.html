Nagarhole

A splendid rendezvous of wilderness with serenity, Nagarhole National Park is a magnificent cluster of untouched natural elements in their best form.

The gurgling roar of the Bengal Tiger, an elephant enjoying amidst the lush forest, or the Ghost of Kabini (black panther) eerily eyeing you from a distance not so far away are sights common to Nagarhole. Sharing boundaries with Bandipur Tiger Reserve and Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary, it is separated from the two by the majestic Kabini Reservoir, which in itself is a sight to behold. It houses the King Cobra, Indian Rock Python, Russel's Viper, tortoises, and even monitor lizards. Cloaked with a quilt of green and peace, Nagarhole welcomes the seekers of solitude with open arms and a warm heart.

Must Visit

Hop onto the back of an elephant to spot rare birds and take the boat safari to go deeper into the parts of the jungle that may not be covered in a jeep safari, to explore the Kabini River. Countless birds, marsh crocodiles resting on the shore, and playful otters make up the scenery. Visit also the gurgling Iruppu Falls and Kerala's Wayanad Tholpetty Wildlife Sanctuary.

Best time to visit

The rocky terrain results in the wheels of the safari jeep bouncing and rolling as the animals peek and pop amidst the leaves, Nagarhole is full of surprises. Winter is the best time to visit since the safari and other activities can be enjoyed to their fullest, with a comfortable ambience to keep you hyped up for more. The flora of the park comes out fresh and welcoming for the visitors of Nagarhole during this time.

Trivia

Known to be the erstwhile hunting ground for the rulers of the Wodeyar dynasty, it derives its name from words in Kannada that mean snake and stream due to its many serpentine streams.

Interest
  • Wildlife

Nagarhole National Park is special for its thriving biodiversity, offering sightings of Bengal tigers, Asiatic elephants, leopards, and diverse bird species. The park’s mix of dense forests, streams, and open meadows creates an ideal setting for wildlife safaris.

Nagarhole National Park

Karnataka

Situated in two districts – Mysore and Kodagu, Nagarhole gets its name from Nagara meaning Cobra and Hole meaning River. Hence, it signifies serpentine streams traversing through the tropical forests of Nagarhole National Park. It houses around 250 species of birds and equally great varieties of antelopes, mammals, and reptiles.

Good To Know: It is teeming with a myriad of wildlife like spotted deer, civet cat, tiger, elephant, sloth bear, jackal, panther, mongoose, hyena, leopards, barking deer, wild boars and four-horned antelopes.

Iruppu Falls

Brahmagiri range, Kurchi, Karnataka 571250

River Lakshmana Tirtha cascades down the Brahmagiri Range as Iruppu Falls. Rameshwara Shiva Temple on the banks of the river is en route to this enthralling fall. Both the temple and the river have legendary folklore associated with them through epics like Ramayana which gloriously narrates their existence. The pristine water of Iruppu Falls is considered to be ideal for bathing for its sacred mystic powers of purification of all sins.

Good To Know: It attracts tourists during Navaratri and monsoons when the Iruppu is full after a torrential downpour.

Wayanad Tholpetty Wildlife Sanctuary

X325+MQ5, Tholpetty, Wayanad, Sultan Bathery, Kerala 673592

The picturesque landscape of this wildlife sanctuary owes primarily to its hilly terrains that intersperse through dense valleys that offer jeep and elephant safari. Elephants, Gaur, Tiger, Panther, Spotted Deer, Sambar, Barking deer, Sloth bears, Wild hog, Nilgiri langur, Bonnet macaque, Wild canine, etc are some of the animals that inhabit the Wayanad timberland. The sanctuary boasts of 203 species of birds and 45 of reptiles and mammals.

Good To Know: Wayanad shares boundaries with Nagarhole and is 12 km away from it, as well as the Iruppu Falls.

Sultan Bathery

Wayanad, Kerela 673592

Sultan Battery’s entire complex was built by Tipu Sultan as a machinery and artillery base; the word “bathery” denotes the firing of canons. Engulfed by extensive plantations of tea and cardamom, the air here has an added scent of peace. There’s a feeling of warmth around this location that attracts tourists here mostly during winter. Adventure activities like banana boat rides, canoeing, windsurfing, and more can be enjoyed here.

Good To Know: It is 78 km from Nagarhole National Park, with Muthunga and Wayanad wildlife reserves being in its proximity.

Bandipur National Park

Karnataka

As you cruise through the paths of Bandipur National Park on an elephant back or a Jeep, animals of a colourful variety shall pass you by like next-door neighbours meeting in a hallway. Teak and sandalwood trees stand tall and bright in the maze of the park. An integral part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve, exploring this natural goldmine is made easier by the well-managed National Park.

Good To Know: It teems with endangered animals like tigers, elephants, deer, and panthers as well as a kaleidoscope of flora and forests.

Local Transport Rental

Owing to the muddy and rough terrain within the boundaries of the national park, local modes of transport mostly include the licensed jeeps and the vehicles of the forest authorities posted there. Foreign or outside vehicles are expected to be parked within the stay premises and are generally not allowed movement within the protected area. Traffic is strictly under check and often, prohibited, during monsoon, thanks to it being the mating season, as well as a time of immense natural adversities within the park.

FAQs

How much are the entry fees for the Park?

The entry fees for the visitors are as follows: Indians – INR 200 Foreigners- INR 1000 Video Camera- INR 1000.

How do I commute to the park from Bengaluru?

Commutation to the park from Bengaluru isn’t a problem at all, for there are cars under the state as well as private organizations, available for booking at pocket-friendly rates. There arem’t many buses operating on the route, but a KSRTC Rajhamsa runs from the city’s main terminus to Kuutta, which is 7 kms from Nagarhole.

What is the best time for spotting wild animals at Nagarhole?

The ideal time to visit this park for a worthy experience is that between the months November-February, for the weather remains favourable for the comfort of the tourists, as well as a bonus for rare sightings to be made.

Are there Indian restaurants inside the park premises?

The park premises apart from having the jungle resorts and authority offices, doesn’t have much. However, there are Indian restaurants located outside the park at menial distances, serving delicious food.

How can I book for the safaris?

Bookings for the safaris can be made in both online as well as the offline modes. The park officials can be contacted for the purpose.

What is the rush season at Nagarhole?

Nagarhole mainly faces a rush in bookings regarding both safaris as well as accommodation, during the winter season. This is because the weather is largely ideal for jungle adventure during this time, especially after the adversities of monsoon clear out.

Are there trains connecting Nagarhole?

As of now, there aren’t any trains connecting Ngarhole specifically. However, the nearest Mysore Junction is a central railway station connecting the region to the rest of the country. Cars and buses can be availed from the station to reach Nagarhole.
