Alibaug

Resplendent with sandy beaches, crystal blue waters, and abundant fresh air, located in the heart of nature, Alibaug is the perfect weekend getaway.

Stretches of land with elegant temples here and there coupled with coconut groves alongside tranquil beaches, contain the perfect solace you are in search for. Alibaug brims with possibilities besides offering a striking serenity. While experiencing gusts of cool wind falling on your face, it will give you a chance to enjoy sunsets and silences. Besides, getting an adventurous thrill of water sports, you can readily explore the captivating fauna of Kanakeshwar Forest. To get a taste of nature in its absolute bliss, Alibaug should be a must visit place on your bucket list!

Must Visit

Alibaug is such a place that it can be visited anytime to spend a pleasurable weekend. The scene is mostly always splendid. However, if you wish to visit it at a time when its most suitable to Indian weather conditions, winter would be your window. The atmosphere is cool and will allow you to explore the area without becoming a perspiring mess.

Best time to visit

Indulging in water sports and exploring the wonderful creatures of nature in the Kanakeshwar Forest is a must. Kihim Beach, Alibaug Beach, Kanakeshwar Devasthan Temple, Kolaba Fort and Hanuman Temple are some of the famous destinations to enjoy. If you wish to travel a little further, you can also visit Murud and Janjita Fort.

Trivia

Alibaug is a loved destination for eminent personalities like Shah Rukh Khan, Sunil Gavaskar and Sachin Tendulkar who have their own bungalows there.

With hills, beaches and an abundance of greenery, Alibaug is a great avenue for the thrill-seeking. Jet skiing, parasailing, banana boat rides along with trekking to nearby hills and forts, such as the Kankeshwar Temple trek are all available to enjoy.The rising and falling waves at Digha’s beaches welcome a diverse range of adventure sports. You can soar in the sky on a parasail or bob on the water on a banana boat. These adrenaline-inducing activities is Digha’s bright calling card.

Alibaug's charm is a bit of an oddball, making it unique and more personal compared to the chaotic energy of Goa. Its serene beaches, historic forts, and tranquil environment allow you to escape bustling tourist spots and indulge in a relaxed retreat, offering a laid-back coastal experience.

A stunning coastal backdrop of pristine beaches, azure waters and luxury resorts provides the perfect setting for a coastal wedding. Some popular wedding venues include the Radisson Blu Resort & Spa and the Taj Exotica that gear up to make your ceremonies memorable.

Modern amenities with a serene backdrop make Alibaug a good MICE destination for conferences and events. Popular spots include the Radisson Blu Resort & Spa and the Taj Exotica, which provide spacious meeting rooms, outdoor settings, and comprehensive event services.

Khanderi & Underi Islands

Maandwa, Thal, Maharashtra

The islands which are now bustling tourist spots, were once used as fortified observatories to keep a check on the Murud-Janjira Fort. The story of the Siddis at Murud Janjira being kept under close supervision dates back to 1600 C.E. The fort has not succumbed to dilapidation, and most of it remains intact till the present day. The structure is largely a lighthouse, which is used to date for navigation purposes, and is a splendour to intake.

Good To Know: You can explore the islands and its forts by taking a ferry from the port that is only 2.8 km away from Kihim Beach.

Kanakeshwar Forest

Maharashtra

With its poignant lush greenery, Kanakeshwar forest is renowned for the overflow of flora and fauna. Besides, a melange of animals like snakes, panthers, leopards and wild boars are found here. Kanakeshwar Forest is an absolute stressbuster in ways inexplicable. There are guides and gunmen for hire, to cater to the needs of tourists who desire a safe enjoyable experience.

Good To Know: The forest is full of poisonous snakes and hence, precautions while travelling within it are more than mandatory.

Kanakeshwar Devasthan Temple

Kankeshwar Mandir, Alibag, Maharashtra 402201

Kanakeshwar is a prime spot for tourist beeline in Alibaug. The Kanakeshwar Devasthan Mandir which is dedicated to Lord Shiva, is located at a distance of 13 km from Alibaug. It is a spectacular experience from the top and is visited by both pilgrims and tourists alike. There is another temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman, within the premises. Both attract tourists from every part of the country, all year round.

Kolaba Fort

Alibag, Maharashtra 402201

The 300-year-old Kolaba Fort was once a chief base for the naval forces. There are old temples, significant walls, artifacts, cannons, and carvings of animals and birds among several other possessions within the boundaries of the fort. Situated in the sea, Kolaba Fort is a protected monument under the Archaeological Survey of India.

Good To Know: The Ganesha Mandir inside built by Rahoji Angre is revered, and still worshipped with offerings, by the local fishermen.

Murud-Janjira Fort

Murud, Raigad, Maharashtra

The turquoise waters glistening like a giant mirror owing to the golden sunshine is what Murud-Janjira Fort is surrounded by. Emperor Malik Amber was behind the foundation of this magnificent monument which has its gate of entrance standing proud and sturdy with a carving of a tiger trapped by six majestic elephants. This fort is a tall, strong remnant of the glorious past it had been associated with. This spot is a must, for the grandeur of it is bound to leave you overwhelmed.

Good To Know: The fort is only an hour and a half away from Alibaug.

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Alibaug?

The most prominent attractions in Alibaug are Alibaug Beach, Kolaba Fort, Murud-Janjira Fort, Kashid Beach, Nagaon Beach and Kanakeshwar Forest

How do I get to Alibaug?

Alibaug is around a three- to four-hour journey from Mumbai and 4-5 hours from Pune. The nearest train station is Panvel (about 30 kilometers away), while the nearest airport is Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport in Mumbai.

What is the best time to visit Alibaug?

The best time to visit Alibaug is in winter, from November to February, when the weather is mild and suitable for beach sports and sightseeing. Monsoon season (June to September) provides lush flora, however beach activities may be limited owing to frequent rainfall.

What are the accommodation options in Alibaug?

Alibaug has a wide range of accommodation options, including resorts, hotels, cottages, and homestays. Popular options include Radisson Blu Resort & Spa Alibaug, U Tropicana Alibaug, and The Fern Silvanus Resort.

What are the activities and things to do in Alibaug?

Relaxing and swimming at Alibaug Beach, exploring the historic forts of Kolaba and Murud-Janjira are a must. Water sports like jet skiing, parasailing, and banana boat rides will add the thrill to your holiday! Visiting local temples, markets, and enjoying seafood at beachside shacks will make your beach vacation complete.

Is Alibaug a family-friendly destination?

Yes, Alibaug is a family-friendly destination that offers activities for all ages, such as beach picnics, fort tours, and cultural experiences.

What are some nearby attractions or day trips from Alibaug?

Some nearby attractions from Alibaug are: Kashid Beach (approx. 30 km away) Murud (for Murud-Janjira Fort, approx. 50 km away) Revdanda (for Revdanda Beach and Fort, approx. 20 km away) Korlai Fort (approx. 20 km away)

What should I pack for a trip to Alibaug?

Pack light, comfortable clothing appropriate for the beach and casual events. Sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses are necessary for sun protection. Carry rain gear and slip-resistant footwear during the rainy season.

Are there any cultural or local festivals celebrated in Alibaug?

Alibaug celebrates many local festivals and events throughout the year, such as Ganesh Chaturthi, Diwali, and Holi. During the peak tourist season, the town also conducts cultural programs.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Alibaug?

Alibaug serves a range of seafood dishes, including fresh fish, prawns, and crabs cooked in the traditional Maharashtrian and Konkani traditions. Popular foods include fish curry, sol kadhi, and modak.

Is Alibaug safe for tourists?

Alibaug is typically regarded as safe for travelers. However, like with any holiday site, it is prudent to use normal safety precautions, particularly during the monsoon season when sea conditions can be unexpected.

Can I find adventure activities or sports in Alibaug?

While Alibaug is more renowned for its peaceful beach activities and fort excursions, adventurers can enjoy water sports including as jet skiing, parasailing, and banana boat rides on some beaches.
