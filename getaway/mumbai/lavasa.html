Lavasa

Immerse yourself in glimpses of the Portofino of India and relish the ideal blend of infrastructure and nature.

This hill city located in Mose valley, Pune was stylistically inspired by Portofino's cotton candy harbour and privately designed decades ago by Ajit Gulabchand. The houses decked up with multitudinous colours are flanked by verdant hills. Stretched across seven hills and a lakefront, this new hangout destination allows for a quick leisure trip. To appreciate substantial downpours and a cornucopia of foliage-cosseted landscape, numerous tourists rush to Lavasa for their weekends. Regardless of whether you wish to simply sluggishly unwind at a swanky resort or need some fun and thrill, Lavasa is replete with choices.

Must Visit

Visit the Mulshi dam, delight in water rides at Vortex Splash Pad Water Park, undertake the entrancing Waterfall Safari and thrilling sports at Xthrill Adventure Academy. Dine at The Deck and The All American Diner and buy exquisite bamboo artefacts from Bamboosa. To shed all your travel fatigue, indulge in signature therapies at The Karvi Spa.

Best time to visit

Lavasa exudes a pleasant aura all year round. The ease of winter's serene weather makes September-March the best time to experience its beauty. You can also consider the festive seasons of Lavasa to choose this as an apt time to visit. The Annual Cultural Festival and Christmas among many other festivities mirror the culture through the active participation of the locals and tribal street performances.

Trivia

Lavasa is famous for water sports because it is favourably located on the banks of Warasgaon Lake.

Interest
  • Offbeat

A planned township inspired by European architecture, Lavasa is a dream that looks resplendent with its lakeside setting along with hill stations as its backdrop. Since it doesn't see a throng of visitors, it retains a quiet charm.

Raigad Fort

Raigad, Maharashtra 402305

Among all the fortresses vanquished, constructed and re-established by Shivaji, this fort was the Capital. The fort stands erect atop of the Murumbadevi Dongar which means the Mountain of the Goddess Murumba. The uppermost point of the fort has remnants of caves, cisterns and palaces. The main gateway is known as the Maha Darwaja.

Good To Know: Whether it is the trekking trail or the aerial tramway up the fort, photographers can’t leave out a moment without capturing the panoramic view of the landscape.

Among the plethora of reasons to visit Lavasa during winter, the view of the rolling mounts and the ambience of chilly winters top the list. November to February are the apt months to relish the winters of Lavasa. You can indulge in a jubilant train tour across the city in trackless trains, a bicycle, segway or plan out a pontoon boat ride to cruise around the city.

Good To Know: Bike rides along the trails of Mulshi Ghat and Lavasa -Temghar Ghat on chilly days are moments to be cherished.

Airway New Pune International Airport

If you want to get to Lavasa by flight then you can choose either Mumbai airport or Pune airport. At a vicinity of 65 km, The New Pune International Airport is preferable to Mumbai Airport which is at a distance of 193 km. Both airports are well-connected and serve domestic and international flights like Go Air, Vistara etc. Once you reach the airport, the easiest way to go to the main city is by booking a cab from the airport.

Railway Pune Junction

Although there isn't any train connectivity to Lavasa, you can avail of trains from Pune Junction which is at a distance of 61.6 km from the city and is the nearest railway station to reach Lavasa. From the railway station, you can hire a rental car or book your cab.

FAQs

What is Lavasa famous for?

Lavasa is well-known for its location on the Warasgaon Lake’s shore which makes it a favourable destination for water sports and activities.

Can I travel to Lavasa by two-wheeler?

Driving through the hills is a great experience and you can surely enjoy two-wheeler ride to Lavasa too by riding 30kms up the hill through the Western Ghats but safety comes first hence; a helmet is compulsory.

How far is Lavasa from Lonavala?

The distance between Lavasa and Lonavala by road is 114kms and 40kms by flight.

How many days trip is required for Lavasa?

2 days – 1night is good enough for an all-round complete trip of Lavasa.

Is Lavasa a failed hill-city?

Although Lavasa is a new go-to destination for many tourists, many construction works are left incomplete and there have been protests ongoing by villagers for years as they claim to be duped for their lands that had been undertaken for the Lavasa project.

Is any music or dance festival hosted in Digha?

If you enjoy music and dance performances, visiting Digha in December is what will make your holiday even more pleasant. The town celebrates the Digha Beach Festival during this time when a multitude of stalls is set up for the carnival. You can relish the delicious appetizers, play games, or watch the music recital and dance showcase that features performers from all over the country.
