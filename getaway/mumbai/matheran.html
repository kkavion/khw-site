Matheran

Matheran's shaded forests, cobbled pathways lined with tall coniferous trees and stunning lookouts opens up the untouched world of nature.

Being the smallest hill station of India, Matheran holds a unique charm with rolling hills blanketed in a lush green cover of forests. Its undiscovered beauty makes it an ideal destination for nature-lovers. Literally translating to “Forest on the Forehead”, the virginal natural beauty of the cosy little hill station is a treat for the eyes and can only be explored by foot or ponies. By paying the meagre fee of Rs 50 (and Rs 25 for children), the world of unsullied nature replete with dainty cobbled paths and quaint hill tops with otherworldly views lies open to you!

Indulgence

Sunset Point, Echo Point, One Tree Hill and Charlotte Lake are some spots where you can lose yourself in the untouched beauty of nature. Visit the Prabalgad Fort for a look back at history, try paragliding, trek through the numerous hidden forest trails, and shop for Kolhapuri chappals and excellent quality leather bags.

Best time to visit

Monsoons in Matheran are magical as the entire town is snuggly wrapped in a blanket of mist and the abundant waterfalls bordering the road to Dasturi from Neral add to that image. Winters are cold and genial with chilly nights and days that are perfect for sightseeing and hiking through the forest trails. For a comfortable stay, the best time to visit Matheran is from September to December.

Trivia

After being declared as an eco-sensitive region, all automobiles were banned within the town limits making Matheran the only automobile-free hill station of Asia!

Louisa Point

Matheran, Warose Tarf Wankhal, Maharashtra 410206

Good To Know: Located in the Raigad district, Louisa Point is about half an hour away from the Matheran market on foot.

Charlotte Lake

Matheran, Maharashtra

As the sun's rays touch the vast expanse of Charlotte Lake, the water shimmers as if made of finely cut diamonds. Surrounded by forests and the hills glowing in the background, the place seems to be out of a postcard. During monsoons, the water overflows and creates a splendid waterfall that attracts its own set of visitors. 2 km from Matheran, it can be easily reached on foot and is frequented by photographers and bloggers alike.

Good To Know: The Pisarnath temple gives the place religious importance.

Prabalgad Fort

Prabalmachi, Maharashtra 410206

This old but majestic fort can be seen from some of the famous viewpoints of Matheran. Built at a distance of 46 km from Matheran, this hilltop fortress was constructed by the powerful Bahmani Sultanate to keep a strict watch on the forts of Panvel and Kalyani.

Good To Know: The steep climb to the fort is often considered to be a great hiking trail by adventure seekers, its base being around three km from Vardoli village.

Owing to its hilly origin, Matheran holds a number of caves tucked in perfect corners, carefully hidden from the unassuming eye. Chanderi caves are one such example of the Matheran caves and are delightful for their heritage and adventure value. Belonging to the Chanderi mountain range, you can trek to these gorgeous caves which extend to a height of 800 m.

Good To Know: Falling on the way from Badlapur to Karjat, it is recommended to plan an overnight stay for a wholesome experience of the place.

Ambarnath Temple

Ambernath Prakash Nagar Shiv Mandir Road, 421, 501 East, Ambernath, Maharashtra 421501

The intricate carvings of the temple and its pillars make it resemble the Dilwara Temples of Mount Abu. The heritage shrine made of black basalt stone is sure to have you captivated by its age-old beauty. The Shiv Temple annually hosts Shiv Ratri which is an important festival in Matheran and the celebration continues for four days.

Good To Know: Inside the temple, once you climb down twenty steps, you would find the mysterious Swayambhu linga which presented itself on its own.

Located at an altitude of approximately 800 m above sea level, Matheran is pleasant throughout the year. During monsoon, this hill station receives moderate to heavy rainfall which lends a sort of ethereal beauty to the town, but at the same time increases the risks of landslides. Matheran in winter is filled with enthusiastic tourists and the chilly air is filled with joy.

Summer April - June

The Summer season which stretches from the month of March till the month of June is usually pleasant, with a cool breeze blowing through the valley. With an average summer temperature of 25 degrees Celsius, Matheran is a hiker's paradise.

Good To Know: It is advised to wear light, comfortable clothing and shoes which are suitable for long distance walking and hiking.

Monsoon July - September

The town receives moderate to heavy showers during the monsoon months (July-September). During this time the hills of Matheran are adorned by beautiful waterfalls, most of which are caused by the overflowing of the lakes like Lake Charlotte.

Good To Know: While monsoons are not safe for outdoor adventure activities, you can always take a soothing walk to one of the viewpoints and drink in the intoxicating sight of the waterfalls.

Winter December - February

Being located in the comparatively lower Western Ghats, Matheran never experiences snowfall during winters. However, in the months of December and January the temperature drops abruptly, especially at night and the weather is generally chilly.

Good To Know: This cold weather and the clear winter skies make it a perfect time for adventure activities like paragliding and extensive trekking.

Railway Karjat Junction

Matheran is connected to the Neral railway junction by a narrow-gauge toy train which does not ply during the monsoon months due to the risk of landslides. The 8 km distance between Neral and Matheran can be covered via cars. An alternate option is the railway station of Karjat Junction which is approximately 19 km from central Matheran.

Roadway State Owned Bus

Matheran is an automobile-free town which means that no cars or buses are allowed to ply within town limits. While State transport buses are available, they are only allowed till Neral from where cars are allowed as close as Dasturi Naka from where visitors either choose to walk till Matheran or ride a pony.

FAQs

Which is a better place to visit, Old Digha or New Digha?

The choice really does lie with the visitor because preferences matter. While Old Digha’s spaced-out hotels and calm shores are perfect for those on a honeymoon, New Digha’s sandy shores, proximity to the park and aquarium and star-rated hotels make it frequently visited as well. Since they’re only 3kms apart, it’s possible to experience both the calm and the bustle.

Which are the top beaches of Digha?

The Old and New Digha beaches along with Udaipur and Shankarpur Beaches are some of the cleanest in West Bengal and offer a charm of their own. Whether you seek seclusion in the waters in a romantic getaway or the classic sandy shores, both can be found on these beaches. The food stalls and water sports available only make the whole experience more exciting.

Are water sports available in Digha?

They are available in abundance on all the beaches, especially the most frequented New Digha Beach. The stalls provide you with equipment and assistance for any activity of your choice. You can be sure to have your enjoyment multiplied with the dash of adrenaline this will bring you. Wearing suitable clothes that won’t wear you down with their weight once wet or fly about if you’re in the air is highly advisable as that makes the experience all the more pleasant.

Which are the best places to eat in Digha?

With the place rapidly developing, the eateries and restaurants in Digha are increasing in number and standard. Local food stalls and those by the beach are sure to give you the perfect savouries to snack on while you laze on the beach or indulge in water sports. Other restaurants that will cater to your seafood cravings and match your expectations are The Bite Restaurant, Mahabhoj Restaurant, Restaurant Bhorpet, Aqua Blues and Koshe Kosha. If you’re looking for cheaper options but good quality food, the Bay Cuffe Restaurant and Elgin Restaurant will fit the bill. All in all the food experience in Digha is one to look forward to and delight in.

What is the fastest route to Digha from Kolkata?

The fastest way to travel to Digha is by cab. It takes approximately 2 hours 33 minutes to get there but the travel can be a bit pricey, ranging from Rs 3,000 to Rs 4,000. Cheaper options will take you longer to reach Digha but the journey will be equally exciting if you’re surrounded by your loved ones. You can take a train from Howrah Station and reach in 3 hours 25 minutes, tickets will be priced anywhere between Rs 150 and Rs 850. Buses from Esplanade will take you there in 4 hours 15 minutes and can charge you Rs 150 to Rs 350.

Is any music or dance festival hosted in Digha?

If you enjoy music and dance performances, visiting Digha in December is what will make your holiday even more pleasant. The town celebrates the Digha Beach Festival during this time when a multitude of stalls is set up for the carnival. You can relish the delicious appetizers, play games, or watch the music recital and dance showcase that features performers from all over the country.
