Matheran

Matheran's shaded forests, cobbled pathways lined with tall coniferous trees and stunning lookouts opens up the untouched world of nature.

Being the smallest hill station of India, Matheran holds a unique charm with rolling hills blanketed in a lush green cover of forests. Its undiscovered beauty makes it an ideal destination for nature-lovers. Literally translating to “Forest on the Forehead”, the virginal natural beauty of the cosy little hill station is a treat for the eyes and can only be explored by foot or ponies. By paying the meagre fee of Rs 50 (and Rs 25 for children), the world of unsullied nature replete with dainty cobbled paths and quaint hill tops with otherworldly views lies open to you!

Must Visit

Sunset Point, Echo Point, One Tree Hill and Charlotte Lake are some spots where you can lose yourself in the untouched beauty of nature. Visit the Prabalgad Fort for a look back at history, try paragliding, trek through the numerous hidden forest trails, and shop for Kolhapuri chappals and excellent quality leather bags.

Best time to visit

Monsoons in Matheran are magical as the entire town is snuggly wrapped in a blanket of mist and the abundant waterfalls bordering the road to Dasturi from Neral add to that image. Winters are cold and genial with chilly nights and days that are perfect for sightseeing and hiking through the forest trails. For a comfortable stay, the best time to visit Matheran is from September to December.

Trivia

After being declared as an eco-sensitive region, all automobiles were banned within the town limits making Matheran the only automobile-free hill station of Asia!

Interest
  • Adventure

Matheran offers an adventure into the untouched beauty of nature. Cut-off from machines, it offers you a chance to explore the terrain and yourself. You can trek to viewpoints like Panorama Point and One Tree Hill, go horse riding along the forest trails, or cross lush valleys for the thrill.

In this protected haven, meandering cobbled tracks lead to the many viewpoints of the town where you can get a splendid view of the celestial bodies lighting up the sky. Founded by the then Governor of Bombay, Lord Elphinstone, the colonial roots are still evident in the architecture and culture of the tiny hill station. Matheran’s serene landscape is dotted by a couple of historical forts and written on their walls are the stories of a glorious past.

Charlotte Lake

Matheran, Maharashtra

As the sun's rays touch the vast expanse of Charlotte Lake, the water shimmers as if made of finely cut diamonds. Prabalgad Fort

Prabalmachi, Maharashtra 410206

This old but majestic fort can be seen from some of the famous viewpoints of Matheran. Built at a distance of 46 km from Matheran, this hilltop fortress was constructed by the powerful Bahmani Sultanate to keep a strict watch on the forts of Panvel and Kalyani.

Good To Know: The steep climb to the fort is often considered to be a great hiking trail by adventure seekers, its base being around three km from Vardoli village.

Irshalgad Fort

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Matheran?

The must-visit attractions in Matheran are Panorama Point, Echo Point, Charlotte Lake, Louisa Point, One Tree Hill Point and Alexander Point.

WHow do I get to Matheran?

Matheran is accessible by road and rail. The nearest railway station is Neral, approximately 21 km away. From Neral, tourists can take the narrow-gauge Matheran Hill Railway (toy train) or hire a taxi to Dasturi Naka, the entry point to Matheran. From Dasturi Naka, visitors can walk or hire a horse/hand-pulled rickshaw to reach Matheran town, as motor vehicles are banned.

What is the best time to visit Matheran?

The best time to visit Matheran is from October to May when the weather is pleasant and ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities. Monsoon months (June to September) also offer lush greenery and beautiful waterfalls, but the toy train service is often suspended due to landslides.

What are the accommodation options in Matheran?

Matheran offers a range of accommodation options including hotels, resorts, cottages, and guesthouses. Popular choices include The Verandah in the Forest, Dune Barr House, Adamo The Resort, and Regal Hotel.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Matheran?

Matheran offers a variety of Maharashtrian and Indian cuisines. Local specialties include Vada Pav, Misal Pav, Puran Poli, and Bhajiyas. There are also options for North Indian, South Indian, and Chinese cuisines in the town’s restaurants.

Is Matheran safe for tourists?

Matheran is generally considered safe for tourists. However, as with any travel destination, it's advisable to take standard safety precautions, especially when exploring isolated areas or trekking.

Can I find adventure activities or sports in Matheran?

Yes, Matheran offers adventure activities such as trekking, horse riding, and exploring various viewpoints. The dense forests and hilly terrain provide ample opportunities for hiking and nature walks.
