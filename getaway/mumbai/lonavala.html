Lonavala

Lonavala's serene surroundings and misty clouds will elevate you to the ethereal.

The hilly town of Lonavala is a natural haven for city dwellers wanting to spend the weekend enwrapped in lush greens and misty clouds. The rough off roads steeping upwards the hill create a scarf for the hills. Here you can trek, camp and indulge in thrilling adventure sports. The air is clean and fresh and instantly rejuvenates your body when it fills the lungs. In monsoon, the water pours down through the tracks rinsing your feet with every step you mount. The trees wreathe a symphony of green harmony, as a beat to every step you take.

Must Visit

When in Lonavala, submerge yourself in the scenic views from Tiger's Leap, Lion's Point and imbibe the serenity of the Lonavala Lake and waterfall. Trek up to the Lohagad and Rajmachi Forts and uncover the ancient ruins of the Karla and Bhaja Caves for a trip back in time while munching on some sweet chikkis. For some thrilling rides, visit the Della Adventure Park!

Best time to visit

The winter months from November to February bestow a romantic atmosphere and your visits to the caves and forts will be smoked in a misty lustre. During the monsoon months from June to September, the Kataldhara waterfalls get their fill and rappelling is available. The forests and caves are freshly washed and make a splendid scene.

Trivia

Dubbed the Jewel of the Sahaydri Range, Lonavala was first discovered by Sir Elphinstone, governor of Bombay in 1811 AD.

Lonavala contains a furious energy where you can set out in an adventure on trekking routes like Rajmachi and Lohagad Fort, or go rappelling and rock climbing. Paragliding for a bird's-eye view of the lush landscape and exploring the caves of Karla and Bhaja are equally thrilling.

Majestic mountains and scenic views form the backdrop of this dreamy wedding destination. There are many popular luxury resorts like Hilton Shillim and Fariyas Resort that can welcome you to celebrate the occasion of your wedding with upscale amenities.

A serene setting with top-notch facilities makes Lonavala the ideal place for corporate events. Its lush scenery and luxury hotels like Hilton Shillim and Fariyas Resort provide the perfect backdrop for conferences, team-building activities, workshops, and retreats.

Kurvande, Maharashtra 410205

Bhaja Caves

Bhaja Caves Road, District, near Bhaja Village, Maval, Lonavala, Maharashtra 412106

The Bhaja Caves is an assemblage of 22 rock-cut caves which was built circa the 2nd century BC. It is located in Lonavala, in the village of Bhaja. These caves belonging to the Hinayana sect of Buddhism represent Buddha symbolically rather than in his physical form.

Good To Know: The most distinguishing feature of this protected monument is the assembly hall or the Chaityagraha of Cave XVII. The other noteworthy feature of architectural interest is the 14 stupas (dome shaped shrine).

Karla Caves

Karla, एकवीरा देवी रोड, Lonavala, Maharashtra 410405

You will feel the looming power of the invisible in this indo-Buddhist shrine in the stories carved on the grey walls. To reclaim power and land the Rajas used the spirit of the divine and in devotion erected this structure. The sculptural merit will remind you of the ancient Vedic art forms and history will dawn upon you like rust and corroding stones being reclaimed and recast.

Good To Know: Don't be daunted by the 300 steps you need to climb to reach this shrine as a beautiful synthesis of history and nature awaits you.

Lohagad Fort

Lohagad Trek Rd, Maharashtra 410406

Built like the arms and shoulders of the hill itself the fort holds steady within the green bowers of the hill. The name of the fort conveys that it is as sturdy as the hill itself. The view becomes grander as you ascend. The clouds seem closer, trees greener and the huge opening of the mouth of the fort is beckoning. A small range connects this fort to the nearby Visapur Fort.

Good To Know: You'll have to climb 350 steps to reach the fort. You must take special care while visiting in monsoon.

Rajmachi Fort

Pune, Maharashtra 410405

The soft moss spreads like a green mat along with the brown roughness of the hill slope. You trek or ride up to the Rajmachi Fort at an apex where the gods unite and the clouds resound their commands. The twin fortresses Shriwardhana Ballekila and Manaranjan Balekilla retain the Hemdpanti style of architecture of 13c Maharashtra.

Good To Know: It reveres the Hindu God, Shiva with its fortifying build that replicates his strength and the harmony with Nature in which it rests.

Visapur Fort

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Lonavala?

The top tourist attractionsin Lonavala are Tiger's Point and Lion's Point, Bhushi Dam, Rajmachi Point, Karla Caves, Lohagad Fort and Tung Fort.

How do I get to Lonavala?

Lonavala is easily accessible by road from Mumbai (approx. 2 hour drive) and Pune (approx. 1.5 hour drive). The nearest railway station is Lonavala itself, well-connected to major cities like Mumbai and Pune. The nearest airport is Pune Airport, approximately 65 km away.

What is the best time to visit Lonavala?

The best time to visit Lonavala is during the monsoon months from June to September when the landscape turns lush green and waterfalls come alive. Post-monsoon (October to February) is also pleasant with cool weather, ideal for sightseeing. Summers (March to May) can be hot but are suitable for outdoor activities.

What are the accommodation options in Lonavala?

Lonavala offers a variety of accommodation options including resorts, hotels, villas, and guesthouses. Popular choices include The Machan, Fariyas Resort Lonavala, Della Resorts, and Upper Deck Resort.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Lonavala?

Lonavala offers a variety of Maharashtrian and local hill station cuisines. Specialties include Chikki (a sweet made of jaggery and nuts), Vada Pav, Misal Pav, and Bhel Puri. There are also options for North Indian and Chinese cuisines in restaurants and eateries.

Can I find adventure activities or sports in Lonavala?

Yes, Lonavala offers a range of adventure activities including trekking, rappelling, rock climbing, zip-lining, and paragliding (in nearby Kamshet). Adventure enthusiasts can also explore caves and historic forts with guided tours.
