Panvel

With the mélange of a panoramic backdrop and intriguing historical remnants, Panvel offers you the chance to unravel in nature and discover comfort and quiet.

Panvel lands you sedated within the laps of nature. The lush green Matheran Mountains rise to the east, as a blunt cape, after having rolled for miles on verdurous grounds, and to the west lies a ruined Fort of Karnal atop a mossy rock face, intriguing and intimidating. Forts built under the great Shivaji still stand erect and manage to inspire. Giving the benefits of city life and also intimacy with nature with its bird sanctuary, and scenic Gadeshwar Dam backdropped by the majestic Matheran Hills, this city is just perfect to get away from the routine life at home.

Must Visit

If you crave a camp experience, the Prabalmachi Fireflies Night Trek is ideal for you. Go trekking to Prabalgad Fort and through Kalavantin Durg. The Dahi handi festival is a celebration you shouldn't miss along with sighting rare birds at the Karnala Bird Sanctuary. Indulge in the Agri Cuisine of Panvelities and take home a box of their modaks.

Best time to visit

Winter with its moderately cool temperatures and complacent atmosphere can enable you to make the most of your short trip. The clouds arrange themselves densely around the peaks, vibrant birds gather around lakes and the water ripples across the surface, over-brimmed by the recent rains. Such sights can induce you to absorb the beauty and the tranquillity to the T.

Trivia

Lying close to the Panvel Creek, this town forms a gateway to the Konkan Coast and had been used as a strategic trading ground for the Mughals and Marathas.

Interest
  • Offbeat

Panvel is less traversed, and its beauty, still untouched. Its proximity to the Karnala Bird Sanctuary, Kalavantin Durg, and Prabalgad Fort is another drawing card. Lesser-known spots and the solace they offer take the cake as compared to crowded tourist hubs.

Karnala Bird Sanctuary

Karnala, Panvel, Maharashtra

This haven for birds in the historic Karnala Fort houses over 222 species of birds including the Indian grey hornbill, red-vented bulbul, owl, and paradise flycatcher. The area witnesses hiking expeditions in plenty and it is a true delight for photographers and bird lovers to be cooped up in the sheds, watching these magnificent creatures flying about.

Good To Know: Species like Small Sunbird, Nilgiri Woodpigeon, White-cheeked Barbet, and Malabar Grey Hornbill are endangered and unique to this region.

Karnala Fort

Karnala, panvel, Maharashtra

Constructed in the 15th century and used as a strategic viewpoint, the Karnala Fort is surrounded by a beautiful bird sanctuary. It can only be reached through a precarious trek up the steep slopes. The lower level bastion anticipates the astonishing appearance of the higher level fort, with the watchtower of Pandu at its centre soaring to a height of 125 ft.

Good To Know: With the forts of Chanderi, Sankshi and Dronagri visible, the Karnala Fort, 439 m above sea level, offers a sight never seen before.

Panvel, Maharashtra

With the fierce jagged Mathern hills towering in the background and the image of the landscape reflected upon the waters of the river, the Gadeshwar Dam offers a placid sense of completeness. Trekking your way here will allow you to explore the Peb Fort, Chanderi Fort, and the Mhaismal Hills.

Good To Know: Although it is inadvisable to travel in monsoon seasons, it is during this time of the year that the river brimming with water attains an unbreakable ferociousness.

Prabalgad Fort

Panvel, Maharashtra

Built originally by the Bahmani sultanate to spy over the Kalyan Fort, Prabalgad Fort later served as a resort during the collapse of Ahmednagar, and put up a relentless, yet fatal fight against the Shivaji’s attack. The Uhas, the Gandhi and the Patalganga River marks its east-west frontiers, while the Karnala Fort marks its north.

Good To Know: With steep climbs and jagged rock structures, this is a difficult trek but ensures the most expansive view. From the village of Shedung, it is a three hours journey to the top.

Irshalgad Fort

Digha, Purba Medinipur, Kolkata, West Bengal 721428

Irshalgad has an interesting structure with cisterns cut deep into the rock and a rugged black form towering over the entire valley. The mossy verdurous grounds stretch along the slopes and the pinnacle, shaped like a cone rises further up comically. The expansive view of the Kalavantin Durg and the rivers Gandhi, Ulhas and Patalganga is perfect for self-discovery and comfort.

Good To Know: From Chauk railway it takes around 2 hours to summit and it is preferable to travel alone to minimise risks to your family.

Beth El Synagogue

Panvel, Maharashtra

Originally constructed with simple bricks and pertaining much to the architecture of 18th century India, Beth El Synagogue is now a marble structure of white Mangalore tiles. The insides are beautifully designed with antique oil lamps, high ceilings, and long wooden benches and when the rabbi reads out in Hebrew, one feels transported to a whole different land.

Good To Know: It is one of the oldest sights of the Bene Israel congregation in the Raigad district.

Ballaleshwar Pali

Panvel, Maharashtra

The Balleshwar temple in Pali, designed in the shape of the auspicious Shri, and renovated with stone, is placed in a manner that the first sunrise falls upon the deity and illuminates the place with a divine glow. It is the first Ganesha murti to be named after its devotee, forming a part of the Ashtavinayak temples. The diamond-studded murti seems to emanate life from it.

Good To Know: Tourists also flock to see the bell brought back from Vasai after the defeat of the Portuguese by Chimai Appa.

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Panvel?

When in Panvel, some sites that you absolutely must visit are Karnala Bird Sanctuary and Karnala Fort, Ballaleshwar Pali (Ashtavinayak temple), Gadeshwar Dam, Prabalgad Fort, Kalavantin Durg (Kalavantin Pinnacle) and Haji Malang (Malang Gad).

WHow do I get to Panvel?

Panvel is well-connected by road and rail. It is a major junction on the Mumbai suburban railway network and also connects to long-distance trains. It is accessible by road from Mumbai, Pune, and other nearby cities. The nearest airport is Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport in Mumbai, about 45 km away.

What is the best time to visit Panvel?

The best time to visit Panvel is during the winter months from November to February when the weather is cool and pleasant for outdoor activities. Monsoon season (June to September) is also a good time to visit if you enjoy lush greenery and waterfalls, though trekking can be challenging due to slippery paths.

What are the accommodation options in Panvel?

Panvel offers a range of accommodation options including hotels, resorts, and guesthouses. Popular choices include Hotel Panvel Palace, Devanshi Inn, Pallavi Avida, and Atasa Resort.

What should I pack for a trip to Panvel?

Pack light and comfortable clothing suitable for walking and trekking. Carry warm clothing for the cooler months and rain gear for the monsoon season. Comfortable walking shoes are essential.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Panvel?

Panvel offers a variety of Maharashtrian and Konkani cuisines. Local specialties include Vada Pav, Misal Pav, Puran Poli, and seafood dishes. There are also options for North Indian, South Indian, and Chinese cuisines in the city’s restaurants.

Can I find adventure activities or sports in Panvel?

Yes, Panvel offers adventure activities such as trekking to various forts and hills, birdwatching in Karnala Bird Sanctuary, and exploring scenic spots like Gadeshwar Dam. Adventure enthusiasts can also enjoy climbing and rappelling at Kalavantin Durg.
