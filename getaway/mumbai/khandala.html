Khandala

Escape the monotonous city life in the long, meandering streets, towering greenery, historical sites, and scenic beauty of Khandala.

A narrow road bisects the hills clad in lush greenery. The hilltops seem eager to kiss the never-ending sky as a blanket of mist and matted cloud enwraps their zenith. From a distance, you hear the faint trickle of a waterfall soothing your senses inured to the cacophony of daily life. Maharashtra's Khandala has often been the respite for many locals. Equipped with versatile hills and sprightly waterfalls, this town is apt for a weekend getaway. Indulgent, affordable, and accessible Khandala lets you enjoy the small town pleasures. You can trek through the trails of The Tiger's Leap and Duke's Nose and explore Buddhist cave temples, forts, and British vestiges.

Must Visit

Visit the Bhaja caves to soak in the calmness and trek through lush greenery in the trails of Tiger's Valley and Duke's Nose. Drive around town to let the undisturbed strains of nature seep into you and do not forget to carry chikki, the candy-bar that is the perfect combination of gooey and crunchy back home.

Best time to visit

The best time to visit Khandala is the monsoon months. The dry and heaving landscape is spirited by the rains. The waterfall and the lakes are shot back to life- the rains like epinephrine to the pulsating heart. A warm cup of chai or a bowl of Maggi and you are all set to enjoy the Khandala.

Trivia

This hilly town made it to pop culture with the song “Aati Kya Khandala” from Amir Khan's popular movie Gulam.

An ideal spot for trekking, Khandala is visited for its verdure and pleasant temperatures. Perched atop the Bhor Ghat, it has several valleys and moors that look particularly stunning in monsoon. With Buddhist influence for many a century, Khandala’s origin is shrouded in considerable mystery. After witnessing great rulers like Shivaji and the Peshwas it finally came under the British who used it as a getaway to beat the heat.

Bhaja Caves

Bhaja Caves Road, District, near Bhaja Village, Maval, Lonavala, Maharashtra 412106

The Bhaja Caves is an assemblage of 22 rock-cut caves which was built circa the 2nd century BC. It is located in Lonavala, in the village of Bhaja. These caves belonging to the Hinayana sect of Buddhism represent Buddha symbolically rather than in his physical form.

Good To Know: The most distinguishing feature of this protected monument is the assembly hall or the Chaityagraha of Cave XVII. The other noteworthy feature of architectural interest is the 14 stupas (dome shaped shrine).

Karla Caves

एकवीरा देवी रोड, Lonavala, Maharashtra 410405

The Karla Caves like the Bhaja Caves are a group of rock-cut monasteries that were constructed between 2nd century BCE and 5th century CE. Out of the collection of 16 caves, the most stunning is the Mahachaitya (Cave VIII).

Good To Know: It has survived almost completely unblemished and its rows of structured pillars make it seem like an optical illusion.

Tiger's Leap

Kurvande, Maharashtra 410205

The Tiger’s Valley provides a panoramic view of the surrounding hilltops and the enveloping foliage. It appears as though the valley is leaping into the vastness like a tiger for which it is named so. The high altitudes and the numerous rocky outcroppings and hills emanate clear and resounding echoes which is why this place is sometimes called the Echo Point.

Good To Know: The Tiger’s Leap is patronised by many trekkers and it is especially beautiful during the monsoons when the waterfall is nourished back to life.

Duke's Nose

Kurvande, Maharashtra 410402

The Duke’s Nose derives its name from its semblance to the long nose of the Duke of Wellington. (Naturally, this cliff seems to be projecting outwardly like a protruding nose). It is also known as Nagphani (hood of the snake) for its structure that appears to be opening up into the void.

Good To Know: A popular trekking trail, the Duke’s Nose can be a little arduous for absolute novices. However, you’re guaranteed commendable clicks while traversing along the trail.

Bhushi Lake

Maharashtra 410401

6 km from Lonavala, the Bhushi Dam, constructed on the Indrayani River enjoys frantic tourist involvement during the monsoons. With the advent of the rains, the frothy waters gush down over a series of steps, flooding the senses of those witnessing the magical sight. It is like a waterfall over the steps. The experience is communal and harmonious and yet another uniting factor for tourists during the monsoons.

Good To Know: Be sure to carry proper footwear for the steps can be pretty slippery.

Lonavala Lake

Lonavala, Maharashtra 410401

This lake is the epitome of peace and quietude. Surrounded by trees on all sides, Lonavala Lake is an idyllic place to relax or rendezvous with your friends and family. It is sometimes referred to as the Monsoon Lake because it is the most stunning when the rains renew the placid water body.

Good To Know: The beauty of Lonavala Lake can be witnessed after a short trip of about 14 minutes from Khandala and all of it will be worth it!

Ancient Jail

Khandala

Although fairly neglected and forgotten, the Ancient Jail at Khandala is a reminder of the Jesuit’s struggle in British occupied India. This prison was constructed in 1896 and the Jesuit priests that founded St. Xavier’s College, Bombay were incarcerated by the colonial authority as prisoners of war.

Good To Know: The old British Jail may seem spooky at times. You can try visiting it after sunset if you get a kick out of the eerie!

FAQs

What to carry to Khandala?

If you’re visiting Khandala during monsoons, carry appropriate footwear, umbrellas and raincoats. Be well equipped to deal with the rains.

What are the drinking laws in Khandala?

Khandala is located in the state of Maharashtra where the legal age of drinking is 25 years. Also, booze tends to be expensive here.

For how long should I stay in Khandala?

A weekend or two days should be enough the tour Khandala. Drive or walk around the town a little bit and definitely visit the Tiger’s Valley, Nagphani and the Bhaja Caves. Go trekking or just relax and soak in hilly airs.

Is Khandala safe?

Khandala is safe with low criminal activities and reports. There are the occasional cases of inconveniences but nothing too serious to frighten you.

Who should tag along on a Khandala trip?

Go solo, with your friends or partners. There aren’t that many things to do in Khandala so it might not entertain your kids all that much.
