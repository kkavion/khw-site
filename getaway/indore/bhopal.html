Bhopal

The perfect amalgamation of the historic, cultural and modern, explore Bhopal, the city of lakes with its Mughal architecture, nawabi cuisine, hills, parks and adventure sports.

Bhopal's greens surely keep up with what Madhya Pradesh is known for and it also surprises with its myriad offerings. Bespeckled with edifices of historical and cultural importance, the second cleanest city in India paints a picture that is intimately entwined with its glorious past. Its numerous lakes, both natural and artificial, and seamless blend of the old and new in the north and south of the Upper and Lower Lakes draw several. The meandering alleys, regal havelis backdropped by a bucolic landscape complemented by the contemporary developments, bustling bazaars and Mughlai cuisine exhibit incontestably that Bhopal is the upcoming weekend getaway to explore.

Must Visit

If you want a piece of Bhopal's history, visit Gohar Mahal, Madhya Pradesh Tribal Museum, the UNESCO heritage sites of Sanchi Stupa and Bhimbetka and Asia's largest mosque, Taj-ul-Masajid. For adventure sports and your time in nature, boat in the Upper Lake and Lower Lake, go flying fox zipping and ropeway riding at the Kerwa Lake and Bhopal Ropeway.

Best time to visit

The winter months from October to March are undoubtedly the best time to visit the capital city of Madhya Pradesh. The cool weather allows an undisturbed experience of the city without the worries of rain or excessive perspiring. It is also the best time to try out all the adventure sports and roam the intriguing lanes. July to September is the next best season to visit the city when its washed clean by the monsoon rains.

Trivia

Bhopal stands out from all other princely states because of its lineage of four consecutive female rulers who governed the land, each being the daughter of the previous queen.

Interest
  • Historical

Proximity to two UNESCO World Heritage Sites makes Bhopal the ideal place to soak in the history. The 30,000-year-old prehistoric cave paintings at Bhimbetka Rock Shelters and the ancient Sanchi Stupa dating back to the 3rd century BCE lend a doorway into the past.

Bhopal boasts a rich historical past. It was established by Raja Bhoj, a legendary king, and the city's name is thought to stem from his name. Several dynasties, especially the Mughals, have affected it over the years. Bhopal is renowned for its unique cultural legacy. The city contains numerous ancient sites, mosques, and palaces that show its architectural and cultural diversity.

Upper Lake

Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh

Lying next to Kamla Park, the Upper Lake is at the centre of Bhopal and forms an important element of Bhopal's landscape. Raja Bhoj, a mythological monarch of the Paramara dynasty, built the lake in the eleventh century. Originally designed to supply water to the city and its surroundings, it is also a valuable recreational and natural asset for Bhopal inhabitants and visitors.

Good To Know: The Upper Lake features a couple of islands, notably the King Bhoj's Island, which houses the Van Vihar National Park—an open zoo and safari park.

Van Vihar National Park

Lake View Walk Path, Krishna Nagar, Shymala Hills, Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh 462002

Founded in 1983, Van Vihar National Park offers a rare opportunity to enjoy nature's splendour while also helping to conserve wildlife. Van Vihar is a combination of a zoo and national park. It is managed as a national park, but has an open zoo area in which animals can walk freely in a semi-natural setting.

Good To Know: The park provides a unique safari experience, allowing guests to explore the area in their own vehicles or use the park's vehicles. It allows you to watch animals in their natural environment.

Gohar Mahal

VIP Road, Nakkar Khana, Peer Gate Area, Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh 462001

Qudisiya Begum, the first female monarch of Bhopal oversaw the construction of this palace in the nineteenth century. The palace combines Mughal and Hindu architectural elements with its exquisite decorations and elaborate carvings. Visitors to Bhopal frequently visit Gohar Mahal to admire its architectural splendour and learn about its historical importance in the region.

Good To Know: It functioned as a palace for the royal women and was mostly utilised for social and cultural activities, such as parties, celebrations, and festivals.

Sanchi Stupa

Sanchi, Madhya Pradesh 464661

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Sanchi Stupa is a pilgrimage site with well-preserved Buddhist stupas. These Buddhist temples are hemispherical in shape and house sacred treasures. The town of Sanchi offers more than just the Great Stupa with several other monuments, monasteries, and pillars. These monuments comprise an archaeological complex that sheds light on Buddhist art and architecture in ancient India.

Good To Know: The major objective of the Sanchi Stupa was to house Lord Buddha's relics.

Bhimbetka

Bhojpur Raisen, Amchha Khurd, Madhya Pradesh 464990

Famous for its cave paintings and rock shelters dating back to the Upper Palaeolithic period with some being 30,000 years old, Bhimbetka unearths fascinating details about prehistoric life here. 400 out of 700 rock shelters here exhibit ancient artwork. The shelters served as homes, and the paintings represent scenes from everyday life like as hunting, dancing, ceremonies, and numerous animals.

Good To Know: Mahabharata's Bhima is said to have been in this region during his exile and has thus inspired its naming.

Madhya Pradesh Tribal Museum

Shyamla Hills Rd, near State Museum, Shymala Hills, Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh 462002

A visit to the Madhya Pradesh Tribal Museum can enrich your understanding of the culture and heritage of Bhopal's native tribes. They do a very good job of preserving all its unique quirks and celebrate the traditional art and craft styles of indigenous communities. It focuses on the distinct customs, art forms, ceremonies, and lifestyles of the region's tribal groups.

Good To Know: Exhibitions, workshops and cultural programs are commonly held here to promote the tribal life and spread awareness.

Taj-ul-Masajid

NH 12, Kohefiza, Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh 462001

Famous for its grand architecture, the Taj-ul-Masajid is one of Asia's largest mosques and a cultural landmark in Bhopal. Launched by Bhopal's female queen, Shah Jahan Begum, the mosque's design incorporates elements of Mughal architecture. It has magnificent domes, minarets, and a spacious courtyard.

Good To Know: Being an Islamic seminary for religious study, the Taj-ul-Masajid plays an important role in the religious and educational lives of the local Muslim community.

Airway Raja Bhoj Airport

The Raja Bhoj Airport of Bhopal is merely 13 km from the city centre. Since it's an international airport, it successfully connects the city to not only the major cities of India but also of the world. Jet Airways, Air India and various other airlines fly to and from here. You can hop in a cab, take a bus or even tuktuk to get to your desired location from the airport.

FAQs

What is the famous thing in Bhopal?

The Taj-ul-Masjid, Jama Masjid, Gauhar Mahal, Shaukat Mahal, Regional Science Centre, Fish Aquarium, Kerwa Dam, Upper and Lower Lakes, Birla Temple, Van Vihar, Bharat Bhavan, Indira Gandhi National Museum of Man, Manu Bhaun ki tekari, cave temples, etc. are just a few of the tourist attractions in Bhopal.

What food is Bhopal famous for?

Known for its variety of chaat offerings, including chole tikki, pani puri, dahi puri, bhel puri, and ice golas and faloodas, this is the most well-known spot to grab a fast lunch for those in the mood for something sweet. We especially suggest trying the samosa kachori chaat.

What is the famous sweet of Bhopal?

The culture and tastes of Bhopal are rich. This city offers a wide range of cuisine, and Imarti is one of the most well-known desserts among Bhopalis. It's a variety of jalebi that has an orange-red tint, tastes sweeter than regular jalebi, and has a slightly different form. The shaan of Bhopal is Paan.

Best time to visit Bhopal?

Which season is ideal for travelling to Bhopal? The winter months of October through March are the ideal times to visit Bhopal. The majestic city's rich tastes may be explored and experienced thanks to the perfect weather. The second-best period to visit the city is from July to September.

Which is the famous market in Bhopal?

This old-fashioned bazaar is well-known for its crowded aisles, long history, and opulent shopping. This Bhopal market is well-known for its sarees, textiles, and handicrafts. If you are a fan of all things elegant and traditional, you really must visit this historic Bhopal marketplace.
