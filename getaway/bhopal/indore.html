Indore

The splendid palaces, exquisite temples, delectable street food, lively night markets, and a cosmopolitan shopping scene make a trip to Indore satisfying in ways unimaginable.

Indore's charm lies in its ability to adapt. The commercial capital of Madhya Pradesh, it is also a household name for those invested in fashion. The cleanliness, the industrialization, the automobile, and handloom industries are the major calling cards of the city. If you think rapid industrialisation results in a loss of nature, Indore makes it a prerogative to change your mind. Nestled in the Malwa Plateau, with undulated rocky terrain, the lush Vindhya's in the south and swift streams, it can become a haven that allows the perfect infusion of buzzing city life with the harmonious symphony of nature.

Must Visit

The brilliance of the Rajwada & Lalbagh Palaces and Kanch Mandir must be experienced in person. Browse through antiquity in the Central Museum, trek to the Patalpani Waterfalls and explore the Ralamandal Wildlife Sanctuary. Enjoy a thrilling day at Mayank Blue Water Park and sate your palate at Sarafa Bazar and Chappan Dukan.

Best time to visit

Visiting Indore between November and March could make your holiday rewarding in many ways. The weather is cold, enabling a sweat-free sightseeing excursion through the city. You can admire the architectural genius of the palaces, invoke divinity in the serene temples and relish the thriving dining and shopping scene without any limitations. Nature too wears its best suit at this time.

Trivia

Over the years it has managed to retain the title of the cleanest city in India consecutively for a record total of four times!

Interest
  • Historical

The landmarks of the Rajwada and Lal Baag Palaces reflect the princely heritage of the Holkar Dynasty. Kanch Mandir shows their patronage of religion and art, while the Holkar School showcases their role in educational advancements during the Maratha period.

Rajwada Palace

Rajwada, Indore, Madhya Pradesh 452002

The Rajwada Palace, a fusion of architectural styles was built by Holkar emperors of the Maratha Empire. Its construction started in the early 18th century and continued throughout time. With a blend of French, Mughal, and Maratha architectural designs, the palace features elaborately carved windows, balconies, tall columns, and a huge entrance. Mirrors and chandeliers throughout the hall capture the grandeur of the time.

Good To Know: The Durbar Hall, where official events and meetings were held, is one of Rajwada's most significant features.

Lalbagh Palace

PR2W+2RH, Nai Duniya, Revenue Colony, Indira nagar, Indore, Madhya Pradesh 452007

A symbol of glory of the Holkar dynasty, Lal Bagh Palace is a majestic palace combining European, Mughal, and Maratha architectural styles. The majestic gates, expansive courtyards and lovely gardens displaying meticulous craftsmanship. The palace is built of red stone, which gives it a distinguishing aspect and adds to its name, "Lal Bagh" meaning Red Garden. On occasion, the palace welcomes visitors with events, festivals, and cultural exhibits. It remains closed on Monday and open from 10 am on others.

Good To Know: A scene from Kalank was shot here.

Kanch Mandir

Pasari Bhavan, Opposite Kaanch Mandir, Itwaria Bazaar, Indore, Madhya Pradesh 452002

Early in the 20th century, affluent Jain trader Sir Seth Hukumchand Jain constructed Kanch Mandir. The shrine is devoted to the 24th Tirthankara of Jainism, Lord Mahavira. The walls, pillars, and dome of the temple are completely decorated with glass mosaic. Detailed patterns, pictures, and scenarios from Jain mythology are all featured in the glasswork. Visitors are free to peruse the temple's interior and take in the exquisite glasswork.

Good To Know: Certain sections may prohibit photography in order to protect the fragile glasswork.

Ralamandal Wildlife Sanctuary

JWW9+V85, Madhya Pradesh 452020

Home to an array of plant species, including trees, shrubs, and herbs, Ralamandal Wildlife Sanctuary is recognised for its diverse biodiversity, which includes a variety of birds, animals, and reptiles. Common sights include deer, monkeys, peafowl, and various bird species. Visitors may wander the sanctuary's pathways and take in the surrounding natural splendour.

Good To Know: While the sanctuary is not overly commercialised, visitors can find basic amenities such as walking routes and lookout points to appreciate the natural environment.

Mayank Blue Water Park

With a variety of water slides, a wave pool and swimming pool, a discotheque, numerous rides, and a multitude of other activities to select from, Mayank Blue Water Park is without a doubt the finest destination for a family summer picnic. Pipliyapala Regional Park

Regional Park Rd, Pipliya Pala, Pawan Putra Nagar, Indore, Madhya Pradesh 452020

Pipliyapala Regional Park is renowned for its picturesque splendour, which includes a huge lake, gardens, and open spaces. One of its distinguishing attractions is the open-air museum, which displays traditional architecture, sculptures, and artefacts from many parts of India. It serves as an educational area where visitors can learn about the country's rich cultural heritage. It features an amphitheatre where cultural activities, performances, and exhibitions take place.

Good To Know: The park includes a lake where guests can enjoy boating.

Chappan Dukan

FAQs

Is Indore good to travel?

Rich in history and quickly industrialising, Indore is well-known for its night markets, street cuisine, majestic palaces and temples, cotton handloom industry, and more.

What food is Indore famous for?

Indore is well-known for a wide range of deep-fried namkeens, including chikkis, mathris, Indore sev, aloo bhujia, hing peanuts, and khatta meetha, in addition to all the delicious sweets and street cuisine. They go well with hot tea or coffee and are flavorful, crunchy, and ideal for snacking at any time of day.

What is Indore special?

One of the most popular tourist destinations in Madhya Pradesh is Indore, which has been named the cleanest city in India multiple times. The city is home to many well-known tourist attractions, as well as shrines, temples, and an unrivalled street food scene.

What to buy in Sarafa Bazar?

Sarafa Bazaar, a well-known Pushkar market, is visited by both locals and visitors looking to buy souvenirs, leather goods, Rajasthani handicrafts, textiles, silver jewellery, and rose-related items.

Which month is best for Indore?

The months of October through March are the ideal times to visit Indore because the weather is suitable for both sightseeing and outdoor activities. During this time, the temperature varies from 8°C to 32°C.
