Panchmarhi

With greenery and wildlife profuse and delightful, Panchmarhi's sparkling waterfalls and impressive views from hill tops create a world that is secluded in a magic that lets you unwind completely

The lush green landscape, cascading waterfalls and spectacular sights has Panchmari crowned as the ‘Queen of the Satpuras'. It lives up to the name as it creates an environment so enchanting, you'd love getting lost in it. Being a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve only adds more value to its worth. Discovered and developed by Captain James Forsyth during the British Raj, a lot of colonial influence can be seen in its settlements. If the natural façade and wildlife of the region did not make it attractive enough, it is also strewn with several temples, caves and historic monuments that are believed to be proof of a mythological past.

Must Visit

When in Panchmarhi, soak up the nature all around at Dhoopgarh and Priyadarshinin Point. Trek through the Pandava Caves and to the Bee Falls, Apsara Vihar and Duchess Fall; you can even bathe in some of them. Observe the wildlife at Satpura National Park and visit the ancient temples of Jata Shankar and Chauragarh Temple to embrace the divine.

Best time to visit

Panchmarhi is that destination that you can visit anytime you like because of its pleasant weather with soothing winds, waterfalls and the nature all around acting as coolants. The peak season is from March to June when the days are enjoyable and nights cool. You can also choose to experience the Bastar Dusshera, Navratri and Panchmarhi Festival during October to December.

Trivia

Named after five sandstone caves, Panchmarhi is believed to be the residence of the Pandava brothers of the Mahabharat when they were in exile.

Interest
  • Adventure
  • Offbeat

Enveloped by the scenic Satpura hills, Panchmarhi has natural trails, rugged cliffs, and cascading waterfalls that provide the perfect space to venture into adventure sports like trekking, rock climbing, parasailing, and cave exploration.

Nestled in the Satpura ranges, Panchmarhi is the only hill station in Madhya Pradesh and yet retains its unspoiled beauty. This less-frequented spot offers a serene escape into ancient caves, lush forests, and hidden waterfalls that help you rediscover yourself and nature.

Pandava Caves

Pachmarhi Cantt, Madhya Pradesh 461881

The Pandava Caves are a collection of ancient caves located near Pachmarhi. Local stories suggest that the Pandava brothers from the Indian epic Mahabharata found refuge in these caverns during their exile. The caves have been designated after the Pandavas, who are claimed to have spent time in them during their exile.

Good To Know: The caverns are notable for their ancient rock paintings, which show a variety of themes and motifs. These murals shed light on the region's medieval art and culture.

Bee Falls

Pachmarhi, Madhya Pradesh 461881

In the Satpura Range, Bee Falls is one of the primary attractions. The journey to Bee Falls includes descending a staircase surrounded by the natural splendour of the forest. The road to the falls is as wonderful as the destination, with stops to see the flora and fauna along the route. The area surrounding the waterfall is notable for its biodiversity.

Good To Know: The waterfall is simple to get to via road, and there is an area for parking near the entrance. Visitors can get to the falls by walking down a set of steps.

Dhoopgarh

Pachmarhi, Madhya Pradesh 461881

Dhoopgarh provides panoramic view of the surrounding landscape, which includes lush flora, hills, and valleys. It is one of the best sites in the region to see the dawn and sunset. Visitors frequently assemble at the lookout to witness the amazing colours and light play that takes place during these natural occurrences.

Good To Know: The Mahadeo shrine, a modest shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, sits atop Dhoopgarh.

Jata Shankar Temple and Cave

Pachmarhi Cantt, Madhya Pradesh 461881

The Jatashankar Temple is nestled in a cave located in the deep forests of Pachmarhi. Devotees consider the temple sacrosanct, and it is a famous pilgrimage destination in the area. The cave is associated with Lord Shiva, and it is thought to be a spot where he hid to flee the demon Bhasmasur.

Good To Know: In accordance with local folklore, the cavern was a shelter for Lord Shiva, and the name is derived from Shiva's matted locks (Jata).

Satpura National Park

Madhai National Park, Pachmarhi, Badkachhar, Madhya Pradesh 461881

From the Madhai Gate near Sohagpur, you can travel to the Satpura National Park which offers your regular jeep safari and also boat or canoe safaris on forest marshes especially for bird watching and spotting crocodiles. The sight of their mud smeared leathery hides sunning in the shallow waters is one that inspires both fear and limpid fascination for nature.

Good To Know: Elephant and walking safaris offer a great shot at spotting furry critters like sloth bears, nilgai as well as the rare white gaur and the predatory leopards, and Royal Bengal tigers.

Duchess fall

Pachmarhi, Jambu Deep, Madhya Pradesh 461881

Surrounded by lush foliage and natural beauty, the prospect of taking a soothing plunge in the chilly waters of this bizarre water body, the appealing sight of its roaring water, and the abundance of nature make this waterfall a favourite tourist destination. Duchess Falls, also known as 'Jalwataran', is a perpetual stream that flows from a height, dividing into three distinct streams. One may devote some time here by a tiny pond, admiring nature's beauty.

Good To Know: Duchess Fall may be reached by climbing over a steep hill of 4 km from the main road.

Chauragarh Temple

Mahadeo Rd, Pachmarhi, Madhya Pradesh 461881

Chauragarh is a popular place for pilgrimage near noted for the Chauragarh Temple located on its peak devoted to Lord Shiva. The temple is especially popular during the Mahashivaratri festival, which attracts a significant number of devotees. Pilgrims think that offering a bell at the temple grants their wishes, and it is customary for devotees to take bells on their journey.

Good To Know: Mahashivaratri is a prominent event that draws enormous crowds of devotees.

Summer April - June

Temperatures can go up to 35 °C during the summer months but its never truly hot. Since it's a hill station, the cool winds blowing make the days pleasant and the nights are cooler. There are also sporadic bouts of rainfall that bring down the temperature.

Good To Know: This is the perfect time to visit Panchmarhi since despite it being summer, it isn't too hot and you can trek through the hill station. You can also experience Holi and Rang Panchami during this time. If timed well, you can even be present for Mahashivratri Mela.

Airway Raja Bhoj Airport, Bhopal

The Raja Bhoj Airport of Bhopal is merely 200 km from Panchmarhi. Since it's an international airport, it successfully connects the city to not only the major cities of India but also of the world. Jet Airways, Air India and various other airlines fly to and from here. The Jabalpur Airport 285 km away is the next closest option. You can hop in a cab or take a bus to get to Panchmarhi from the airport.

FAQs

What is famous in Panchmari?

The most popular sights in Pachmarhi are Apsara Vihar, Bee Falls, and Satpura National Park.

Which month is best for Pachmarhi?

Pachmarhi throughout the winter months of January, February, October, and November. The winter months, starting in October, are the best times to visit Pachmarhi. The months of October through February are ideal for travel because of the excellent weather and romantic atmosphere.

Which is the nearest Airport to Panchmari?

The closest airport is RajaBhoj Airport, located in Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh. It is situated 200 km from Pachmarhi and 70 km from Narmadapuram. The airport has daily flights to Delhi and Mumbai. A taxi from Bhopal to Pachmarhi is easily accessible.

What are the famous food items in Panchmari?

Madhya Pradesh's regional specialties include Lassi and Sugarcane Juice in addition to dishes like Dal Bafla, Biryani, Kebabs, Bhutte ki Kees, Korma, Poha, Rogan Josh, Jalebi, and Ladoos.

What language is spoken in Pachmarhi?

Although Hindi is the official language, people also speak English, Urdu, and the primary regional languages of Bhilodi, Nahali, and Gondi.
