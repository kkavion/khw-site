Ujjain

The city of temples, Ujjain allows an intimate meeting with the divine in its hallowed temples and an easy immersion into the country's profound cultural history.

The aroma of incense wafting through the air and devoted pilgrims flocking to the numerous temples to offer their prayers are a sight common in Ujjain. Known as one of the seven most sacred cities in India, it plays host to the Kumbh Mela which takes place once in 12 years and gathers over 100 million. Adorned with 108 temples, the city wonderfully exhibits the richness of the Hindu culture. The serene Shipra River, museums and palaces with a distinct style of architecture are an added bonus to this holy city. Home to the Mahakaleshwar Temple, one of the 12 Jyotirlingas in the country, it is the most vibrant during Nagpanchami and Mahashivratri.

Must Visit

To soak in the divine atmosphere and pay reverence to the deities, visiting the Shri Mahakaleshwar Temple and Kal Bhairav Temple is a must. Feel the tranquillity at Gomti Kund and when you take a dip in the Shipra River. Offer up your prayers through an aarti at Ram Ghat. Browse through history at the Triveni Museum and experience the festive fervour at Kumbh Mela.

Best time to visit

The most fitting time to visit Ujjain would be the winter months spanning from October to March. The cool weather offers the opportunity to roam the streets and spend hours at temples without feeling uneasy or uncomfortable due to the heat. The festivities have the city in an uproar, especially in March when the Kumbh Mela is hosted here once every 12 years.

Trivia

Ujjain is characterised as a city with rich cultural heritage and warm people in Meghadūta by Kalidasa, the renowned 5th century classical poet in Vikramaditya's court.

Interest
  • Pilgrimage

Hosting the Kumbh Mela once every four years is the pilgrimage site of Ujjain. Its religious significance is also due to the Mahakaleshwar Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas. The whole city emits a divine calm that allows a spiritual awakening.

Ram Ghat

Fatehpur Sikri, Agra, Uttar Pradesh 283110

Ram Ghat has religious and cultural significance, particularly during the Kumbh Mela. It is regarded a sacred bathing site, and pilgrims come here to take a holy plunge in the Shipra River. Bathing here is thought to purify the soul. Ram Ghat, like many of Ujjain's ghats, offers daily aarti celebrations and devotees come in the evening to offer prayers.

Good To Know: During the Kumbh Mela, which takes place in Ujjain every 12 years, Ram Ghat becomes a focal point for millions of people.

Gomti Kund

Agra Fort, Rakabganj, Agra, Uttar Pradesh 282003

Gomti Kund, a water tank located in tranquil surroundings, was once an important supply of water for Sandipani Ashram. It is claimed that taking a plunge in the tank's sacred water might help heal the ailments of devotees who come here to pray. According to mythology, Lord Krishna collected sacred river water from several pilgrimage sites and deposited it here.

Good To Know: While visiting the kund, be sure to stop by the ashram's temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Kumbh Mela

Ujjain

The Kumbh Mela is a prominent Hindu event and pilgrimage held every 12 years at Ujjain. The festival is part of a broader cycle of Kumbh Melas that take place in four different cities across India: Prayagraj, Haridwar, Nashik, and Ujjain. Millions flock here to take a holy dip in the Shipra River. Taking a dip in the sacred river during Kumbh Mela is thought to purify one's sins.

Good To Know: The Kumbh Mela is not merely a religious celebration, but also a cultural showcase. The event includes a variety of cultural performances, such as music and dance.

Shri Mahakaleshwar Temple

Mahankal Mandir, Jaisinghpura, Ujjain, Madhya Pradesh 456001

Mahakaleshwar, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, has been devoted to Lord Shiva and is regarded his most sacred residence. The temple's architectural styles include Maratha, Bhumija, and Chalukya. The temple's spire is ornately carved and sculptured. It is said that worshipping at this Jyotirlinga aids in salvation and desire fulfilment.

Good To Know: Maha Shivaratri is celebrated with great enthusiasm at the Mahakaleshwar Temple.

Kal Bhairav Mandir

Goyala Buzurg, Madhya Pradesh 456003

Dedicated to Lord Bhairav, a manifestation of Lord Shiva, Shri Kal Bhairav Temple serves as both a religious and cultural centre. Kal Bhairav is regarded as a guardian deity who is said to protect the city of Ujjain and devotees seek his mercies for protection and to overcome challenges in their lives. The temple is renowned for its unique rituals and traditions.

Good To Know: Devotees present liquor to Lord Bhairav as part of their worship, which is seen to be a distinct component of the temple's traditions.

Kaliadeh Palace

Island on the bank of Kshipra River, Madhya Pradesh 456003

Renowned for its stunning architecture and picturesque location, Kaliadeh Palace is located on an island in the Shipra River. It features a mix of Hindu and Mughal architectural elements with the design representing the region's cultural heritage. The palace provides a stunning view of the sunset across the Shipra River, becoming a famous tourist destination.

Good To Know: Visitors can go to the island and explore its surroundings by boat on the Shipra River. It provides a leisure component to the visit.

Triveni Museum

Hari Phatak Over Bridge, Keshav Nagar, Ujjain, Madhya Pradesh 456010

This festival stages the most popular musical drama experience and gives you an authentic feel of being an audience to a dramatic production that also introduces you to the heritage and culture of India. Titled 'The Saga of Love', this romantic historical folk tale is a series of plays staged every day at 6:30 PM. In it, you can feel the history of the ancient rajas and the chivalric pursuits of their ladyship.

Good To Know: You can purchase original artistic pieces as a memento or gift for your near and dear ones from here.

FAQs

What is the famous thing in Ujjain?

The Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga Temple is the main draw of Ujjain. It is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, and Ujjain is renowned for its daily Bhasma aarti ritual, which you should not miss.

What is the famous food of Ujjain?

Ujjain is famed for its Dal Bafla. Bafla is a well-liked meal. The dough balls for bafla One highly popular street snack is aalu bada.

Which time is best for Ujjain?

March to October. When the weather is nice, from October to March—or from November to February, it is the ideal time of year to visit Ujjain. June through September are the months with moderate wintertime temperatures in Ujjain. It rains in the city during these months.

Which pooja is Ujjain famous for?

India's Madhya Pradesh city of Ujjain is revered as a sacred location for Mangal Dosh Puja ceremonies. One of India's twelve Jyotirlingas, the well-known Mahakaleshwar Temple, is where the Mangal Dosh Puja is typically held in Ujjain.

How to attend Bhasma Aarti?

Bookings must be made online or offline in order to attend Bhaishankar aarti. Note that there is no charge to schedule or attend the bhasma aarti. You can make an online reservation by following the guidelines on the Mahakaleshwar website. The Bhaska Arti Booking Counter is open daily from 10 to 3 for offline reservations.
