Kumarakom

Kumarakom is perfect splash of blue and green that will satiate the wanderlust of your soul.

This is a story about a couple of kings who fortified this little village on the banks of the magnificent lake; they didn't really know what would come of this place in the future but now, this little place doesn't give you reasons to stay in your bed, it gives you reasons to go to the snake boat race, to extend your trip to Onam, to stay in your room. With a culture so rich, it will make you never want to leave. The locals will accept you with open arms and their joy will make you feel like you are the most special person in the world.

Must Visit

Visit the Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, trek up the Jayatu Rock, and witness the snake boat race, to see the roars crash against the water. Eat Karimeen Pollichathua, a local dish that is a beautifully cooked fish full of different spices and wrapped in a banana leaf. Buy your Traditional Silk Saree to take home its riches.

Best time to visit

Going to Kumarakom in every season has some perks with it. In summer, the clear waters allow you to see the fish flow and in monsoon, the rain brings relief from the wretched heat during summer. Winter, is a favourite with the cool breeze that allows you to go trekking sweat-free. The snake boat races and the festival of Onam will give you more reasons to visit and return again than to leave.

Trivia

This little town found special mention for its backwater tourism in 2023's New York Times' January issue where Kerela claimed a spot in the top 52 must-see destinations in the world.

Kumarakom's setting allows you an adventure into the blue waters with activities like kayaking, canoeing, water skiing, and houseboat rides through its serene backwaters. You can even birdwatch at Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary or go on fishing tours to imbibe the local appeal.

A potpourri of Kerela's rich culture, Kumarakom's traditional boat races, Kathakali performances, temple festivals, and local art forms and cuisine allow you to completely immerse yourself in its culture and experience the vibrant traditions of the locals.

Though it's a small city, the lush scenery and the cerulean waters make the perfect ambience for you to bond with your partner. Its seclusion from the hustle of bustling tourist destinations allows you to have an intimate experience. Private boat cruises, candlelight dinners by the water, and rejuvenating Ayurvedic treatments add to its romantic charm.

Added to the calm waters and the peaceful environment, Kumarakom has yet more to offer to resuscitate you. Ayurvedic resorts like The Zuri Kumarakom and Kumarakom Lake Resort are some from a list of many wellness retreats that provide holistic Ayurvedic treatments, yoga retreats, and spa therapies.

With an intimate atmosphere, Kumarakom allows you to get up close and personal with your beloved and your families as you tie the knot against its stunning backdrop. Kumarakom Lake Resort and many others offer waterfront settings for ceremonies that blend nature with elegance to make it look as dreamy as you can desire.

Kumarakom can offer you that bout of inspiration that you seek to refresh your mind, reorient yourself, and work more efficiently. Luxury resorts like Aveda and The Zuri Kumarakom facilitate this with their conference facilities. The backwater setting therefore becomes a great spot for corporate meetings, incentives, and retreats.

Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary

Kavanattinkara, Kumarakom, Kerala 686563

The Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary is a forested area sprawling over fourteen acres of land in the small village of Kumarakom in the district of Kottayam. A haven for exotic migratory birds, the sanctuary also appeals to a lot of hikers as it offers beautiful secluded trails that wind through the forests. This is a paradise for both nature lovers and those seeking peace in the silence of the trees.

Good To Know: This refurbished British rubber plantation is home to the rare Siberian Crane as well as to the more common owls, cuckoos, waterfowls, and herons.

Vembanad Lake

Kerala

Vembanad Lake forms the heart of the water channels supporting not just Alleppey but also the neighbouring cities of Kottayam, Kuttanad, and Kochi. The glittering expanse of the lake is dotted with many tiny islands which can be accessed easily from Kochi. Surrounded by swaying palm trees, this serene lagoon forms a major part of the Kerala backwaters and ornate houseboats light up the lake at night, twinkling like stars.

Good To Know: Identified as a Wetland of International Importance by the Ramsar Convention, it is the longest lake in the entire country!

Kumarakom is a small village that is mostly connected to the rest of the Indian cities through Kottayam which is only 16 km away. With the distance to travel being short compared to other towns and cities, there really isn't much need for buses or taxis. Airway Cochin International Airport

The nearest airport is almost 45 km from the nearest city which itself is nearly 15 km from Kumarakom. Though Cochin International Airport is linked to several different airports, it takes a long time to really reach Kumarakom but don't let the distance deter you. The upside is that journeying by road to this quaint little backwater town with greens and blues all around allows you to explore the hinterland and make discoveries of your own.

Local Transport Rental

The little town of Kumarakom is comparatively small and doesn't really need a great deal of bus connectivity; however, there is a bus that comes to this town from the nearest city of Kottayam, and some even from Kochi to Kottayam. Taxis are also available but auto-rickshaws are a lot more popular as they are cheaper. Traversing the land on foot by rickshaws will allow you the opportunity to experience the beauty of the town that you might have otherwise missed.

FAQs

How to reach Kumarakom?

Kumarakom in Kerala, South India is just a few miles (16 km) from the nearest city, Kottayam. As entry point to India; you can fly to Mumbai (Bombay) or Chennai (Madras).

What is Kumarakom famous for?

Kumarakom is an unbelievably beautiful paradise of mangrove forests, emerald green paddy fields and coconut groves interspersed with enchanting waterways and canals adorned with white lilies.

What are the best places to stay in Kumarakom?

The best places to stay in Kumarakom are Lemon Tree Vembanad Lake Resort, The Zuri Kumarakom Resort, and Deshadan Backwater Resort.

What are the places to eat in Kumarakom?

The best places to eat in Kumarakom are Tharavadu Family Resort, Vembanad Kumarakom Lake Resort, and Ettukettu Speciality Restaurant.

What are the top things to see in Kumarakom?

The top things to see in Kumarakom are Ettumanoor Shri Mahadeva Temple, and Old Curiosity Shop Kumarakom.
