Kovalam

Enshrouded by copious coconut and palm trees and outlined by the foamy waves of the Arabian Sea, Kovalam is a versatile beachside town for globe trotters!

Those looking for a seaside haven to relax in the heart of God's own country will find all their answers in Kovalam. Today, the trinity of crescent-shaped beaches in the town, the overarching Lighthouse, and the shallow backwaters have turned it into a hub of tourist activity. You can do a bit of soul-searching in quiet sandy alcoves, spend quality time with loved ones on board a houseboat, and experience the world-famous Ayurvedic spa, all the while keeping a thumb on the frenetic pulse of global tourism!

Must Visit

Visit Lighthouse Beach and sunbathe here, go parasailing or snorkelling in shallow water! The Ayurvedic spas indigenous to Kerala are a must-try. Sample quality seafood focusing on crabs and lobsters. Take back textiles and home decor items. Stay in houseboats that will also take you for a tour of wide paddy fields and green vistas by the bank.

Best time to visit

The months from September to March are considered ideal as the temperatures are between 20-25°Celsius. The refreshing coolness of the sea during the day and the light breeze in the evening allow you to travel without being exhausted by the heat. Some beach fairs and carnivals are also in vogue at this time and will add another fun dimension to your stay!

Trivia

When Tranvancore's regent, Maharani Sethu Lakshmi Bayi built the beach resort of Halcyon Castle here in the late 1920s, Kovalam drew the attention of the locals.

Interest
  • Honeymoon
  • Wedding

Kovalam is an idyllic honeymoon destination with its crescent-shaped beaches, calm waters, and stunning sunsets. Couples can enjoy romantic walks along the Lighthouse Beach, cozy stays in luxury resorts, and rejuvenating Ayurvedic massages, making it a perfect romantic escape.

Kovalam is renowned for its wellness offerings, particularly its Ayurvedic therapies. Luxury wellness resorts like Somatheeram and Niraamaya Retreats offer holistic treatments, yoga sessions, and rejuvenating Ayurvedic massages in a serene beachside setting, making it a wellness haven.

Kovalam's pristine beaches and stunning ocean views make it an enchanting wedding destination. Luxury resorts like The Leela Kovalam and Taj Green Cove offer breathtaking backdrops for beachside ceremonies, providing a perfect blend of nature and elegance for unforgettable weddings.

The quaint village of Kovalam is lined by the sparkling blue of the Arabian sea and dotted with coconut trees. Here, you will find pristine beaches stretching for miles on end, with shallow waters that will allow you to try out a variety of water sports. The natural beauty of Kovalam is in its close proximity to the sea. Lose yourself to the unique experience of soft sands between your toes and feel the untamed depths of the ocean gently lapping at your feet!

Thevally, Kollam - 691009

Houseboats crowd the shores in attractive retinues in Kovalam. An exotic experience, entirely exclusive to the backwaters is staying overnight in them. Good To Know: Ashtamudi Lake and Karamana River are popular choices to access houseboats from.

Lighthouse Beach

Light house Junction, Vizhinjam, near Kovalam, Kovalam, Kerala 695521

Built out of red and white stone, Vizhinjam Lighthouse is perched atop the Kurumkal hillock and looks pensively out into the horizon. Samudra Beach

VP 1, GV Raja Rd, Kovalam, Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala 695527

With a rustic appeal, Samudra Beach is perched on the northern extreme, separated from the Southern side by a promontory, and backed by a margin of stony outcropping. The most interesting view on this beach is that of shoreline fishing, as you have the best seats in the house to observe local fishermen practising their daily trade from close quarters!

Good To Know: The mosque on the Samudra Beach can be spotted easily on the skyline of Kovalam and adds texture to both the natural and cultural fabric of the place.

Hawah Beach

9XPG+QQM, Light House Rd, Kovalam, Kerala 695527

Hawah Beach is interestingly named to remind you of the spiritual and mental freedom you can enjoy on its windy shores. A relatively isolated curve of sandy expanse, the Hawa Beach was the first topless beach of India. Although topless bathing has been banned, this beach is still colloquially known as Eve’s beach and is relatively quieter, having more privacy than the other famous beaches.

Good To Know: The selling point of this beach is the unmatched view of gilded sunsets that you can catch from here, so make sure to stay just long enough!

Halcyon Castle

Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala 695527

Now converted into a luxury hotel, this exquisite castle and its lush green grounds are a dynamic combination that made Kovalam so wildly popular among the British in the past century. Stay within the walls to bask in the heritage of Kerala, enshrouded by palm trees with the constant gust of seawater breeze. Most of the surrounding area is carefully preserved so that visitors can enjoy the natural beauty of the sandy beaches.

Good To Know: You can visit this place to experience the view of the sunset on the Kovalam Beach from its lofty vantage point.

The German Bakery

Light House Beach Rd, Kovalam, Kerala 695527

The Germany Bakery and Waves restaurant share roof space near Lighthouse Beach and this particular eatery has become all too popular among international tourists. The restaurant is set up with a Western ambience and the menu is full of European delicacies like puffs, pastries, breads and croissants! You can enjoy ludicrously beautiful international cuisine while looking out at the uninterrupted view of the beach next door! Veli Tourist Village

GV6Q+2W9, Veli Sanghumugham Rd, Madhavapuram Colony, Veli, Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala 695021

The Veli Tourist Village is an effort on the government’s behalf to amplify and commercialise the natural appeal of the lagoon formed in the mouth of the River Veli. From speed-boats to horse rides, swimming pools to cafeterias and shopping complexes, this village is a one-stop tourist destination. The panoramic beauty of the scenery which is a perfect duo of green trees and blue waters makes for an ideal picnic spot.

Police Station, Vizhinjam, Kovalam, Kerala 695521

Oldest of their kind in Kerala, Vizhinjam rock-cut caves are a natural and historical marvel. The image of Shiva Parvati as well as the icon of the Malayali God Tripurantaka; both draw attention to the artistic elegance of the caves. You will discover that the caves are separated into north and south zones to represent the Chera and Ay communities respectively. The skilful design and brilliance are things you will not find elsewhere.

Good To Know: Look out for the sculptures dating as far back as the 8th century on the rock-hewn cave walls.

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Kovalam?

The top tourist attractions in Kovalam are Lighthouse Beach, Hawa Beach (Eve's Beach), Samudra Beach, Vizhinjam Lighthouse, Kovalam Art Gallery, Vellayani Lake, Halcyon Castle (Kovalam Palace) and Thiruvallam Parasurama Temple.

How do I get to Kovalam?

The nearest airport is Trivandrum International Airport, approximately 15 km away. The nearest railway station is Thiruvananthapuram Central (Trivandrum), about 11 km from Kovalam. Buses and taxis are available for road travel to Kovalam from Thiruvananthapuram and other nearby cities.

What is the best time to visit Kovalam?

The best time to visit Kovalam is from November to February when the weather is cool and pleasant. The monsoon season (June to September) brings heavy rainfall but offers a unique experience with lush greenery. The summer months (March to May) can be hot and humid.

What are the accommodation options in Kovalam?

Kovalam offers a wide range of accommodation options including luxury resorts, mid-range hotels, budget guesthouses, and homestays. Popular choices include The Leela Kovalam, Taj Green Cove Resort & Spa, Uday Samudra Leisure Beach Hotel, and budget-friendly options like Little Elephant Beach Resort and Beach Hotel Neelakanta.

What should I pack for a trip to Kovalam?

Pack light and comfortable clothing suitable for the tropical climate. During the monsoon season, bring rain gear like umbrellas and raincoats. Don’t forget essentials like sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, swimwear, and comfortable footwear.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Kovalam?

Kovalam is known for its Kerala cuisine, including dishes like appam and stew, fish curry, prawns roast, Kerala sadya (a traditional vegetarian feast), and various coconut-based dishes. Seafood is particularly popular, and local delicacies like Karimeen pollichathu (pearl spot fish) are a must-try.

Can I find guided tours or local guides in Kovalam?

Yes, guided tours and local guides are available in Kovalam. Many tourists prefer hiring a guide to gain deeper insights into the local culture, history, and natural beauty of the region. Tour operators offer various packages, including backwater tours, city tours, and adventure activities.
