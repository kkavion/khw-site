Kollam

The land of a thousand shades, the coastal hamlet of Kollam will satiate your wanderlust amidst emerald pearls of the ocean and sunshine peeking through the corners of the coconut and palm groves.

Indulge in the kaleidoscope of cultures accompanied by a regale hint of colonialism as you backpack through the rustic lanes of the picturesque little hamlet against the soothing backdrop of exuberant greeneries and a calming sea. Embrace the euphoric essence building in your soul as you row through the effervescing pristine backwaters of God’s Own Country. Listen to the beautiful gurgles of the ocean as the tranquil silence of the winds keeps you company. Immerse yourself in the lively ethos of togetherness and gently tap your feet along with the rhythmic drum beats and mellow musical notes while soaking in the rustic culture of Kollam.

Must Visit

Immerse your feet into nature's unknown at Kollam Beach. Glide through the air in a cable car and engage in a paintball match at Jatayu Earth Centre. Rejuvenate yourself with ayurvedic treatments, savour the taste of Fish Moilee and take back a variety of tea leaves. Experience euphoria on the special houseboat in the backwaters of Ashtamudi Lake.

Best time to visit

Nestled in the pockets of exuberant coconut and palm groves immersed fully in serenity tranquillity, Kollam has something beautiful to offer all year round. The pristine clear skies and the gentle cool breeze during winter make it the best time to visit Kollam. Engage in a candid heart-to-heart with the spiritual essence of the town and make memories of a lifetime.

Trivia

The 'Land of Cashews', Kollam's port, dates back to a time in history when it was a major commercial centre for ivory, timber and spices.

Kollam offers a unique outlook on adventure. It offers kayaking and canoeing through its azure backwaters, trekking in the nearby Western Ghats, and parasailing and windsurfing at Thirumullavaram Beach. Somehow, along with the thrill, it manages to provide a calm that is unfound elsewhere.

Kollam has a furious energy during festivals like Kollam Pooram with its traditional arts & crafts reflecting its heritage. Its ancient temples, like the 400-year-old Sri Mahadeva Temple, and history as a key port on the Spice Route add to its cultural depth.

Secluded backwaters, a romantic houseboat, and stunning scenery make Kollam the picture-perfect honeymoon destination. You can indulge in private boat cruises with your partner in the Ashtamudi Lake and savour the natural beauty of this quiet coastal retreat.

Something about Kollam feels undisturbed and pure. It's as if time has stopped to let you explore its untouched backwaters, quiet beaches, and lesser-known cultural gems. You can also ride on a houseboat and uncover hidden trails in the Western Ghats.

With an emerging wellness scene, Kollam is now home to several Ayurvedic centers that offer traditional Kerala Ayurvedic treatments and massages, Ashtamudi Wellness Resort being one of them. Rejuvenation amidst Kollam's lush greenery and calm waters is the most gratifying experience.

Airway Trivandrum Intl. Airport

The nearest functional airport is the Trivandrum International Airport, located at a distance of 69 km from Kollam. Established in the year 1932, the airport is connected to most of the major cities of the subcontinent including the Metropolitan states. It also has the provision of being connected to several international destinations such as Doha, Kuwait, Kuala Lumpur, Singapore, etc. From Thiruvananthapuram, embark on a smooth hassle-free ride to the enchanting fortress of Kollam nestled in the verdant greenery.

FAQs

How to Reach Kollam?

Kollam is situated around hour and a half away from the capital city of Thiruvananthapuram, which is well-connected to most of the part of the country with all three means of transport. A ferry boat station in Kollam also connects it to major port cities of India.

What is popular in Kollam?

Kollam has been the centre of cashew industry in Kerala from the time of the Portuguese in 16th c. Having hundreds of working cashew factories in the district, Kollam still continues to be the largest processed cashew exporter in India.

What are the best places to stay in Kollam?

The best places to stay in Kollam are Samiira on Ashtamudi Lake, All Season’s D’Fort Ayurvedic Resort, and Munroe Island Lake Resort.

What are the best places to eat in Kollam?

The best places to eat in Kollam are Fayalwan Hotel, Chef NalaN Restaurant, and Fusion Table

What are the top things to see in Kollam?

The top things to see in Kollam are Thangassery Light House, RP Mall Kollam, and Kollam Beach.
