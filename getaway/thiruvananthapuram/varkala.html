Varkala

With a 2000-year-old temple and pristine waters that cleanse the soul, Varkala teems with a nascent bohemian culture at the rare cliff, offering the thrill of adventure sports and the serenity of the sea.

A vivid splash of blue paints the horizon above and below at the quaint coastal town of Varkala. The golden sands of the Varkala Beach and the several massage parlours along it find a remarkable backdrop in the only cliff that borders the azure Arabian Sea in southern Kerela and is recognised as a national geological monument. The dense verdure on the cliff proffers the most incredible view of the sea and is the site to enjoy the delicious snacks from the eateries that dot the area. The numerous temples, pilgrimage sites and ayurvedic spas make it the ideal place to connect with the divine and rejuvenate your body and soul. Abounding in beaches, hills, lakes, natural springs and forts, it is a haven for one and all.

Must Visit

Sunbathe and surf at the Varkala Beach, visit the Janardana Swami Temple and relax in the various ayurvedic massage parlours. Go paragliding from the cliff, munch on snacks from the shacks and try water sports at Thiruvambadi Beach. Boat in the serene Kappil Lake, picnic at the Anjengo Fort and Ponnumthuruthu Island and pay homage at Sivagiri Mutt.

Best time to visit

Winter (October-March) is the most coveted season for tourists to visit Varkala due to the temperature drop. It's the perfect time to try out various water sports. You can even enjoy the 10-day Janardana Swami Temple and the Shivagiri Mutt Festivals hosted during this time.

Trivia

When a group of pilgrims notified Narada that they had sinned, he urged them to pray for redemption at Papanasam, the precise location of Varkala Beach, famed for its ayurvedic centre and healing springs.

Varkala offers thrilling adventure activities such as paragliding, surfing, and jet skiing along its pristine coastline. Its unique cliffs overlooking the Arabian Sea provide an excellent spot for trekking and rock climbing, making it a hub for adventure seekers.

Varkala's serene beaches, breathtaking cliff views, and tranquil environment make it a romantic destination for newlyweds. The sunsets from the cliffs, intimate beach walks, and cozy beachside resorts provide the perfect setting for a romantic and peaceful honeymoon experience.

Varkala’s relatively lesser-known status compared to Kerala’s other beach destinations lends it an offbeat charm. Its quiet beaches, dramatic cliffs, spiritual vibe from temples, and a mix of adventure and wellness draw travelers looking for a unique experience away from the crowds.

Varkala is home to renowned Ayurvedic wellness centers and spas, including the famous Sanjeevani Ayurveda and Kairali Ayurvedic Health Resort. Its tranquil atmosphere, yoga retreats, and holistic treatments make it an ideal destination for wellness and rejuvenation.

With picturesque cliffside views and pristine beaches, Varkala offers stunning backdrops for beach weddings. Popular venues such as Soul & Surf and Varkala Cliffside Resorts offer both luxury and scenic beauty, making your wedding memorable against the backdrop of the Arabian Sea.

Anjengo fort

MQ77+3QF, Anchuthengu, Kerala 695309

Formerly known as Anchuthengu Fort, Anjengo Fort is a mediaeval fort close to the town of Varkala. The British East India Company built the fort in the 17th century, and it has a rich historical background. It functioned as a crucial defensive and commercial outpost. A historic lighthouse at Anjengo Fort dates back to the British era.

Good To Know: Anjengo Fort's appeal has increased as a result of movie sets using it and its picturesque surrounds.

Shivagiri Mutt

Sivagiri Rd, Varkala, Kerala 695141

A spiritual and cultural hub, the Sivagiri Mutt is located on Sivagiri hill close to Varkala. A significant location for pilgrims and devotees of Sree Narayana Guru's teachings is the Sivagiri Mutt. Sree Narayana Guru's ultimate resting place, or saddhi, is the Mutt. Respect is paid at the samadhi, a place of meditation and devotion, by both tourists and devotees.

Good To Know: The Varkala Tunnel, a historically significant tunnel that links the Sivagiri Mutt to Papanasam Beach, is also connected to the Mutt.

Summer April - June

Monsoon July - September

Monsoon is considered an off-season for tourism in Varkala due to the heavy rains, especially during August. The cloudy skies against the blue of the Arabian Sea will etch itself into your memories.

Good To Know: The temperature and price drop make it a favourable time to travel here when on a tight budget. Onam and Karkidaka Festival also draw many during this time.

Airway Trivandrum Intl. Airport

The nearest functional airport is the Trivandrum International Airport, located at a distance of 41.4 km from Varkala. It is connected to most of the major cities of the subcontinent including the Metropolitan states. It also has the provision of being connected to several international destinations such as Doha, Kuwait, Kuala Lumpur, Singapore, etc. You can reach Varkala in 1 hr 10 min via the Chilakkoor - Vallakkadavu Road by a taxi from there. Varkala also has its own helipad at the Varkala cliff.

Railway Varkala Sivagiri Railway Station

Varkala's Sivagiri Railway Station is a major station after Trivandrum Central on the Kollam-Thiruvananthapuram railway route. The second busiest station in Thiruvananthapuram, it is also known to generate the third highest revenue. It is well connected by regular trains from major Indian cities such as Bangalore, Chennai, Kolkata, Goa, Mumbai, Pune, Delhi, etc. You can take a taxi or an auto rickshaw from the station to your desired location.

Roadway Varkala Bus Stand

Several buses travel to the town of Varkala, many of them pass through Thiruvananthapuram and are operated by the KSRTC. There are some popular private bus services that offer services in the southern states of Tamil Nadu, Karnataka and Kerela which are also opted for by tourists visiting from the nearby cities. You can even hire private cars to drive down the scenic roads. The most common route is that of the NH6 which passes through Thiruvananthapuram.

Local Transport Rental

The town of Varkala is most intimately explored on foot. If you aren't a fan of walks, then you can opt for auto rickshaws but be sure to bargain as there aren't any fixed prices. You can also hire a motorbike or scooter and ride through the blue town. You can also travel to the heart of the city by water through the Varkala Tunnel which is connected to the National Waterway 3 and have a unique experience of the town. The local transports shut down by 9pm, so it is advisable to plan your ride from before.

Van, Auto Rickshaw, Cycle rickshaw, Motor Bike

FAQs

How to reach Varkala?

Varkala is located in the state of Kerala and is well-connected with Thiruvananthapuram. Thiruvananthapuram in turn is accessible from all major parts of India by road, rail and air.

What is Varkala famous for?

Varkala is renowned for its stunning cliffside views overlooking the Arabian Sea, creating a breathtaking backdrop for relaxation and exploration. The town's iconic cliff, known as the Varkala Cliff, is a geological wonder that attracts visitors from far and wide.

What are the best places to stay in Varkala?

The best places to stay in Varkala are Gate Varkala,Indra Pastha Beach Resort, and Hindustan Beach Retreat.

WWhat are the best places to eat in Varkala?

The best places to eat in Varkala are Darjeeling Cafe, ABBA Restaurant, and Seasun Cafe.

What are the top things to see and do?

The top sights to look out for in Varkala are Sivagiri Mutt, Varkala Beach, and Kappil Beach.
