Kottayam

The land of latex, legends and lakes, Kottayam is a vast sea of culture and festival, shrouded with an emerald green and boasting a treasure trove of silk.

East flanked by the mighty and majestic Western Ghats and the west edged with paddy fields and Vembanad Lake, Kottayam is an alluring city situated in the “Spice Coast of India”, Kerala. A bounteous visual treat with prolific rivers, undulating mountains, pristine backwaters, vast rubber plantations, waterfalls, ancient pilgrimage centres and scrumptious delicacies will delight your senses. The first town in India to acquire a 100% literacy rate, this “Akshara Nagari” or “The city of letters”, possesses many regal titles such as the land of latex, legends and lakes.

Must Visit

Visit the Vembanad Lake, Vaikom, Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, Poonjar Palace, and Illikal Kallu for their mesmerising charm. Trek in Nadukani, go kayaking & canoeing at Kuttickattil Home Stay. Experience a houseboat ride, the local cuisine and authentic Ayurvedic treatments. Buy Kerala handicrafts, organic fresh spices, and Kerala silk.

Best time to visit

If you want to explore the beauty of this town at its best weather then pack your bags and head off to Kottayam between September and February when the mild warmth of the day mingles with pleasant cold breezes with the setting sun. You can experience the charming winter months and the festivals of Onam and Food Festival during this time.

Trivia

Kottayam had the first printing press of Kerala, the first college of the state, first and sole Cooperative society of writers, first tobacco-free district and first to be adapted as an ‘Eco-City'.

Interest
  • Wellness

Ayurveda finds its origins in the heart of Kerala with traditional therapies, massages, and holistic healing, which makes Kottayam its esemplar in essence. Wellness centres like Somatheeram and Kumarakom Lake Resort provide it all. Nothing works better to rejuvenate your body and mind than the lush greenery and tranquil backwaters of this city.

Vembanad Lake

Kerala

Vembanad Lake forms the heart of the water channels supporting not just Alleppey but also the neighbouring cities of Kottayam, Kuttanad, and Kochi. The glittering expanse of the lake is dotted with many tiny islands which can be accessed easily from Kochi. Surrounded by swaying palm trees, this serene lagoon forms a major part of the Kerala backwaters and ornate houseboats light up the lake at night, twinkling like stars.

Good To Know: Identified as a Wetland of International Importance by the Ramsar Convention, it is the longest lake in the entire country!

Vaikom

Kottayam, Kerela 686141

A temple town, Vaikom is famous for its backwaters and its ancient Mahadeva temple which is believed to be a work of Parasurama, the creator of Kerala. Backwater trip in Vaikom is a popular activity among tourists who enjoy the rides on conventional non-motorized kettuvallam across the beautiful narrow canals of Vikom. Backwater trips are accessible from KTDC Jetty and Vaikom Boat Jetty.

Good To Know: The ‘Asthami’ celebration here is held twice in a year during February-March and November-December for 12 days, making it the best time to visit Kottayam.

Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary

Kavanattinkara, Kumarakom, Kerala 686563

The Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary is a forested area sprawling over fourteen acres of land in the small village of Kumarakom in the district of Kottayam. A haven for exotic migratory birds, the sanctuary also appeals to a lot of hikers as it offers beautiful secluded trails that wind through the forests. This is a paradise for both nature lovers and those seeking peace in the silence of the trees.

Good To Know: This refurbished British rubber plantation is home to the rare Siberian Crane as well as to the more common owls, cuckoos, waterfowls, and herons.

Poonjar Palace

PHC Rd, Panachippara, Poonjar, Kerala 686581

A regal palace with heritage values and royal elegance, Poonjar Palace is furnished with royal statues, palm leaf engravings, antiques, giant chandeliers, weapons, a sculpture of Nataraj, a treatment bed for Ayurvedic massages made from a single piece of wood, and many more which highlight the royalty of Kottayam of the bygone era.

Good To Know: Prime attractions of this place are Madurai Meenakshi Temple and the recreation of tales from Puranas through ancient sculptures.

Illikkal Kallu

Illikkal Kallu Rd, Moonnilavu, Poonjar Vadakkekara, Kerala 686586

Situated at the utmost peak of Illickal Malaa, it is a major tourist attraction of Moonilavu village in the Meenachil Taluk. Winding paths lead to the majestic monolith only half of which is present now. From the top, the Arabian Sea can be seen at a distant horizon as a subtle blue stroke and the view of the full moon is awe-inspiring. It is said that the legendary blue flower, Neela Koduveli, believed to have mystical powers to yield riches and rich harvest, grows here.

Good To Know: You need to trek a minimum of 1 km to reach the top of this summit.

Summer April - June

Summers in Kottayam lasts from March to June. Temperatures can range between 22°C and 39°C with April being the hottest month among all. The heat along with high humidity might make it an uncomfortable time to visit Kottayam.

Good To Know: Light cotton clothes, umbrella and sunscreen would be your best company as you explore Kottayam in summer.

Monsoon July - September

The monsoons begin in June and end in September, receiving rain from two monsoon season. One is the south-west rain that tactfully calms down the extreme hot wrath of nature with its cooling downpour and the other one is the north-east monsoon.

Good To Know: During the pre-monsoon rain in Kerala, Kottayam receives the highest rainfall so do not forget to carry an umbrella and necessary rain gear to experience the heavenly showers in this pristine backwater town.

Airway Cochin International Airport

Although Kottayam has no airport of its own, the closest airport is the Cochin International Airport which serves as a link with the cities of New Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, and Coimbatore. The airport connects to foreign cities such as Colombo, Singapore, and Kuala Lumpur and also to several cities in the Middle East. Jet Airways, Indigo, Air India, Emirates, Air Asia, and many others serve as eminent modes of influx for tourists all across the globe. Once you alight at the Kochi International Airport, you can board a train or travel by other local services to reach Kottayam.

Railway Kottayam Railway Station

The Kottayam railway station which is 49 km away is linked to other cities like New Delhi, Ahmadabad, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Kolkata, and Chennai. It lies at the closest proximity of only 2 km from the KSRTC bus stand. The nearby places like Changanassery and Vaikom are linked by railways. The express train runs regularly from Kottayam station to the cities of Ernakulam, Kollam, and Thiruvananthapuram. The seven railway stations in Kottayam are – Piravom, Vaikom, Ettumanoor, Changanassery, Kottayam, Chingavanam, and Kuruppanthara.

Local Transport Rental

Kottayam has several state-owned buses that run across the city making it a convenient and economical option. However, they can get extremely congested during peak hours or on roads like M.C. Road. Taxis can be boarded at the taxi stands near the District Collectorate and the District Hospital. Although taxis are a convenient and comfortable option, they are also expensive compared to auto-rickshaws. Even though, rates are usually fixed; the rickshaw drivers generally charge higher fares at night.

FAQs

What is the best time to visit Kottayam?

Considering the tropical sultry climate, winter seasons- September to February is a pleasant time to visit Kottayam.

Is there a bus route to IllikalKallu?

Well, although there was a test run, there isn’t a direct bus route yet. Theekoy is the nearest town from where the bus can be boarded.

What is Kottayam famous for?

Kottayam is well-known for its natural rubber, lakes and panoramic scenic beauty.

How many days are enough for Kottayam trip?

A properly planned itinerary of 3 days is enough to travel in and around Kottayam.

Is camping allowed at IllikalKallu?

Camping here isn’t recommended considering the terrain that might prove dangerous.

What is the distance between Ernakulam to Kottayam?

The fastest travel means is a direct cab but buses are also available. By road, the distance between Ernakulam to Kottayam is approximately 68 km.
