Poovar

A Holy Grail to those who seek tranquillity and peace in the arms of nature, Poovar's shores are graced by the azure water of the Arabian Sea, becoming an image drawn by God's hand.

Found close to the capital of Trivandrum, Poovar is the unassuming, pristine haven in God's Own Country, Kerala. The picture-perfect expanse of the Poovar beach, cradled by two bodies of sapphire, gushing water is an alluring attraction for visitors. The muddled backwaters of the town lie in undisturbed bliss, only interrupted by the cruises wading through their expanse in search of their natural treasures. The palm and coconut trees stand green and tall on the sands, tickled by the wings of the exquisite birds and wildlife that inhabit the area. It makes for the perfect spot to purge all your stresses and give in to the bounty of nature.

Must Visit

Enjoy the spectacular Poovar beach, the cascading waters of Thirparappu Falls, the Fishing Village and Vizhinjam, for an insight into Poovar. Cruise through the backwaters to delve into nature's beauty and rejuvenate through the relaxing Ayurvedic therapies. Delicacies like fresh fish curry and other South Indian fare are a must-eat here.

Best time to visit

October to February is the most inviting time to visit Poovar. In the summer it is plagued by a scorching sun while its monsoons mean deluges of rainfall. The winters are a breath of fresh air on the beaches of Poovar, making the atmosphere a heavenly mix of the scent of the sea and comforting weather.

Trivia

Strangely enough, Poovar is thought to be the famous wealthy port of Ophir from the Bible, where King Solomon's ships had landed.

Poovar is a perfect honeymoon destination, with its serene beaches, tranquil backwaters, and luxurious floating resorts. The untouched natural beauty, boat rides through mangroves, and the peaceful ambiance offer newlyweds a romantic and secluded escape.

Poovar is an offbeat gem in Kerala, known for its secluded backwaters and golden sand beaches. Less commercialized than other destinations, Poovar offers tranquility and scenic beauty with its floating cottages, island resorts, and serene boat rides, making it a hidden paradise.

Poovar is a wellness retreat known for Ayurvedic treatments and holistic therapies. Renowned centers like Estuary Island and Poovar Island Resort offer Ayurvedic massages, detox programs, and yoga sessions, making it an ideal destination for relaxation and rejuvenation.

Good To Know: This beach is a unique strip that separates the Neyyar River from the Arabian Sea.

Thiruparappu Falls

97W6+5WV Water Falls, Tiruparapu, Tamil Nadu 629151

This man-made waterfall is a mesmerising picture of sparkling water falling from its tall, 50-feet cliffs. Flowing streams join the cascade of the Thirparappu Falls, pooling into a magical reservoir of translucent water. This pool is enveloped by rich greenery and native flora and fauna. This hypnotising waterfall is popular amongst families for picnics as well as activities like boating. A short trip from Poovar takes you to this wonderful haven.

Good To Know: Less than 30 km from Poovar awaits the gushing flow of Thirparappu Falls.

Summer April - June

The tropical environs of Poovar take on a golden glow in the summer, which sets in around March and persists till the end of May. The sun shines brightly on the crystal - clear waters of the beach. However, summers in Poovar are absolutely scorching, with temperatures rising to 37°C, making sightseeing and other activities exceedingly difficult.

Good To Know: It is better to avoid Poovar during the summer, but one can try to enjoy the respite of the waters of the sea.

Roadway State Owned Bus

The roads and paths to Poovar are almost as picturesque as the town. Abundant bus and taxi services, state-run as well as private - ply to and from Poovar. Poovar's bus station is managed by the Kerela State Road Transport Corporation on State Highway 2. The nearest bus stop from this town is in Neyyanttinkara, 10 km away. The journey between the two points is short and quick, enhanced by beautiful landscapes. You can also reach Poovar by travelling on a boat or houseboat over the pristine waters of Kerela's coast.

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Poovar?

The top tourist attractions in Poovar are Poovar Beach, Poovar Backwaters, Golden Sand Beach, Thirparappu Falls, Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary, Vizhinjam Lighthouse and Kovalam Beach which is quire near.

How do I get to Poovar?

The nearest airport is Trivandrum International Airport, approximately 30 km away. The nearest railway station is Thiruvananthapuram Central (Trivandrum), about 27 km from Poovar. Buses and taxis are available for road travel to Poovar from Thiruvananthapuram and other nearby cities.

What is the best time to visit Poovar?

The best time to visit Poovar is from November to February when the weather is cool and pleasant. The monsoon season (June to September) brings heavy rainfall but offers a unique experience with lush greenery and full backwaters. The summer months (March to May) can be hot and humid.

What are the accommodation options in Poovar?

Poovar offers a range of accommodation options including luxury resorts, mid-range hotels, and budget guesthouses. Popular choices include Poovar Island Resort, Estuary Island Resort, Isola Di Cocco Beach Resort, and budget-friendly options like Havelia Island Resort and Over The Hill.

What should I pack for a trip to Poovar?

Pack light and comfortable clothing suitable for the tropical climate. During the monsoon season, bring rain gear like umbrellas and raincoats. Don’t forget essentials like sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, swimwear, and comfortable footwear. Insect repellent is also advisable.

Are there any cultural or local festivals celebrated in Poovar?

Poovar, like the rest of Kerala, celebrates various local festivals and events including Onam (the harvest festival) in August/September, Vishu (the Kerala New Year) in April, and regional boat races during the monsoon season. These festivals offer a glimpse into the local culture, traditions, and arts.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Poovar?

Poovar is known for its Kerala cuisine, including dishes like appam and stew, fish curry, prawns roast, Kerala sadya (a traditional vegetarian feast), and various coconut-based dishes. Seafood is particularly popular, and local delicacies like Karimeen pollichathu (pearl spot fish) are a must-try.

Can I find guided tours or local guides in Poovar?

Yes, guided tours and local guides are available in Poovar. Many tourists prefer hiring a guide to gain deeper insights into the local culture, history, and natural beauty of the region. Tour operators offer various packages, including backwater tours, city tours, and adventure activities.
