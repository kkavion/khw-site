Sariska

Sariska allows a unique experience of the silence of the deserts coupled with the essence of traditionalism and safaris that will keep you on your toes.

The Sariska National Park, snuggled within the Aravali Hills is a destination within the rich aesthetics of Rajasthan that abounds in the very quintessential north Indian scenery of grasslands and arid forests. It is rich in mineral resources, abundant in deciduous forests, and packed with the ideal Rajasthani flavours. It lies within a short distance from Jaipur and Delhi, thus sharing their cultural zest and piquancy. Not only does Sairiska function as a perfect destination for Safaris and wildlife photography but it is also great for exploring the native, unwritten historical beauty that might interest a few.

Must Visit

The Silisher Lake, Kankwari Fort, Bhangarh Fort, Pratapgarh Fort, and Ajabgarh Fort will entice your senses. Safari is the primary attraction here and seeing a wild animal only a few feet away is in itself exciting. Try the Kalakand, Gheewar, and Dal Bati Churma. Bag some bangles and Rajput-style jewellery to take back.

Best time to visit

In winters colourful and vibrant migrant birds visit the lakes and hills, in the summers the mammals come out of their hiding for water. The best time to visit would be anything between October to June as the park remains closed from July through September. You can enjoy the thrill of a safari with clear skies and suitable clothing. Be sure to have your cameras ready to capture the wild beauties to not miss your chance.

Trivia

Declared a wildlife Reserve in 1955, and included within the Tiger Project in 1978, it became a key location for biodiversity in the Northern Aravalli leopard and wildlife corridor.

Interest
  • Wildlife

Sightings of Bengal tigers, leopards, rhesus macaques, chital, jungle cats, sambar, and wild boars are common in this wildlife sanctuary. The verdant landscape attracts many, and jungle safaris through dense forests, rocky terrain, and historical ruins like Kankwari Fort add to the thrill.

Siliserh Lake

Kishanpur, Rajasthan 301001

Silisherh is enclosed by thickly wooded hills so that the view from any point in the lake is one of unmatched panoramic natural extravagance. Boating and fishing are popular activities here and if in luck, you can spot myriad migratory birds flitting through the trees or dancing across the ripples.

Good To Know: A hunting chalet overlooking the lake from 1845 has been converted into a heritage hotel by the RTDC so that tourists can rely on imperial luxury while revelling in light glinting off the lake’s placid surface.

Sariska Palace

, Sariska Palace Rd, Ganeshpura With Rundh Kali Ghati, Sirska, Rajasthan 301001

Originally built as a resting place for maharajas on hunting expeditions, the Palace now serves as a three-star heritage hotel with luxurious accommodations and the experience of a traditional grand stay. It is close to the sanctuary and set within a vast expanse of wildlife and forests. The palace appears glistening white in the scorching sun and takes on a golden glow in the dead of the night.

Good To Know: The gift shop Sariska Khazana within the premises is an antique traditional boutique replenished with colourful souvenirs.

Bhangarh Fort

Rajgarh Tehsil, Bhangarh, Rajasthan 301410

The Bhangarh fort was essentially a self-sufficient and prosperous township in its heyday- the premises retain memories of many temples, palaces, rows of common houses, and bustling marketplaces. The dilapidated Hindu temples of the Nagara style of architecture from the 17th century and several havelis recite the tales of the past lives of its residents, from kings and queens to priests and dancing girls.

Good To Know: It is hailed as a haunted site and many tourists and locals have corroborated its paranormal associations with eerie real-life experiences!

Pratapgarh Fort

27, Pratapgarh-Burja Tiraha Rd, Mayur Vihar, Malviya Nagar, Alwar, Rajasthan 301001

Built by Rajkumar Pratap Singh, Pratapgarh Fort is the pinnacle of a whole culture that must be experienced in the streets and narrow allies of this beautiful town. Although it is closely related to the Bhangarh Fort, the Pratapgarh Fort has its own legends and circulated myths. The town is a seamless blend of royal and tribal cultures as the very infrastructure has a quaint authenticity about it that is indigenous and unique.

Good To Know: The city is also famous for its Thewa art and its edible hing and jiralun spices which you can explore.

Neemrana Hill Fort - Kesroli

Kesroli, Rajasthan 301030

The 14th-century fort was apparently built by descendants of Lord Krishna. The Hill Fort Kesroli is a magnificent architectural delight with its turrets, ramparts, and lovely arched verandahs. The granite structure exudes a serene ambience as well as offers a cool respite from the harsh heat.

Good To Know: You will be utterly enchanted by the rooftop view which is the perfect seat for observing a gilded sunset or for relaxing with a variety of birds twittering around you!

Kankwari Fort

Kankwari Fort, SH 29A, Rundh Kankwari, Rajasthan 301410

The Kankwari Fort is a former prison built uniquely upon a short hill surrounded by high forests and mountains. Tourists are attracted by the scenic beauty and panoramic views. The drive up is risky yet exciting. The rooms and hallways have intricately painted designs and the balconies open up to blue skies and a huge expanse of landscape.

Good To Know: Famous for the imprisonment of Dara Shikoh by his brother Aurangzeb, a major portion of his scholarly work is known to have been performed while in exile including the translation of the Vedas to Persian.

FAQs

What is Sariska Tiger Reserve known for?

Sariska Tiger Reserve is known for its significant population of tigers, leopards, deer, and various bird species. It is a popular wildlife sanctuary located in the Alwar district of Rajasthan.

How do I get to Sariska Tiger Reserve?

The nearest airport to Sariska is Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi, approximately 160 km away. The nearest railway station is Alwar Junction, about 36 km from the reserve. From Alwar, visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Sariska.

What is the best time to visit Sariska Tiger Reserve?

The best time to visit Sariska Tiger Reserve is from October to June. The park remains closed during the monsoon season from July to September. The winter months (October to March) are particularly pleasant, while the summer months (April to June) offer better chances of spotting tigers near water sources.

What are the accommodation options near Sariska Tiger Reserve?

There are various accommodation options near Sariska Tiger Reserve, including resorts, lodges, and hotels. Popular choices include Sariska Palace, The Sariska Retreat, Sterling Sariska, and budget-friendly options like RTDC Tiger Den.

What are the safari options in Sariska Tiger Reserve?

Visitors can opt for jeep safaris to explore the reserve. Jeep safaris are available in the morning and afternoon, providing an excellent opportunity to spot wildlife and enjoy the natural beauty of the park.

What should I pack for a trip to Sariska Tiger Reserve?

Pack comfortable and light clothing suitable for the season. Neutral-colored clothing is recommended for safaris to blend in with the surroundings. Don’t forget essentials like sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, binoculars, and a good camera for wildlife photography. Warm clothing is necessary for early morning and evening safaris during the winter.

Is Sariska a family-friendly destination?

Yes, Sariska is a family-friendly destination with activities suitable for all age groups, including wildlife safaris, nature walks, and visits to historical sites.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties near Sariska?

The cuisine in the Sariska region includes a mix of Rajasthani and North Indian dishes. Local specialties include Dal Baati Churma, Gatte ki Sabzi, Ker Sangri, and traditional sweets like Ghewar and Ladoo. Most resorts and lodges offer a variety of Indian and continental cuisines to cater to tourists.

Is Sariska Tiger Reserve safe for tourists?

Sariska Tiger Reserve is generally safe for tourists. However, it is advisable to follow the guidelines provided by park authorities and guides during safaris and to avoid venturing into the forest alone.

Can I find guided tours or naturalists in Sariska Tiger Reserve?

Yes, guided tours and experienced naturalists are available in Sariska Tiger Reserve. They can enhance the safari experience by providing valuable insights into the flora, fauna, and the park’s ecosystem.

What are the rules and regulations for visiting Sariska Tiger Reserve?

Visitors must follow the park rules and regulations, which include: o No littering inside the reserve o No feeding or disturbing the wildlife o Staying within the designated areas during safaris o Maintaining silence to avoid disturbing the animals o Not using flash photography
