Alwar

Alwar beckons you to experience the quintessential Rajasthani lifestyle being an exemplary amalgam of scenic natural features, historic forts, architectural marvels, and colourful festivals.

The salubrious air of Alwar is in itself an enticing welcome to all the travellers in dry Rajasthan! Comfortably ensconced in the foothills of the benign Aravallis, the city offers a gleeful melange of panoramic natural beauty, the mysticism of old architecture, vibrant cultural practices, and exotic wildlife. The city is an ecological epicentre that allows Royal Bengal Tigers to co-exist with white-throated kingfishers. Centuries of royalty have left behind spectacular specimens of architecture in the form of palaces, hunting chalets, forts, and gardens here. Come, join the expedition into a city that mirrors the true heart and soul of Rajasthan!

Must Visit

The haunted fort of Bhangarh and the wonderfully maintained Sariska Tiger Reserve draw droves of interested explorers annually! For a scintillating experience, you should boat in the placid Silisherh Lake and trek along the Sariska-Alwar-Ambar trail. You must also try the delicate Kalakand and collect terracotta statues and handicraft items.

Best time to visit

Winter is the most coveted season for tourists and locals alike in Alwar. In the months spanning November to February, the natural setting of the lush city awaits your arrival eagerly. The congenial weather is conducive both for work and play. It is the ideal time to flit around the myriad tourist spots, visit the shopping centres, and gorge on tasty food items on the go!

Trivia

Unknown to most would be the Pandavas spending the last few years of their exile in Alwar in around 1500 BC, making it Rajasthan's oldest city.

Interest
  • Wildlife

Due to its proximity to Sariska Tiger Reserve, Alwar has become a wildlife attraction. Sightings of Bengal tigers, rhesus macaques, chital, sambar, & wild boars are common here. Jungle safaris through dense forests, rocky terrain, and historical ruins like Kankwari Fort add to the thrill.

From ancient temples and shrines to architectural marvels steeped in history, any tourist will be overwhelmed by the sheer diversity of the city. The fragrant local cuisine, the colourful traditional attire of its people and the bustling markets will draw your attention every which way.

Silisherh Lake

Kishanpur, Rajasthan 301001

Silisherh is enclosed by thickly wooded hills so that the view from any point in the lake is one of unmatched panoramic natural extravagance. Good To Know: A hunting chalet overlooking the lake from 1845 has been converted into a heritage hotel by the RTDC so that tourists can rely on imperial luxury while revelling in light glinting off the lake’s placid surface.

Bala Qila

Alwar, Rajasthan 301001

Governed by a litany of great dynasties like the Khanzadas, the Mughals, the Pathans, and the Jats, it was originally a mud fort dating back to the 10th century. The newer fort was built on the ruins atop a hill giving you an unobstructed view of the whole city. Very interestingly the robust fortress has an almost Greco-Roman structure with elegant marble columns and intricately arranged latticed balconies.

Good To Know: Be mindful of the pathways within as the six gateways leading to over sixty fortified towers form a complicated maze within its walls!

Bhangarh Fort

Rajgarh Tehsil, Bhangarh, Rajasthan 301410

The Bhangarh fort was essentially a self-sufficient and prosperous township in its heyday- the premises retain memories of many temples, palaces, rows of common houses, and bustling marketplaces. The dilapidated Hindu temples of the Nagara style of architecture from the 17th century and several havelis recite the tales of the past lives of its residents, from kings and queens to priests and dancing girls.

Good To Know: It is hailed as a haunted site and many tourists and locals have corroborated its paranormal associations with eerie real-life experiences!

Alwar City Palace

City Palace, collectorate, Mohalla Ladiya, Alwar, Rundh Bhakhera, Rajasthan 301001

Located just below Bala Quila, the Alwar City Palace is a beautiful medley of Hindu-Mughal architecture. You will be awed by the wonder of an artificial lake within the palace grounds. The elevated marble pavilions set on lotus flower bases in the central courtyard add a delicate grace to its design. The halls and pillars speak of past Rajput glory, the radiant opulence, and their tremendous achievements.

Good To Know: Now, you will be pleasantly surprised by the hum of activity on the lower floors of the palace which house the government offices.

Summer April - June

Summers are rarely kind to a city in West Rajasthan. But fortunately for its denizens, Alwar is mostly protected from the harsh summer heatwaves by the towering Aravallis. The months of April to June are typically dry and hot.

Good To Know: The heat is at an all-time high during May when the temperature can soar beyond 40 degrees Celsius. It is a time mostly avoided by tourists and somewhat uncomfortable even for locals.

Railway Alwar Junction

Alwar is only four hours away by train from the capital city, making it quite suitable for weekend trips. Superfast and Intercity express trains eat up the miles with utter ease. As a junction station, Alwar is also connected to all its neighbouring cities by both local and express trains. A popular route is one from Mumbai which is via the other picturesque towns of Rajasthan like Jaipur and Rajkot.

Roadway State Owned Bus

Super-deluxe buses and cars are usually employed in travelling via roadways- the NH-48 ensures the possibility of a short and swift commute. Both private and government buses are available on the Jaipur-Alwar route, which is operational from 5 am to 10 pm, and offer different tariff categories as per your preference. If you are carrying a lot of luggage or would rather travel by car, there are taxi services available in both Jaipur and Delhi to ferry you to and fro Alwar.

Local Transport

Like most Rajasthani cities, Alwar is quite dependent on autos and cycle rickshaws as bigger public transport is not in use. The tongas are also availed by the locals as they are both cheap and easy to get around in. For travel outside the city, you can take any local bus from the depot situated on the outer fringes of the town.

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Alwar?

The top tourist attractions in Alwar are Bala Qila (Alwar Fort), City Palace (Vinay Vilas Mahal), Siliserh Lake and Palace, Sariska Tiger Reserve, Moosi Maharani Ki Chhatri, Bhangarh Fort and Neelkanth Mahadev Temple.

How do I get to Alwar?

Alwar is well-connected via road and rail. The nearest airport is Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi, which is roughly 140 kilometers away. Alwar Railway Station has daily trains that link to Delhi, Jaipur, and other major cities. Road transportation options include buses and taxis.

What is the best time to visit Alwar?

The best time to visit Alwar is between October and March, when the weather is cool and ideal for touring. Summers (April to June) can be quite hot, while the monsoon season (July to September) provides considerable rainfall.

What are the accommodation options in Alwar?

Alwar has a wide range of accommodation options, including vintage hotels, resorts, and budget hotels. Popular choices include Neemrana Fort Palace, Alwar Bagh, Dadhikar Fort, and more affordable options such as Hotel Aravali and Sparrow Inn.

What are some nearby attractions or day trips from Alwar?

Some of the nearby attractions from Alwar are: • Sariska Tiger Reserve (approx. 35 km away) • Neemrana Fort Palace (approx. 50 km away) • Bhangarh Fort (approx. 85 km away) • Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary (Keoladeo National Park, approx. 100 km away) • Jaipur (approx. 150 km away)

What should I pack for a trip to Alwar?

Pack comfortable, light attire appropriate for walking and sightseeing. Carry warm clothing in the winter and sun protection in the summer, such as hats, sunglasses, and sunscreen. Comfortable walking shoes are needed.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Alwar?

Alwar is famous for its Rajasthani food, which includes Dal Baati Churma, Gatte ki Sabzi, Ker Sangri, and Rajasthani sweets like Alwar ka Mawa (Kalakand) and Ghewar.
