Agra

A mystical state, Agra teleports you to a glorious past embellished with the ever-enchanting Taj Mahal and fascinating legacy of forts, tombs and mausoleums.

Despite the crowds of people hovering to get a glimpse of the Taj Mahal, you will find yourself drawn to its magnificent beauty. Crowned with the sparkle of brilliance, the Taj Mahal is a year-round attraction for tourists internationally. Behind the musk of the red valour of the Red Fort and veiled by the tranquil shades of the tombs and mosques were the dust and decrepitude of negligence. The Mughal kings took up the responsibility of its creation. Flavoured by the sweet delight of Petha and splashed with the bustling joy of street shopping, this city of antiquity is the romantic ruins of the Mughal dynasty.

Must Visit

Admiring the Taj Mahal should be on the top of your list when in Agra. Explore the beauties of the Mughal era, especially the Fatehpur Sikri, Red Fort, Itimad-ud-daulah's Tomb and Akbar's Tomb to fall in love with its charm. Imbibe the religious essence of Jama Masjid, witness the Kalakriti Dance Drama Show and relax at the Dolphin Water Park.

Best time to visit

The best time to visit would be between November to March, which is also the travel season. You might find a bit of a crowd visiting at this time to hear Agra's testimony and see its elegance. The cool weather will allow you to spend a lot of time under the sun and gazing at the jewels of the Mughal Empire.

Trivia

Agra is home to the three UNESCO heritage sites of Taj Mahal, Red Fort and Fatehpur Sikri. It is also the second largest exporter of footwear in the world so be sure to pick up some pairs when here!

Taj Mahal

Dharmapuri, Forest Colony, Tajganj, Agra, Uttar Pradesh 282001

Commemorating the love of Shah Jhan for Mumtaz, the Taj Mahal is a talisman with Qur’anic inscriptions, deeming itself an everlasting sign of divinity. Cloaked in regal white, the seventh wonder of the world can be visited anytime from 6 am to 7 pm on ordinary days. On a more evangelical day of the full moon, the gates will be open for you even from 8:30 pm till 12:30 am.

Good To Know: To enter and spectate the mausoleum closely an entry fee of ₹250 is charged for Indians, ₹1300 for foreigners, and none for children below 15. It is closed on Fridays.

Akbar’s Fort - Sikandra

Tomb of Akbar The Great Area, Sikandra, Agra, Uttar Pradesh 282007

In 1605, around 10 km from Agra and Sikander, Akbar constructed this fort to unite Hindus and Muslims by combining the two styles in its architecture to express a unified harmony between the two forces. When you visit you will notice the use of red sandstone and white marble fused as a piece of peace ennobling harmony. To this, he added a lot of ornamental carvings and constructed this fine building.

Good To Know: The structure is a moral lesson to the world and can be visited with an entry fee of ₹30 for Indians and ₹310 for foreigners.

Jama Masjid

Subash Bazar, Kinari Bazar, Hing ki Mandi, Mantola, Agra, Uttar Pradesh 282003

Visible from the Agra Fort, this mosque is connected to it with a metal band of railway tracks that link their destinies together. Shah Jahan's construction of this cultural artifact in 1684 is a mark of history defining itself and creating a headband that represents the richness of our culture. Garbed in white and red, the sandstone and marble paint a picture that resembles the humble earthenware and the rich white royalty conjoining in harmony.

Good To Know: It's a place of piety where on Friday the devotees gather to share their faith and beliefs.

Kalakriti Culture & Convention Centre

41/142, A/1 VIP Road-Taj Mahal Road, Fatehabad Rd, Agra, Uttar Pradesh 282001

This festival stages the most popular musical drama experience and gives you an authentic feel of being an audience to a dramatic production that also introduces you to the heritage and culture of India. Titled 'The Saga of Love', this romantic historical folk tale is a series of plays staged every day at 6:30 PM. In it, you can feel the history of the ancient rajas and the chivalric pursuits of their ladyship.

Good To Know: You can purchase original artistic pieces as a memento or gift for your near and dear ones from here.

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Agra?

The most prominent attractions in Agra are Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, Fatehpur Sikri, Mehtab Bagh, Itmad-ud-Daulah's Tomb (Baby Taj) and Akbar's Tomb in Sikandra.

How do I get to Agra?

Agra is well-connected via road, rail, and air. The city is accessible from big cities such as Delhi (about 230 kilometres distant). The nearest airport is Pandit Deen Dayal Upadhyay Airport (Agra Airport), which offers few domestic flights. The main railway stations are Agra Cantt, Agra Fort, and Raja Ki Mandi, which have numerous trains connecting to Delhi, Jaipur, and other destinations.

What is the best time to visit Agra?

The greatest season to visit Agra is in the winter, from October to March, when the weather is cool and perfect for sightseeing. Summers (April-June) can be extremely hot, while the monsoon season (July-September) delivers moderate to heavy rain.

What are the accommodation options in Agra?

Agra has a variety of accommodation options, including luxury hotels, mid-range hotels, budget hotels, and guesthouses. Popular alternatives include The Oberoi Amarvilas, ITC Mughal, Taj Hotel & Convention Centre, Trident Agra, and more affordable options include Zostel Agra and Hotel Sidhartha.

What are the must-do activities in Agra?

When in Agra you simply must admire the elegant Taj Mahal, enjoy a view of it from Mehtab Bagh, explore the formidable Agra Fort and tour Fatehpur Sikri. Shopping for local handicrafts, marble inlay work, leather goods in the bazaars and savouring the local Mughlai cuisine will make your visit truly rewarding!

Is Agra a family-friendly destination?

Yes, Agra is a family-friendly destination with a wide range of historical sites, gardens, and cultural events to suit all ages.

What are some nearby attractions or day trips from Agra?

Some nearby attractions from Agra are: Fatehpur Sikri (approx. 40 km away) Mathura and Vrindavan (approx. 60 km away) Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary (Keoladeo National Park, approx. 60 km away) Gwalior (approx. 120 km away) Chambal Safari (for a wildlife experience, approx. 70 km away)

What should I pack for a trip to Agra?

Pack comfortable, light attire appropriate for walking and sightseeing. Carry warm clothing in the winter and sun protection in the summer, such as hats, sunglasses, and sunscreen. Comfortable walking shoes are needed.

Are there any cultural or local festivals celebrated in Agra?

Agra celebrates a variety of local holidays and events, including the Taj Mahotsav (a cultural festival held in February), Diwali, Holi, and Ram Barat. These festivals provide insight into the local culture, arts, crafts, and cuisine.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Agra?

Agra is noted for its Mughlai cuisine, which includes biryani, kebabs, and tandoori meals. Other local delicacies include Petha (an ash gourd-based dessert), Dalmoth (a spicy lentil mix), and Bedai and Jalebi (a popular morning dish).

Is Agra safe for tourists?

Agra is generally regarded as secure for travelers. However, it is best to follow conventional safety procedures, stay alert of your surroundings, and avoid straying into less frequented places, especially at night.

Can I find guided tours or local guides in Agra?

Yes, guided tours and native guides are easily accessible in Agra. Many tourists prefer to hire a guide to learn more about the history and architecture of the city's monuments. Official guides can be hired at major tourist attractions or through travel agencies.
