Bharatpur

Bharatpur's natural allure and historical magnificence make it the perfect getaway in the haven of cultural royalty.

Bharatpur is the ideal tourist destination for travel enthusiasts who are looking for natural beauty set against a backdrop of great historical significance. The Keoladeo National Park, the magnificent Brij Festival and the Lohagarh palace fort are artful embellishments on the gorgeous fabric of this antiquated city. Bharatpur is one of the shining jewels in the diadem of royalty! The “Eastern Gateway to Rajasthan” is replete with forts, palaces, and gardens which are architectural wonders! You will certainly find yourself revelling in the cultural as well as natural refuge of this marvellous city of Rajasthan!

Must Visit

The Lohagarh Fort and the Bharatpur Palace are top tourist attractions owing to their extravagance. You must feature bird watching at the Keoladeo National Park on your to-do list along with shopping for authentic Rajasthani shoes or handicraft jewellery from the local bazaars. Feast on Chicken Tikka, Paneer Tikka, Afghani Chicken, and of course the traditional Rajasthani Thali while you're at it!

Best time to visit

The city of Bharatpur can be enjoyed best during the colder months spanning November to February. At this time you can feel the mellow winter sun on your skin and the city is at its liveliest. The arrival of the spring festivities is imminent and you can watch the city come alive. This season will let you feel refreshed and energetic enough to indulge in all your travel plans!

Trivia

The erstwhile capital of the Jat kingdom, Bharatpur gets its name from Lord Ram's brother Bharat and is also known to be the eastern gateway of Rajasthan.

Interest
  • Wildlife

Famous for the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Keoladeo National Park, Bharatpur is a haven for birds, hosting around 370 species, with the likes of the Siberian crane enhancing its global appeal. If you find joy in observing these delicate creatures, this place could be your paradise.

Lohagarh Fort

Gopalgarh, Bharatpur, Rajasthan 321001

Surrounded by deep moats, Lohagarh Fort has been a mute witness to generations of attacks and sieges since the 1730s. In the absence of flamboyant ornamentation, its solid strength has turned it into a bastion of iconic beauty. Within the fort, you must visit Mahal Khas, Kamra Palace and Palace of Badan Singh along with Kishori Mahal and Kothi Khas. The Ashthadhatu gate serves as the main entrance and is imprinted with huge elephants.

Good To Know: You can visit Nehru Park and the Government Museum when here.

Bharatpur Palace

Bharatpur, Rajasthan 321001

Built by a retinue of kings in a series of phases, Bharatpur Palace displays the conjunct of Rajput and Mughal architecture stunningly in the intricate designs and thrives on indulgent luxury. Prepare to be awestruck by a collection of 81 stone sculptures, 861 local art and craft wares and ancient scriptures dating back to the 2nd Century housed in the famous Bharatpur museum, Kamra Khas.

Good To Know: History enthusiasts may keep an eye out for the Golbagh Palace, Moti Mahal and Laxmi Vilas Palace too.

Keoladeo National Park

Bharatpur, Rajasthan 321001

The UNESCO World Heritage Site of the Keoladeo Ghana National Park has spurred its reputation as an Ornithologist’s Paradise. You will hear the constant soothing thrum of life as the 364 breeds of birds, 379 floral species, 13 species of snakes, 5 of lizards and a variety of others frolic around you. The Siberian crane is almost exclusive to this place.

Good To Know: Closed from May to June, the most lucrative window to observe the widest variety of marvellous beauties is during the months of November to March.

Ganga Mandir

Bharatpur, Rajasthan 321001

The temple is a blend of South Indian, Mughal and Rajput styles of architecture. Inside, there is a deity of Ganga in unmarred white marble, delicate architecture of the pillars and walls, and beautiful idols of King Bhagirath, Lord Krishna and Goddess Ganga. Ganga Dusshera and Gangashaptami draw hordes of tourists and devotees here every year!

Good To Know: Built in ninety years under Maharaja Balwant Singh in the mid-19th century, all affluent families donated a month’s pay towards its construction.

Dholpur Palace

Nihal Ganj, Dholpur, Rajasthan 328001

Popularly known as Raj Niwas Palace, Dholpur Palace is over two centuries old and made of red sandstone. The passages hold delicately carved designs of flowers that tell stories of past ages. The Rajputana is ingrained in every brick as the palace is composed of locally quarried stone! The sight of the setting sun on the burnished stone is one of awe-inspiring beauty.

Good To Know: You can even choose to stay within these halls as the Raj Niwas Palace has recently been developed into a luxury resort!

Bharatpur has a typical desert climate with long hot summers, mild monsoons and pleasant winters. While summers can become rather uncomfortable due to the sun beating down harshly on the locals, there is comfort in the cool breeze at night. Residents breathe a sigh of collective relief as temperatures drop considerably with the arrival of the rains. But the most anticipated season is certainly winter when people gear up for many activities and the quick influx of tourists!

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Bharatpur?

The top tourist attractions of Bharatpur are Keoladeo National Park (Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary), Lohagarh Fort, Bharatpur Palace and Museum, Deeg Palace, Ganga Mandir and Banke Bihari Temple.

How do I get to Bharatpur?

Bharatpur is well-connected via road and rail. The nearest airport is Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi, which is roughly 190 kilometers away. Bharatpur Junction is the city's railway station, with daily trains running to Delhi, Agra, Jaipur, and other major towns. Road transportation options include buses and taxis.

What is the best time to visit Bharatpur?

The best time to visit Bharatpur is in winter, from October to March, when the weather is pleasant for birdwatching and sightseeing. The monsoon season (July-September) delivers moderate rainfall and abundant foliage.

What are the accommodation options in Bharatpur?

Bharatpur has a variety of accommodation options, including hotels, guesthouses, and resorts. Popular alternatives include The Bagh Resort, Laxmi Vilas Palace, and Chandra Mahal Haveli, as well as more affordable options like Hotel Sunbird and Hotel Surya Vilas Palace.

What are some nearby attractions or day trips from Bharatpur?

Some of the nearby attractions from Bharatpur are: • Fatehpur Sikri (approx. 25 km away) • Agra (approx. 55 km away) • Mathura and Vrindavan (approx. 65 km away) • Deeg Palace (approx. 32 km away) • Jaipur (approx. 185 km away)

What should I pack for a trip to Bharatpur?

Pack comfortable, lightweight attire for walking and birdwatching. Carry warm clothing in the winter and sun protection in the summer, such as hats, sunglasses, and sunscreen. Comfortable walking shoes and binoculars for birdwatching are required.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Bharatpur?

Bharatpur is famous for its Rajasthani food, which includes Dal Baati Churma, Gatte ki Sabzi, Ker Sangri, and traditional desserts like Ghewar and Ladoo.
