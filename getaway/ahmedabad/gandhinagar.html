Gandhinagar

The greenest capital city of Asia, Gandhinagar exhibits a refreshing amalgam of culture, modernism and spirituality with its smartly organized streets, contemporary hubs and exquisite temples.

The greenest capital city of Asia, Gandhinagar exhibits a refreshing amalgam of culture, modernism and spirituality with its smartly organized streets, contemporary hubs and exquisite temples.

Must Visit

Gandhinagar’s famed Akshardham Temple should be the first spot on your must visit list; Adalaj Stepwell and Rani Roopmati Mosque should be next with their remarkable designs. Picnic at the Sarita Udayan and Indroda Nature Park - you can even see skeletons of whales here! Experience the thrill with rides at Fun World and shop at Craftsmen’s Village.

Best time to visit

Winter followed by Monsoon constitutes the best time to visit Gandhinagar. The period from July to March sees a favourable drop in temperature and makes for a pleasant visit. The rains wash the already clean city and bring a vibrancy to the lush landscape while the cool winds in winter let you explore the city without breaking a sweat. Since it is also the time for festivities, the city is decked and its spiritual essence is even more prominent.

Trivia

The city’s name is a tribute to the father of the nation, Mahatma Gandhi for his unwavering faith in his people and his resilient attempts at achieving freedom by upholding the ideals of peace and non-violence.

Relatively less frequented than other cities in Gujrat, Gandhinagar offers a charm that is less explored but rich in its culture. The planned cityscape, stunning Akshardham Temple, scenic parks, proximity to Sabarmati Ashram, & vibrant festivals enhance its appeal.

Akshardham temple

J Road, Sector 20, Gandhinagar, Gujarat 382020

The Akshardham Temple in Gandhinagar honours Lord Swaminarayan, the chief figure of the Swaminarayan faith. Well-known for its architectural grandeur and beautiful embellishments, the complex frequently features cultural displays depicting Lord Swaminarayan's life and teachings. These exhibits include multimedia presentations, dioramas, and artifacts. It has extensive gardens as well as a magnificent musical fountain show.

Good To Know: Visitors can relax in the peaceful surroundings and watch the mesmerising fountain show during their visit.

Trimandir

Trimandir Ahmedabad-Mehsana, Highway, Adalaj, Gujarat 382421

This "Temple of the Divine Trinity" honours Lord Simandhar Swami, Lord Krishna, and Lord Shiva. Founded on the spiritual teachings of Param Pujya Dada Bhagwan, it seeks to foster unity among various sects and beliefs by integrating the worship of three deities. Its non-sectarian stance welcomes people of all faiths to come and pray to increase religious concord and spiritual understanding.

Good To Know: Trimandir frequently serves as a community centre, organising events, meetings, and activities that foster spiritual growth and unity.

Adalaj stepwell

Adalaj Rd, Adalaj, Gujarat 382421

Adalaj Stepwell's creation is accompanied by an intriguing historical and romantic story. Queen Rudabai had it built in the 15th century to commemorate her husband, King Veer Singh of the Vaghela dynasty. It is an outstanding example of Indo-Islamic construction and craftsmanship designed in the Solanki architectural style, which features elaborate carvings and ornamentation.

Good To Know: It has five floors and is embellished with magnificent carvings of mythological figures, religious symbols, and elaborate designs.

Craftsmen village

Pethapur Gujrat

Well known for its ancient hand block printing industry, Pethapur is a hamlet in Gandhinagar district. The community has a long history of making handcrafted textiles, such as block-printed fabrics. Its artisans are well-known for their artistry and use of natural colours in block printing. Pethapur's textiles frequently combine traditional motifs with contemporary styles.

Good To Know: Pethapur is home to artisan communities that practice and preserve the traditional art of hand block printing.

Indroda Nature Park

Gandhinagar, Gujarat 382421.

Widely regarded as the world's second-largest dinosaur egg hatchery, Indroda Nature Park is popular among paleontologists and nature lovers. It is home to a unique variety of plant and animal species. With life-size dinosaur replicas and dinosaur history exhibits, the Dinosaur and Fossil Park is one of its primary attractions.

Good To Know: Indroda Nature Park has a butterfly park where visitors may see several species of butterflies, as well as a jungle safari where tourists can explore the natural habitats of specific animals in the park.

Rani Roopmati mosque

2HJM+PG9, Mirzapur Rd, Old City, Delhi Chakla, Dariyapur, Ahmedabad, Gujarat 380001.

Though the Rann of Kutch earthquake of 1819 caused partial damage to the minarets, this architectural wonder still stands with its intricately carved windows and flower motifs. One of the mosque's distinguishing features is its latticed windows and well-decorated dome ceilings. The religious edifice also has an exquisitely sculpted prayer hall.

Good To Know: The mosque is located in the old mediaeval city, close to important landmarks such as Sidi Saiyyed Mosque, Lal Darwaja, Bhadra Fort, Teen Darwaza, and Rani no Hajiro.

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Gandhinagar?

Some of Gandhinagar's prominent tourist attractions are the Akshardham Temple, Indroda Nature Park, Sarita Udyan, Adalaj Stepwell, and Mahatma Mandir.

How do I get to Gandhinagar?

Gandhinagar is well-connected via road, rail, and air. The nearest airport is Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport in Ahmedabad, which is around 30 kilometers away. Gandhinagar has its own railway station, and buses run frequently to and from other major cities in Gujarat.

What is the best time to visit Gandhinagar?

The best time to visit is in the winter, between November and February, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for sightseeing.

What are some popular festivals celebrated in Gandhinagar?

The prominent festivals celebrated in Gandhinagar are Uttarayan (Kite Festival), Navratri, Diwali, Holi, and the International Kite Festival.

What are the accommodation options in Gandhinagar?

Gandhinagar has a wide selection of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to economical guesthouses. Cambay Sapphire, Fortune Inn Haveli, and The Leela Gandhinagar are some of the most popular hotels.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Gandhinagar?

Gandhinagar offers a variety of Gujarati cuisine, including dishes like Dhokla, Thepla, Khandvi, and Fafda-Jalebi. Street food is also popular, with items like Pani Puri, Dabeli, and Bhajiya.

Is Gandhinagar a safe city for tourists?

Gandhinagar is considered one of the safest cities in India for tourists. However, it's always advisable to take general safety precautions.

What makes Gandhinagar different from other cities in Gujarat?

Gandhinagar is known for its planned layout, extensive green cover, and wide roads. Unlike many other Indian cities, it offers a more organized and serene environment.
