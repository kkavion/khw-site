Vadodara

Vadodara, Gujrat's cultural capital, enchants with its unique blend of culture, history, and modernization and tops it up with a delectable cuisine and buzzing nightlife.

Bustling markets, exquisite structures and lush gardens populate the majority of this vibrant city. Boasting one of the most spectacular palaces in India, Vadodara has much more than just the brilliance of the Lakshmi Vilas Palace to offer. The splendid fusion of architectural styles in the temples will have you rooted to take in its beauty and also offer reverence. Its museums and eateries have a whole fandom of their own but the most awaited nine-day celebration of Navratri takes the cake with its festive grandeur. The UNESCO World Heritage Site of the Champaner Pavagadh Archaeological Park does enough to transport you to the world of Ramayana.

Must Visit

Known for its intricate architecture, Vadodara's must visit sights include many temples like that of Kirti Mandir, Nyay Mandir and Baps Sri Swaminarayan Mandir. Explore the crafts of the bygone era in the stunning Laxmi Vilas Palace and Vadodara Museum and Picture Gallery and the nature around at Champaner Pavagadh Archaeological Park and Sayaji Baug.

Best time to visit

Vadodara becomes the most welcoming city in the winter months spanning October to March. The cool winds provide a pleasant respite from the usual humid atmosphere of the city and the sun's soft warmth allows a cosy exploration of the city. You could even partake in the grand Navratri celebrations during this time!

Trivia

The Lakshmi Vilas Palace, built in 1890 by Maharaja Sayajirao Gaekwad III, is four times the size of Buckingham Palace.

Interest
  • Historical

Vadodara boasts the grand Indo-Saracenic Laxmi Vilas Palace, Kirti Mandir with royal tombs, and the colonial-era Gaekwad Baroda Golf Club. Its museums, picture galleries, and the nearby UNESCO-listed Champaner Pavagadh Park further enhance its historical allure.

Laxmi Vilas Palace

J N Marg, Moti Baug, Vadodara, Gujarat 390001

Well known for its architectural magnificence and historical value, and among the biggest private homes in the world, the magnificent Lakshmi Vilas Palace is a regal palace in Vadodara. Maharaja Sayajirao Gaekwad III combined Islamic, Indian, and European architectural elements in this Indo-Saracenic Revival architecture. The Laxmi Vilas Palace Museum is housed in a portion of the palace that was originally built.

Good To Know: Lakshmi Vilas Palace is regarded as one of the world's largest private homes; its size even eclipses that of Buckingham Palace.

Champaner Pavagadh Archaeological Park

FGMJ+RXV, Champaner, Gujarat 389360

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park is renowned for its historical, architectural, and archaeological significance and for fusing Hindu and Islamic architectural forms. The remnants of the mediaeval Gujarati city of Champaner, founded by Sultan Mahmud Begada, are located within the park. Excavations here have produced a variety of buildings and artefacts that help comprehend its history.

Good To Know: The Jami Masjid and Sahar ki Masjid or the Mosque of the Dawn are the park's most notable buildings.

Kirti Mandir

Kothi Rd, Dak Bunglaw, Sayajiganj, Vadodara, Gujarat 390001

Kirti Mandir pays tribute to the Gaekwad emperors while also reflecting Vadodara's rich past. Its architectural style combines Hindu, Jain, and European features. The central shikhara (spire) features elaborately carved embellishments and statues. The temple area has images of Lord Shiva and other deities, as well as a sanctum sanctorum where people can pray and worship becoming a site of Vadodara's religious and cultural legacy.

Good To Know: The compound also features the Jagdambi Mata Mandir, a temple honouring the goddess Jagdambi.

Nyay Mandir

76X3+6GC, Sultanpura, Vadodara, Gujarat 390001

A symbol of the administration of justice in the city, Nyay Mandir is a historic building that houses the District Court in Vadodara. It is historically significant since it has long served as a courthouse. The building's architectural design combines traditional and colonial influences. Since it is located in the middle of Vadodara, it is a convenient and accessible venue for court procedures.

Good To Know: The complex houses not only courtrooms, but also offices for legal practitioners, judges, and other administrative services.

Baps Sri Swaminarayan Mandir

Shastri Yagnapurushdas Marg Atladara, Vadodara, Gujarat 390 012

The temple complex is affiliated with the Bochasanwasi Akshar Purushottam Swaminarayan Sanstha (BAPS), a prominent Hindu organisation. The temple is renowned for its magnificent architecture, which incorporates traditional Hindu architectural components and elaborate sculptures. The use of pink sandstone enhances the temple's aesthetic appeal.

Good To Know: BAPS is known for its engagement in various community service activities, and the temple often serves as a centre for social and humanitarian initiatives.

Sayaji Baug

Vinoba Bhave Rd, Near Kala Ghoda, Dak Bunglaw, Sayajiganj, Vadodara, Gujarat 390005

Sayaji Baug or Kamati Baug is one of the biggest gardens in Western India, well-liked by tourists and locals alike for leisure and cultural activities. Maharaja Sayajirao Gaekwad III, the visionary ruler of the former Baroda State, founded the garden in 1879. Sayaji Baug has a Victorian garden-style layout with well-kept lawns, walkways, and different buildings.

Good To Know: The Baroda Museum and Picture Gallery is located in Sayaji Baug and is housed in an Indo-Saracenic-style edifice.

Vadodara Museum and Picture Gallery

Kamatibaug, Dak Bunglaw, Sayajiganj, Vadodara, Gujarat 390020

Established in 1887, the Vadodara Museum and Picture Gallery's collection comprises artefacts from various eras, including antique sculptures, manuscripts, and archaeological finds. Visitors can go through galleries dedicated to archaeology, ethnology, natural history, and ancient civilizations. The museum frequently hosts special exhibitions, which feature rare collections and thematic displays.

Good To Know: An Egyptian mummy is one of the museum's most unusual attractions, adding a fascinating element to the eclectic collection.

Maharaja Fateh Singh Museum

Jawaharlal Nehru Marg, Navapura, Vadodara, Gujarat 390001

Opened in 1961 and named after the monarch of the princely state of Baroda, the Maharaja Fateh Singh Museum is a cultural gem that provides a rich and instructive experience, making it an important stop for art and history fans in Vadodara. Visitors can examine a rich ceramic collection that includes artefacts from various regions and times. It houses a collection of European and Japanese porcelain, as well as finely carved marble pieces.

Good To Know: One of the museum's standout characteristics is its collection of magnificent Indian miniature paintings.

Airway Vadodara Airport

Gujarat's first green airport, Vadodara Airport is situated in the north-east of the city. Vadodara offers direct flights to Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad and New Delhi. Air India and IndiGo have frequent flights from the airport. The Vadodara airport also includes a new unified international terminal since October 2016. After Kochi, it is India's second green airport.

Railway Vadodara Railway Station

In 1861, Vadodara was a stop on the historic Bombay, Baroda, and Central India Railway. Vadodara Railway Station presently belongs to the Western Railway zone of Indian railroads and is a significant junction on the Western Railway Main Line, after the BBCI Railway was united with the Saurashtra, Rajputana and Jaipur railroads to form the Western Railway. Currently undergoing construction, the Mumbai-Ahmedabad high-speed rail corridor, India's first high-speed rail line, would stop at the existing Vadodara Junction railway station.

FAQs

What are the top tourist attractions in Vadodara?

Vadodara’s top tourist attractions include Laxmi Vilas Palace, Sayaji Baug (Kamati Baug), Baroda Museum and Picture Gallery, EME Temple, Kirti Mandir and Sursagar Lake.

How do I get to Vadodara?

Vadodara is well-connected by road, rail, and air. The Vadodara Airport has regular flights to major cities in India. Vadodara Junction is a major railway station with connections to various parts of the country.

What is the best time to visit Vadodara?

The best time to visit Vadodara is during the winter months, from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and suitable for sightseeing.

What are the accommodation options in Vadodara?

Vadodara offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Popular hotels include Welcomhotel Vadodara, The Fern Residency, and Grand Mercure Vadodara Surya Palace.

What are the local cuisines and food specialties in Vadodara?

Vadodara offers a variety of Gujarati cuisine, including dishes like Sev Usal, Farsan, Khaman, and Undhiyu. Street food is also popular, with items like Vada Pav, Pav Bhaji, and Dabeli.

What are some shopping destinations in Vadodara?

Some of the best places to shop In Vadodara are Inorbit Mall, Vadodara Central, Seven Seas Mall, Mandvi Gate Market and Raopura Market.
