Udaipur

Udaipur leaves you spellbound with hemmed hills, azure lakes, a kaleidoscope of heroic past, fairy- tales of palaces, temples, and grandiose architecture.

Udaipur is an embellished jewel in the majestic crown of the regal state of Rajasthan. The Aravalli hills extending their nestling arms, embrace the “Venice of the East” from all sides. The mesmeric sheen of Udaipur has added feathers to its crown with its moniker the “Kashmir of Rajasthan” where you can cherish a beauty akin to it. With exquisite palaces made of marble adorning the city, it's no wonder why Udaipur is known as the “White City”. The city is the muse of poets, writers, and painters. A trip with the ideal blend of sight, experience, and sound will etch the pith of your memory lanes.

Must Visit

Experience the bewitching sheen of the City Palace, royalty at Shilpgram, and the thrill of the Mansapurna Karni Ropeway ride. The Vintage Car Museum, Snow Park, and Crystal Gallery are a must-visit for some downtime. Dive into the Rajasthani thali that serves Dal Baati Churma, orange Jalebis and more. Pick up Bandhani and Batik print textiles and Jaipuri Razai.

Best time to visit

The winter months from September to March are the ideal time to visit for a respite from the sweltering climate of Rajasthan. November to February is the peak season for its soothing environment and also the renowned annual Shilpgram Craft Fair. The colourful pageantry of Mewar Festival draws crowds in summer and monsoons bring the best deals for you to enjoy.

Trivia

Unknown to most would be the Pandavas spending the last few years of their exile in Alwar in around 1500 BC, making it Rajasthan's oldest city.

Udaipur, a cultural hub, is renowned for its royal heritage and palaces like City Palace and Lake Palace, reflecting Rajput grandeur. The city's serene lakes, cultural festivals like Mewar and Shilpgram, significant temples, traditional art, and vibrant performances offer a rich cultural experience.

Udaipur, known as the "City of Lakes," is a top honeymoon destination with its romantic lakeside views, luxurious resorts like Taj Lake Palace and Oberoi Udaivilas, and private boat rides. The city's regal palaces and tranquil gardens offer a fairytale setting, while its pleasant climate ensures a perfect, intimate getaway.

Udaipur is emerging as a wellness destination with luxury resorts like Oberoi Udaivilas and Ananta Spa & Resort offering Ayurvedic treatments and holistic therapies. Its serene lakes and hills provide a tranquil setting for yoga, meditation, and nature walks. Udaipur combines natural beauty with luxurious wellness experiences, making it ideal for relaxation and rejuvenation.

Renowned for its royal charm and scenic beauty, Udaipur is the most sought-after wedding destination. With opulent venues like City Palace and Taj Lake Palace, stunning lakeside settings, and luxury resorts such as Oberoi Udaivilas, it offers a regal backdrop for a fairy-tale wedding.

With luxurious venues, modern infrastructure, and scenic surroundings, Udaipur is a rising MICE destination. Resorts like The Leela Palace and Oberoi Udaivilas offer modern facilities and a regal backdrop. The Aravallis, Lake Pichola, and heritage sites further enhance its appeal for corporate events.

Lake Pichola

Pichola, Udaipur, Rajasthan 313001

Being one of the largest and oldest artificial lakes in the city, Lake Pichola is flanked by temples, ghats, islands, ornamental arch bridges, palaces, kiosks, lofty hills, and a lot more. Your visit to Lake Pichola will be incomplete without three things- boating in the dawn, and the surreal view of sunset and sunrise.

Good To Know: Picchu Banjara, a Banjara tribesman had built this mesmeric lake, which enthralled Maharana Udai Singh to such an extent that he established the Udaipur city in its banks.

Jag Mandir

Pichola, Udaipur, Rajasthan 313001

Located on Lake Pichola’s southern island, the three-storied palatial edifice of Jag Mandir overlooks the azure waters and is made of yellow sandstone- marble. Adorned with eight majestic life-sized elephants made with white marble, vibrant gardens, exquisite decor, restaurants, museums, and many more captivating regal fascinations, it is a delight to watch and experience.

Good To Know: En route to the palace, you can visit the Kunwar Pada ka Mahal, Bara Patharon ka Mahal, Zenana Mahal, and Gul Mahal - a mosque, where Shah Jahan stayed with his family.

City Palace

Old City, Udaipur, Rajasthan 313001

Flanked by the placid Lake Pichola, this magnificent royal edifice is the pride of Rajasthan. With an astounding blend of Chinese and Medieval European architecture, it is regarded as the largest regal complex in the princely state. The mesmeric marble and granite edifice forms a perfect contrast with the scenic natural background.

Good To Know: The marvellous assortment of towers, museums, marble domes and pavilions, majestic palaces, crystal and porcelain figures, temples, and lavish decor showcases the finest elements of Rajput art and culture.

Shilpgram

JM66+9JP, Shilpgram, Udaipur, Rajasthan 313001

When we think of a market, we picture shops laid across narrow lanes and vibrant goods dangling at their entrance. Shilpgram is that sprawling stretch of land which is an incredible heritage craft village with a rustic aura. Mansapurna Karni Ropeway

Deen Dayal Park, Doodh Talai, Rajasthan 313001

The first ropeway cable in Rajasthan, joining Deendayal Upadhyay Park and the Manasapurna Karni Temple at Machhala Hill is one of the fascinating tourist places of Udaipur. Located in the core of the city, this ropeway serves the dual purpose of curtailing the travelling distance to the pious yet once-remote Karni temple and providing an amazing and comfortable ride with spectacular views.

Good To Know: Hop into one of the Gondolas (the cable cars) around sunset, grab your grub from the Sunset Point, and capture the view of the setting sun.

Vintage Car Museum

Gulab Bagh Road, Brahmpuri, Udaipur, Rajasthan, 313001, India

Vintage Car Museum is a trove of bygone royalty that displays exquisite collections of antique cars. Owing to the Mewar Dynasty's royal collection, this museum is a fascinating spot for automobile and heritage lovers. Treat your eyes to the antique assortments under the ownership of the present heir- Rana Shri Arvind Singh Mewar.

Good To Know: Cherish the view of the Rolls Royce Phantom that was used in the Bond movie Octopussy, Mercedes, Cadillac that had escorted Queen Elizabeth to the airport, MG_TC Convertible, Cadillacs, Vauxhall, and many more.

Airway Maharana Pratap Airport

Udaipur has its own airport that is at a distance of 22 km from the heart of the city.
