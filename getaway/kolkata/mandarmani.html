Mandarmani

Mandarmani beckons its visitors to experience the sandy shores, the water sports and the serenity of the azure waters.

A long stretch of the sandy beach with coconut groves all around, the seamless sea and thrilling water sports are what Mandarmani has to offer. The seemingly unending stretch of one of the longest motorable beaches in Mainland Asia abounding in venturous activities will have a rush of adrenaline pumping into your veins. A serene seaside resort village in West Bengal, Mandarmani gives you a chance to enjoy unrestrained tranquillity and the perfect respite from the chaos and din of city life.

Must Visit

Monsoon conjures the ideal atmosphere and the beach looks ethereal. It's the peak season here as it offers a mesmerizing view of the beach and the rain. The sight is spectacular and accommodations are sparse. However, the aesthetically appealing view attracts people from all over. Winter the next best season to visit with the ‘Surf n Sand' festival gathering many here.

Best time to visit

What must be done while you're in Mandarmani is to experience the beach and the eternal waters in all their glory while you indulge in gratuitous water sports and fill your stomach with desired delights till the exhaustion of the city life wears out of you and all that remains is an ecstatic joy that never wears off!

Trivia

The sight of red crabs crawling alongside the beach made it appear as a shimmering field of red flowers making the residents initially name it ‘Mandar', and later on change it to Mandarmani.

Railway Howrah - Kanthi P. H.

The town of Digha has the nearest railhead. There are three trains available from Howrah to Digha. Contai, at a distance of 17 km, has the next nearest railway station of Kanthi. If you're travelling from Kolkata you can take a train from Howrah and switch to a cab from Kanthi Railway Station to reach Mandarmani. Travelling will take about 3 hours and cost you anything between Rs 700 and Rs 1,500.

FAQs

Should I visit Mandarmani or Digha?

If you’ve visited one, it is worthwhile to give the other a chance. There is a distinct difference in the beaches and hotels that are there in both places. While Digha is more upscale with several eateries and star-rated hotels around, it’s usually very crowded. Mandarmani however, gives you a quiet experience of the quaint resort village. The beach is less crowded and the hotels are spaced out allowing you to seclude yourself in nature.

Are there accommodations by the sea?

Yes, there are many hotels by the sea and it is possible to acquire rooms that will give you a blissful view of the beach side with the endless sky and water as its backdrop, making you not want to go back to the city again. Aqua Marine Drive In, Hotel Sonar Bangla, Victoria Beach Resort, Viceroy Beach Resort, and Candlewood Park Beach Resort are some of the resorts that will allow you the best view of the sea from their enclosures.

What activities can be done in Mandarmani?

Mandarmani is more of a destination to soak up the sun while lazing on the beach. However, if you’re the adventurous kind, feel free to indulge in the manifold water sports available at the beach. You can munch on treats served in the beach stalls to revive your body and go for the next round of activity. Be sure to visit the Mohana and experience the splendid view in person.

What activities can be done in Mandarmani?

With the place rapidly developing, the eateries and restaurants in Digha are increasing in number and standard. Local food stalls and those by the beach are sure to give you the perfect savouries to snack on while you laze on the beach or indulge in water sports. Other restaurants that will cater to your seafood cravings and match your expectations are The Bite Restaurant, Mahabhoj Restaurant, Restaurant Bhorpet, Aqua Blues and Koshe Kosha. If you’re looking for cheaper options but good quality food, the Bay Cuffe Restaurant and Elgin Restaurant will fit the bill. All in all the food experience in Digha is one to look forward to and delight in.

Where are the best places to eat in Mandarmani?

If you’re visiting Mandarmani you’re probably excited to try out all the seafood there to satiate your cravings. Several open food shacks will delight you with the local delicacies and many variations of pakoras. Destination specialties like Daab Chingri, Chingri’r Malaikari, Kakrar Jhaal, and Tawa Fried Pomfret should be tried in Park Hotel & Restaurant and Saathi Restaurant.

Is drinking on the beach allowed?

Yes, it is allowed provided public decorum is maintained. Littering on the beach can become a problem so be sure to properly dispose of empty bottles and glasses. The beach restaurants and properties may not stock up on a wide variety of alcohol so it is advisable to carry your preferred drinks with you while travelling.

What is the safest/fastest/cheapest route to Mandarmani from Kolkata?

The fastest way to Mandarmani is by cab. It takes approximately 2 hours 26 minutes to get there but the transport can be expensive, ranging from Rs 3,700 to Rs 4,400 unless you’re driving there yourself. Both options are equally safe. You can also choose to travel by train from Howrah to Kanthi Station (Contai) and then take a cab to Mandarmani from there. It will take about 3 hours and cost you anything between Rs 700 and Rs 1,500. A cheaper option would be taking a bus from Esplanade to Mandarmani’s Chaulkhola Bus Stand and a cab from there to the Mandarmani resort village. The journey will roughly take you 3 hours 26 minutes and cost you about Rs 800.
