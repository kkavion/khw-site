Shantiniketan

“Fall in love with the seamless blend of nature, culture and the arts in the quaint town of Shantiniketan.”

The brainchild of The Bard of Bengal Rabindranath Tagore, Shantiniketan is, as the name suggests an "Abode of Peace". Inspired by the refreshing philosophy of learning in an atmosphere "where the mind is without fear ", The Vishwabharati University here epitomizes the art of learning amidst nature whereas, Shantiniketan sums up the art of living amidst the same. It is a potpourri of culture where music, dance, literature, folk traditions, arts and crafts converge offering you a unique slice of Bengal.

Must Visit

The Poush Mela and Basanta Utsav with its festivities – quirky food stalls offering Bengali delicacies and handicrafts specially fashioned by the local artisans bring the town alive and so should be experienced. Exploring the museums is also an indulgence that can transport you back in time.

Best time to visit

The Poush Mela and Basanta Utsav witness art melas and the Baul mystics putting up rustic musical oeuvres that draw the crowds in. Winter is the ideal time to visit Shantiniketan as the weather is chilly and exploring the area is an enjoyable experience. It's when the city comes alive with the Poush Mela as the university town is caught up in the busy humdrum of the festival.

Trivia

Shantiniketan was initially the name of a house in the land of Birbhum that Maharshi Debendranath bought which inspired him to name the town so.

Sonajhuri Sight-seeing, Nature Walk

Birbhum, West Bengal 731204

A tourist destination, Sonajhuri is situated at a distance of 3.1kms from Santiniketan. It embodies natural diversity in the form of Laalmati (red soil), Sonajhuri (earleaf acacia), eucalyptus trees and eroded soil (khowai) which lends this place an extraordinary scenic beauty. The place derives its name from the Sonajhuri tree and offers respite to travellers.

Good To Know: The nature walks allow a unique solidarity with nature and usually leaves a tourist imbibing the serenity of the forest.

Rabindra Bhavan Museum Activity

Road around Ashram-Math, Bolpur, West Bengal 731204

Popularly known as Bichitra, this museum was constructed in the year 1961,Tagore's birth centenary year. It is a storehouse of original manuscripts, letters documents, medallions, plaques, paintings and so much more. The dated objects and their magnificent past is sure to make you reminiscence the events of the years gone by.

Good To Know: Except Wednesdays and Saturdays, this museum is open on all other days for you to take a trip down Tagore's memory lane.

Vishwa Bharati University Sight-seeing, Nature Walk

Shantiniketan, Bolpur, West Bengal 731235

Tagore established a school here in the middle of the pastoral setting in 1901 which later evolved into the Vishwa Bharati University. With liberal arts and the nature being the ultimate focus of this University, it draws students from across the world and is a sanctuary housing Bengal's art and culture.

Good To Know: Rabindranath Tagore's Patha Bhavan, whose centre of attraction was learning in nature turned into a University in the year 1921.

Upasana Griha Sight-seeing

West Bengal 731204

Rooted in the philosophy of spirituality, the Upasana Ghar does justice to the ethos of Santiniketan. Made from Belgian glass, this prayer hall is surrounded by painted glass allowing the natural sunlight to seep through in varied colours, infusing an aura of calm and beauty that helps focus on the divine. The candles lining the hall every evening accentuate its splendour when its deprived of the morning light.

Good To Know: Prayers are mainly conducted on Wednesdays in this Kaanch Mandir.

Prakriti Bhavan Museum Activity

Amar Kutir Road, Kabi Mohanpur, West Bengal 731204

The only Nature Art Museum in India, Prakriti Bhavan is situated in the Ballavpur area of Shantiniketan. Located in one of the most visually appealing locales of the university town, natural sculptures in driftwood and dry wood with an open-air garden consisting of rock sculptures are a part of this museum.

Good To Know: It is often perceived as one of the perfect examples of Tagore's most preached philosophy due to its congenial existence with nature.

FAQs

What is Shantiniketan famous for?

There is no one thing that Shantiniketan is famous for since it itself is an amalgam of many facets of Bengal. Some are drawn to the place for Rabindranath Tagore and the legacy that he has left behind, others for the rich culture the place exudes especially during festivities or its close association with nature through the Sonajhuri forest and the Deer Park. Those particularly interested in art visit Shantiniketan to see the craftsmanship of the local artisans and procure items to take back.

What are the top sites to visit in Shantiniketan?

Uttarayan complex which houses five buildings Tagore lived in is usually the top site to be visited in Shantiniketan. Apart from that, Kala Bhavan and the Vishwa Bharati University are also places of both historic and cultural value that see many tourists throughout the year. If your reason to visit is to feel closer to nature then you must stop over at Deer Park and Sonajhuri Forest to witness its serenity.

What activities to do in Shantiniketan?

When in Shantiniketan you simply must tour Rabindra Bhavan which is the main house Tagore lived in that now holds important artefacts and items of historical significance in relation to the man. Experiencing the festivals of Poush Mela and Basant Utsav is a must-do as it will take you to the roots of Bengal’s culture. Meditating in Chattimtala should also be on your to-do list if you wish to feel solidarity with the divine.

Which are the best places to eat in Shantiniketan?

If it is the traditional Bengali delicacies coupled with a soothing ambience you seek, the best places to eat in Shantiniketan are Sonajhuri Restaurant, Ghore Baire, 9X9 Restaurant, Shakuntala and Rasna. If you’re looking for slightly cheaper options but good food SK Restaurant and Vishwa Bharati Cafeteria are the way to go.

What to shop for in Shantiniketan?

Items that you must shop for when in Shantiniketan are kantha stitch clothes, tanth sarees, sculptures, terracotta, leather items, brassware, handloom & handicrafts and jute products. The Poush Mela, Amar Kutir, Srijani Shilpagram, Basundhara Arts & Crafts, and Khoai Mela popularly called ‘the Saturday Fair’ are the destinations where you will find your must-buys.

What is the safest/fastest/cheapest route to Shantiniketan from Kolkata?

The fastest and cheapest way to get to Shantiniketan is by taking a train from Howrah Station. It roughly takes about 2 hours and costs Rs 1,000 to get to Bolpur Station and take a cab from there to the town center. Another route for railway transport is to take the train from Sealdah. It takes 2 hours 24 minutes to reach Shantiniketan but costs about the same. Buses can also be boarded from either Esplanade or Howrah and take you there in 4 or 2 hours respectively, costing you a maximum of Rs 370. If you want to experience a long road journey then booking a cab or driving there yourself is also a possible and quite enjoyable option, though a bit on the pricier side. It takes 2 hours 21 minutes to get to Shantiniketan and costs about 2,000 to Rs 4,500.
