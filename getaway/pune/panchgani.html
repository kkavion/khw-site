Panchgani

With the mélange of a panoramic backdrop and intriguing historical remnants, Panvel offers you the chance to unravel in nature and discover comfort and quiet.

Panvel lands you sedated within the laps of nature. The lush green Matheran Mountains rise to the east, as a blunt cape, after having rolled for miles on verdurous grounds, and to the west lies a ruined Fort of Karnal atop a mossy rock face, intriguing and intimidating. Forts built under the great Shivaji still stand erect and manage to inspire. Giving the benefits of city life and also intimacy with nature with its bird sanctuary, and scenic Gadeshwar Dam backdropped by the majestic Matheran Hills, this city is just perfect to get away from the routine life at home.

Must Visit

If you crave a camp experience, the Prabalmachi Fireflies Night Trek is ideal for you. Go trekking to Prabalgad Fort and through Kalavantin Durg. The Dahi handi festival is a celebration you shouldn't miss along with sighting rare birds at the Karnala Bird Sanctuary. Indulge in the Agri Cuisine of Panvelities and take home a box of their modaks.

Best time to visit

Winter with its moderately cool temperatures and complacent atmosphere can enable you to make the most of your short trip. The clouds arrange themselves densely around the peaks, vibrant birds gather around lakes and the water ripples across the surface, over-brimmed by the recent rains. Such sights can induce you to absorb the beauty and the tranquillity to the T.

Trivia

Lying close to the Panvel Creek, this town forms a gateway to the Konkan Coast and had been used as a strategic trading ground for the Mughals and Marathas.

Devrai Art Village

Dharmapuri, Forest Colony, Tajganj, Agra, Uttar Pradesh 282001

It is a showcase platform as well as a shopping hub of all incredible, unique artworks such as paintings, rock - stone sculptures-‘Rock Dhokra’ being the most famous among all (brass-stone fusion) that are made by the extraordinarily skilled craftsmen of the Adivasi community, based on the symbiotic relationship and sacredness of mother earth.

Good To Know: The firm believers of “empowerment and not charity” have opened up many scopes and possibilities for Adivasi communities.

Rajpuri Caves

41/142, A/1 VIP Road-Taj Mahal Road, Fatehabad Rd, Agra, Uttar Pradesh 282001

This chain of four caves surrounded by water ‘kunds’ has historical and spiritual significance related to the Pandavas and also the penance and rituals performed by Lord Kartikeya. Lord Kartikeya temple is believed to be built with the sand of these caves that bear inscribed stone plates, images of Nandi, archways, stone stairs, underground tunnels in three of them, and sacred water flowing from Gomukh in the other.

Good To Know: Do visit this temple in the caves during the pompous celebration of Thaipooyam festival.

Book Village

41/142, A/1 VIP Road-Taj Mahal Road, Fatehabad Rd, Agra, Uttar Pradesh 282001

Snuggled between Panchgani and Mahabaleshwar, the first book village of India at Bhilar, 8km from Panchgani, was initiated to encourage reading. 25 vibrantly painted houses with a designated mini-library and reading room allow you to venture into varied genres starting from religion, folk literature, history, and many more. The murals on each house depict the genre available- a wonderful way to ensure you land up in the right house with stocks of your interest.

Good To Know: As a general caution do remember that all books here are in the Marathi language.

Monsoon July - September

The Monsoon Season lasts from July to September with an average rainfall of over 3250 mm every year. The rivers attain volume, while the leaves and trees are refreshed in a vibrant green. A chilly moistness fills the air and atop the hills the heavy clouds float, making the scene appear majestic. You can also witness the festivals of Ganesh Chaturthi and Dahi-handi at this time.

Good To Know: Although the city is extremely prone to flooding and sightseeing may be quite disturbing with the constant showers, it is one of the best times for trekking.

Railway Panvel Junction

Originally opened to assist in rail freight, the Panvel station is one of the ways you can reach the city. It connects to Mumbai through the Harbour Line and is part of the Mumbai Suburban Railway station. It also connects to the Navi-Mumbai Airport and connects to Karjat, Kahndeshwar, and Belanpur. There are shuttle trains available as well from Bhiwadi, Vasai, and Virar and this superb connectivity ensures travellers visit from various places. It is a major suburban station and handles 116 trains from Panvel to various parts of Mumbai.

Roadway State Owned Bus

The road is one of the best ways to get to Panvel. Bus services through the Mumbai-Pune Expressway, NH 4B, Sion-Panvel Expressway, and NH 66 are frequently available to travel from Mumbai or Pune which is just a 1-hour and 2-hour ride, respectively. From places like Karjat, Pen, Alibaug, Bhiwandi, Kalyan, Khopoli, Roha, and Thane, buses are available to the ST station, which also provides buses within the city. However, the easiest and hassle-free commute would be to hire cars from Mumbai and Pune or simply pack up and get on a long refreshing ride from your city.

Local Transport Rental

Within the city, private cars, auto-rickshaws, hired taxis, and buses can be used to get from one place to the other. Although auto rickshaws can charge a bit extra at times, prices are generally fixed and reasonable. For sightseeing one may have to travel to the outskirts, and agencies provide vehicles for visiting neighbouring areas and towns as well. There are two bus terminals, ST station and NMMT station which provide essential bus services required from a purely economic point of view. Since it is a major industrial city, transport for carrying goods is available at low costs.
